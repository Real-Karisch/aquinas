Aquinas Commentary: Romans Chapter 13

Return to Aquinas Commentary Home Page | Return to Romans Contents

13:1 Let every soul be subject to higher powers. For there is no power except from God, and those who are ordained of God.
13:2 Therefore, he who resists the power resists the ordinance of God. And they who resist purchase to themselves damnation.
13:3 For princes are not a terror to the good work, but to the evil. Do you will then to not fear power? Do what is good: and you will have praise from the same.
13:4 For he is God’s minister to you for good. But if you do what is evil, be afraid: for he carries not the sword in vain. For he is God’s minister: an avenger to execute wrath upon him that does evil.
13:5 Therefore one must be subject of necessity: not only for wrath, but also for conscience’s sake.
13:6 For therefore also you pay tribute. For they are the ministers of God, serving unto this purpose.
13:7 Render therefore to all men their dues. Tribute, to whom tribute is due: custom, to whom custom: fear, to whom fear: honor, to whom honor.
13:8 Owe no one anything, except to love one another. For he who loves his neighbor has fulfilled the law.
13:9 For: you shall not commit adultery: you shall not kill: you shall not steal: you shall not bear false witness: you shall not covet. And if there be any other commandment, it is comprised in this word: you shall love your neighbor as yourself.
13:10 The love of our neighbor works no evil. Love therefore is the fulfillment of the law.
13:11 And knowing this time, because it is now the hour for us to rise from sleep. For now our salvation is nearer than when we believed.
13:12 The night is passed, but the day is at hand.
Let us, therefore, cast off the works of darkness and put on the armor of light.
13:13 Let us walk honestly, as in the day:
not in rioting and drunkenness, not in chambering and impurities, not in contention and envy.
13:14 But put on the Lord Jesus Christ: and make not provision for the flesh in its concupiscences.
