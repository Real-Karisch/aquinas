      secundo ex prophetarum praenuntiatione, ibi: et dixi a, a, a; second, from the predictions of the prophets, at and I said: a, a, a (Jer 14:13); tertio, ex pristina populi dilectione, ibi, numquid projiciens abjecisti Judam? third, from the former love of the people, at have you utterly cast off Judah? (Jer 14:19). Circa primum duo. Concerning the first, there are two points. Primo ponit prophetae orationem, in qua proponit tria, misericordiam confitens: si iniquitates nostrae responderint, in poenam. Isa. 59: multiplicatae sunt iniquitates nostrae coram te, et peccata nostra responderunt nobis etc. Ps. 45: adjutor in tribulationibus quae invenerunt nos nimis. First, he gives the prophet’s speech, in which he proposes three things: trusting in mercy—if our iniquities should testify against us, for punishment. Our iniquities are multiplied before you, and our sins bear witness against us (Isa 59:12). A help in tribulations which have found us too often (Ps 45:2). Deinde proponit constantiam: quare quasi colonus futurus es in terra? ac si dicat: indignum constantiae tuae est ut vineam tuam relinquas, quasi colonus alienum agrum, quasi viator, domum tuam, quasi viator nocturnum hospitium. Then he sets forth constancy: why shall you be like a stranger in the land? It is as if he were saying, “It is unworthy of your constancy to forsake your vineyard, as one who tills another’s field”; like a traveler, you forsake your own house, as a traveler forsakes his night lodgings. Quare futurus es quasi vir vagus? hereditatem tuam, quasi homo vagus, et indeterminatae mansionis, proprium habitaculum. Supra 12: reliqui domum meam dimisi hereditatem meam. Why shall you be as a vagrant man, abandoning your inheritance as a man who is vagrant and of indeterminate residency abandons his own dwelling place. I have left my house, I have forsaken my inheritance (Jer 12:7). Proponit etiam potentiam: et fortis qui non potest salvare; quasi dicat: indecens est potentiae tuae ut non salves quos semel in protectionem suscepisti. Num. 14: dicent Aegyptii: non poterat introducere populum in terram pro qua juraverat: idcirco occidit eos in solitudine. Et proponit ei debitam esse illorum curam: tu autem in nobis, quasi in hereditate, et nomen tuum, sicut sub nomine regis, subditi defenduntur. 2 Corinth. 6: inhabitabo in illis, et inambulabo inter eos, et ero illorum Deus, et ipsi erunt mihi populus. Et concludit: ne derelinquas nos, ne tua constantia et potentia blasphemetur. Psalm. 26: ne derelinquas me, Domine, neque despicias me, Deus salutaris meus. He also sets forth power: as a strong man who cannot save; it is as though he were saying, “It is not fitting to your power that you should not save those whom once you took into your protection.” The Egyptians will say, ‘He was not able to bring this people into the land for which he had sworn’ (Num 14:16); therefore, he killed them in the wilderness. And he sets forth the idea that their care is due to him: but you are in our midst, as an inheritance, as it were, and your name, just as subjects are defended by the name of the king. I will dwell amongst them, and walk amongst them, and I will be their God, and they will be my people (1 Cor 6:16). And he concludes: do not forsake us, lest your constancy and power be blasphemed. Do not forsake me, O Lord, nor despise me, O God of my salvation (Ps 26:9). Secundo ponitur Domini responsio: haec dicit Dominus. Second, he gives the Lord’s response: thus says the Lord. Et primo proponit culpam: qui dilexit movere pedes, affectus, propter inconstantiam in bono, et de malo in malum. Prov. 7: mulier vaga, quietis impatiens, nec valens in domo consistere pedibus suis. Vel mittens in Aegyptum pro auxilio. Isa. 30: Aegyptus frustra et vane auxiliabitur. Ideo clamavi super hoc: superbia tantum est; quiesce. First, he exposes their guilt: who loves to move their feet, the affections, because of inconstancy in the good and movement from evil to evil. A wandering woman, impatient of quiet, her feet unable to stay at home (Prov 7:11). Or because of sending to Egypt for help: Egypt will help in vain, and to no purpose. Therefore, have I cried out concerning this: it is only pride; be still (Isa 30:7). Secundo comminatur poenam: nunc recordabitur, poenam inferens. Psal. 88: visitabo in virga iniquitates eorum, et in verberibus peccata eorum. Second, he threatens punishment: now shall be remembered, referring to punishment. I shall visit their iniquities with a rod, and their sins with stripes (Ps 88:32). Tertio excludit remedia: Third, he excludes any remedy: et primo orationum. Et dixit Dominus: noli orare pro populo isto in bonum. Supra 7: tu ergo noli orare pro populo hoc, nec assumas pro eis laudem et orationem, et non obsistas mihi: quia non exaudiam te. and first, of prayer: do not pray for the good of this people. Above: therefore, you shall not pray for this people, neither take up praise, or prayer for them and do not stand against me, because I will not hear you (Jer 7:16). Secundo jejunii, cum jejunaverint. Isa. 58: quare jejunavimus, et non aspexisti; humiliavimus animas nostras, et nescisti? Second, of fasting: though they fast. Why have we fasted, and you have not regarded; we have humbled ourselves, and you did not know (Isa 58:3). Tertio sacrificii. Si obtulerint, Malach. 1: non est mihi voluntas in vobis, dicit Dominus exercituum, et munus non suscipiam de manu vestra. Third, of sacrifice: if they offer. I have no pleasure in you, says the Lord of hosts, and I will accept no gift from your hand (Mal 1:10). Lectio 3 Lecture 3 Destruction of false prophets 14:13 Et dixi: a, a, a, Domine Deus. Prophetae dicunt eis: non videbitis gladium, et fames non erit in vobis; sed pacem veram dabit vobis in loco isto. 14:13 And I said: a, a, a, O Lord God. The prophets say to them, ‘You shall not see the sword, and there will be no famine among you, but he will give you true peace in this place.’ 14:14 Et dixit Dominus ad me: falso prophetae vaticinantur in nomine meo. Non misi eos, et non praecepi eis neque locutus sum ad eos. Visionem mendacem, et divinationem fraudulentiam, et seductionem cordis sui prophetant vobis. 14:14 And the Lord said to me: the prophets have prophesied falsely in my name. I have not sent them, and I have not commanded them, neither have I spoken to them. They prophesy to you a lying vision, divination, deception, and the seduction of their own heart. 14:15 Idcirco haec dicit Dominus de prophetis qui prophetant in nomine meo, quos ego non misi, dicentes: gladius, et fames non erit in terra hac: in gladio et fame consumentur prophetae illi, 14:15 Therefore, thus says the Lord concerning the prophets, whom I have not sent, who prophecy in my name, saying, ‘There shall be no sword or famine in the land.’ These prophets shall be consumed by sword and famine. 14:16 et populi quibus prophetant, erunt projecti in viis Jerusalem prae fame, et gladio, et non erit qui sepeliat eos: ipsi, et uxores eorum, filii, et filiae eorum: et effundam super eos malum suum. 14:16 And the people to whom they prophecy will be cast out in the streets of Jerusalem because of famine and the sword, and there will be none to bury them, they and their wives, their sons and daughters; and I shall pour out this evil upon them. 14:17 Et dices ad eos verbum istud: deducant oculi mei lacrimam per noctem et diem, et non taceant: quoniam contritione magna contrita est virgo filia populi mei, plaga pessima vehementer. 14:17 And you shall speak this word to them: let my eyes stream down tears by night and by day, and not be silent, for with a terrible destruction is the virgin daughter of my people destroyed, by an exceedingly great blow. 14:18 Si egressus fuero ad agros, ecce occisi gladio; et si introiero in civitatem, ecce attenuati fame. Propheta quoque, et sacerdos abierunt in terram quam ignorabant. 14:18 If I go out to the fields, behold those killed by the sword; and if I go into the city, behold those wasted by famine. The prophet also and the priest have gone away to a land they knew not. 90. Hic sumit argumentum a prophetarum praenuntiatione: 90. Here, he takes up his argument from the predictions of the prophets. et primo ponitur prophetae allegatio, a, a, a, pro triplici poena, quam immediate tetigerat. Prophetae, falsi, pacem veram, diuturnam, et non simulatam ab hostibus. Supra 4: ergo ne decepisti populum istum, et Jerusalem dicens: pax erit vobis? et ecce pervenit gladius usque ad animam. First, he gives the intercession of the prophet: a, a, a, for the threefold punishment he had just mentioned. The prophets, false prophets; true peace, lasting and unfeigned on the part of the enemy. Why have you deceived this people and Jerusalem, saying, ‘You shall have peace? And behold the sword has come even unto the soul’ (Jer 4:10). Secundo ponitur Domini responsio: et dixit Dominus. Second, he gives the Lord’s response: and the Lord said. Et primo ostendit prophetarum falsitatem quantum ad actionem quam assumunt: non misi, ad exequendum, non praecepi, injungendo officium, neque locutus sum, inspirando prophetiae donum. Infra 23: non mittebam prophetas, et ipsi currebant; non loquebar ad eos, et ipsi prophetabant. Et quantum ad pacem quam praedicabant: visionem mendacem, seductionem cordis, idest verba excogitata ad seducendum. Ezech. 13: vident vana, et divinant mendacium, dicentes, ait Dominus, cum Dominus non miserit eos. First, he shows the falseness of the prophets with regard to the actions they take: I have not sent, to perform anything; I have not commanded, by enjoining a duty upon them; neither have I spoken, by inspiring the gift of prophecy. I did not send prophets, yet they ran; I did not speak to them, yet they prophesied (Jer 23:21). And with regard to the peace which they predicted: a lying vision . . . the seduction of their own heart, that is, words thought out to lead others astray. They see vain things, and divine a lie, saying, ‘The Lord has spoken’ when the Lord did not send them (Ezek 13:6). Secundo comminatur poenam tam prophetis praedicantibus, quam populo credenti: ideo haec dicit Dominus. Isa. 9: erunt qui beatificant populum istum seducentes, et qui beatificantur praecipitati. Second, he threatens a punishment as much for the prophets who preach as for the people who believe: therefore, thus says the Lord. There will be those who bless this people and lead them astray, and those who are blessed will be cast down headlong (Isa 9:16). Tertio annuntiatur populo prophetae compassio: et dices ad eos: deducant oculi mei (vel vestri) lacrymam. Et sic indicitur planctus poenitentiae. Thren. 2: deduc quasi torrentem lacrymas. Cujus ratio assignatur: quoniam contritione, propter multitudinem occisorum, pessima, propter incorrigibilitatem, vehementer, propter continuitatem. Isa. 30: comminuam eos sicut conteritur lagena figuli, contritione pervalida. Third, the compassion of the prophet is made known to the people: and you shall speak to them: let my (or your) eyes stream down tears. And in this manner the lament of repentance is made known. Stream down tears like a torrent (Lam 2:18). The reason for this is given: for with destruction, because of the multitude of the slain; terrible, because of their incorrigibility; exceedingly, because of its duration. I shall break them small, as a clay jar is broken with a mighty destruction (Isa 30:14). Et assignat contritionis modum, quantum ad poenam occisionis: si egressus, foris, et si introiero, per considerationem. Thren. 1: foris interficiet eos gladius, et intus pavor, juvenem simul, et virginem, lactentem cum homine sene. Quantum ad captivitatem, propheta quoque. Supra 9: disperdam eos in gentibus, quas non noverunt ipsi, et patres eorum. And he gives the manner of their destruction with regard to the punishment of slaughter: if I go out, of doors, and if I go into, by consideration. Without, he shall slay them with the sword, and within, fear—the young man together with the virgin, the nursing mother, and the old man (Deut 32:25). With regard to the captivity: the prophet also. Above: I will scatter them among the nations, which they have not known, neither they nor their fathers (Jer 9:16). Lectio 4 Lecture 4 Jeremiah’s prayer for Israel 14:19 Numquid projiciens abjecisti Judam, aut Sion abominata est anima tua? Quare ergo percussisti nos, ita ut nulla sit sanitas? Expectavimus pacem, et non est bonum; et tempus curationis, et ecce turbatio. 14:19 Have you utterly cast off Judah, or has your soul detested Zion? Why have you struck us so that there is no healing? We have waited for peace, and there is no good; a time of healing, and behold, trouble. 14:20 Cognovimus, Domine, impietates nostras, iniquitates patrum nostrorum, quia peccavimus tibi. 14:20 We have acknowledged our wickedness, O Lord, and the iniquities of our fathers, for we have sinned against you. 14:21 Ne des nos in opprobrium propter nomen tuum, neque facias nobis contumeliam solii gloriae tuae: recordare ne irritum facias foedus tuum nobiscum. 14:21 Do not make us a reproach, because of your name, neither disgrace in us the throne of your glory; remember, and do not break your covenant with us. 14:22 Numquid sunt in sculptilibus gentium qui pluant; aut caeli possunt dare imbres, nisi tu volueris? Nonne tu es Dominus Deus noster, quem expectavimus? Tunc enim fecisti omnia haec. 14:22 For are there among the idols of the nations any who make rain, or can the heavens give showers, unless you will it? Are you not the Lord our God, for whom we wait? For you have made all these things. 91. Hic sumit argumentum ex pristina populi dilectione: et circa hoc tria. 91.
