      Caput 17 Chapter 17 Causes of Israel’s obstinance Lectio 1 Lecture 1 Curses and blessings 17:1 Peccatum Juda scriptum est stylo ferreo in ungue adamantino, exaratum super latitudinem cordis eorum, et in cornibus ararum eorum. 17:1 The sin of Judah is written with an iron pen, and a nail of diamond, it is graven upon the breadth of their heart, and on the horns of their altars. 17:2 Cum recordati fuerint filii eorum ararum suarum, et lucorum suorum, lignorumque frondentium, in montibus excelsis, 17:2 When their sons shall remember their altars, and their groves, and their leafy trees, upon the high mountains, 17:3 sacrificantes in agro: fortitudinem tuam, et omnes thesauros tuos in direptionem dabo, excelsa tua propter peccata in universis finibus tuis. 17:3 those sacrificing in the field; I will give your strength, and all your treasures as plunder; and your high places for sin in all your borders. 17:4 Et relinqueris sola ab haereditate tua quam dedi tibi, et servire faciam te inimicis tuis in terra quam ignoras, quoniam ignem succendisti in furore meo, usque in aeternum ardebit. 17:4 And you will be left stripped of your inheritance, which I gave to you, and I will make you serve your enemies in a land you know not, for you have kindled a fire in my wrath, and it shall burn forever. 17:5 Haec dicit Dominus: maledictus homo qui confidit in homine, et ponit carnem brachium suum, et a Domino recedit cor ejus. 17:5 Thus says the Lord: cursed is the man who puts his trust in man, and makes flesh his arm, and his heart departs from the Lord. 17:6 Erit enim quasi myricae in deserto, et non videbit cum venerit bonum; sed habitabit in siccitate, in deserto, in terra salsuginis, et inhabitabili. 17:6 For he shall be like a tamarisk in the desert, and shall not see when good comes, but he shall dwell in a dry place in the desert, in saltiness where none may dwell. 17:7 Benedictus vir qui confidit in Domino, et erit Dominus fiducia ejus. 17:7 Blessed is the man who trusts in the Lord, and the Lord will be his confidence. 17:8 Et erit quasi lignum, quod transplantatur super aquas, quod ad humorem mittit radices suas, et non timebit cum venerit aestus. Et erit folium ejus viride, et in tempore siccitatis non erit sollicitum, nec aliquando desinet facere fructum. 17:8 And he shall be like a tree which is planted above the waters, which sends forth its roots to the moisture, and shall not fear when the heat comes; and his leaf will be green, and in the time of dryness he will not be troubled, nor shall he cease to put forth fruit at any time. 17:9 Pravum est cor hominis, et inscrutabile: quis cognoscet illud? 17:9 The heart of man is wicked, and inscrutable; who shall know it? 17:10 Ego Dominus scrutans cor, et probans renes, qui do unicuique juxta viam suam, et juxta fructum adinventionum suarum. 17:10 I the Lord examine the heart, and test the reins; it is I who give to each one according to his way, and according to the fruits of his doings. 99. Hic ostenditur explicite populi obstinatio, quae est reprobationis causa: 99. Here, he shows explicitly the stubbornness of the people, which is the cause of their rejection. unde primo ostendit eorum obstinatam malitiam; Whence, he first shows their obstinate malice; secundo consummatam poenam, 19 cap.: haec dicit Dominus etc. second, the punishment consummated, at thus says the Lord (Jer 19:1). In prima duo. Regarding the first, there are two points. In prima ponuntur obstinationis causae; In the first are given the causes of their stubbornness; secundo probatur ipsa obstinatio per experimentum, 18 cap.: verbum quod factum est ad Jeremiam a Domino. second, the obstinacy itself is proven through experience, at the word which came to Jeremiah from the Lord (Jer 18:1). Prima in duas. The first is divided into two parts. In prima ponit causas obstinationis, In the first, he sets down the causes of their obstinacy; in secunda revocat eos ad cultum pristinae religionis, ibi, haec dicit Dominus etc. in the second, he calls them back to the practice of their original religion, at thus says the Lord (Jer 17:19). Circa primum duo. Concerning the first, there are two points. Primo tangit unam causam, scilicet affectum iniquitatis, ponens culpam, in qua ponit exemplum: peccatum Juda, duarum tribuum, scriptum est, idest affectui eorum ita profunditus impressum, praecipue idolatriae, sicut scriptura, quae fit ferreo stylo, in ungue, idest in adamante, qui est lapis politus ad modum unguis. Zech. 7: cor suum posuerunt ut adamantem, ut non audirent legem, et verba quae misit Dominus exercituum in spiritu suo sancto per manum prophetarum priorum. Vel peccatum, idest sententia pro peccato. Et exponit peccati affectum: exaratum, idest profundatum, super latitudinem, idest super cor dilatatum in affectione peccati: unde cor sic dilatatum est loco adamantis, et cornua ararum, idest idolorum, sunt loco styli ferrei. Praeceperat enim Deus ut ex altari procederent quatuor cornua, in quibus penderet craticula, Exod. 27: et simile fecerant in aris idolorum. Oseae 8: multiplicavit sibi Ephraim altaria ad peccandum, factae sunt ei arae in delictum. First, he touches upon one cause, namely, the disposition for iniquity: speaking of their guilt, he gives an example of it: the sin of Judah, of the two tribes, is written, that is, so deeply impressed upon their disposition, particularly to idolatry, that it is impressed, like writing made with an iron pen, with a nail, that is, of diamond, which is a stone polished after the manner of a nail. They have set their heart like diamond, that they may not hear the law and the words which the Lord of hosts has sent in his Holy Spirit through the hand of his former prophets (Zech 7:12). Alternatively, sin, that is, the sentence for sin. And he speaks of their disposition to sin: it is graven, that is, deeply, upon the breadth, that is, upon the heart enlarged in its disposition to sin; for this reason, a heart so enlarged is in place of a diamond, and the horns of their altars, that is, of the idols, are in place of the iron pen. For God had commanded that four horns should protrude from the altar, from which the grating would hang (Exod 27:2–4), and they had done likewise on the altars to the idols. Ephraim has multiplied altars unto himself to sin, altars have been made for him to sin (Hos 8:11). Ponit etiam affectus signum in successione prolis: cum recordati fuerint filii eorum, in affectu retinentes, in diversitate loci, ararum, quae in civitatibus construebantur. Supra 2: sub omni ligno frondoso tu prosternebaris meretrix. He also gives the sign of this inclination in subsequent offspring: when their sons, who retain this inclination, in a variety of places, shall remember their altars, which will be built in their cities. Above: and under every leafy tree you have lain as a harlot (Jer 2:20). Et adjungens poenam, scilicet bonorum direptionem quae pertinent ad necessitatem vitae, fortitudinem, idest bona, in quibus putabas te fortem esse, sicut arena, castra. Supra 15: divitias tuas, et thesauros in direptionem dabo gratis in omnibus peccatis tuis, et in omnibus terminis tuis. Idest, cum malitia tua ita consummata fuerit ut filii patres imitentur, in omnibus locis idola colentes, tunc fortitudinem tuam, et omnes thesauros tuos in direptionem dabo. Et bonorum quae pertinent ad cultum idolatriae, excelsa tua, in quibus idola colebant. Ezech. 6: ecce ego inducam super vos gladium, et disperdam excelsa vestra, et demoliar aras vestras, et confringentur simulacra vestra, et dejiciam interfectos vestros ante idola vestra. He adjoins the punishment, namely, the plundering of goods which pertain to the necessities of life; strength, that is, the possessions because of which you thought yourself to be strong, such as the place of battle, and the military encampments. Above: I shall give your riches and your treasures freely for spoil for all your sins and in all your borders (Jer 15:13). That is, when your malice has become so complete that sons should imitate their fathers, worshiping idols in every place, then I will give your strength, and all your treasures as plunder. And the goods which pertain to the practice of idolatry: your high places, in which they would worship idols. Behold, I shall bring the sword upon you, and I shall destroy your high places, and pull down your altars, and your images shall be broken, and I shall cast your slain before your idols (Ezek 6:3–4). Comminatur etiam captivitatem: relinqueris, o Jerusalem, ab hereditate, a populo tibi subjecto. Vel, o gens, a terra, dum in captivitatem ibis. Thren. 1: quomodo sedet sola civitas plena populo, facta est quasi vidua domina gentium, princeps provinciarum facta est sub tributo? Comminatur et servitutem: et servire faciam te inimicis tuis. Deut. 28: servies inimico tuo quem immittet tibi Dominus, in fame, et siti, et nuditate, et omni penuria; et ponet jugum ferreum super cervicem tuam, donec te conterat. Et tangens proximam poenae causam, scilicet divinam iram, quae est voluntas puniendi: quoniam ignem succendisti. Deut. 32: ignis succensus est in furore meo, et ardebit usque ad inferni novissima. He also threatens captivity: you will be left stripped, O Jerusalem, from your inheritance, from a people subject to you. Alternatively, you will be left stripped, O nation, from the land, while you will go into captivity. How she sits alone, the city filled with people; the mistress of the nations has become like a widow, the chief of the provinces has been made tributary (Lam 1:1). Slavery is also threatened: and I will make you serve your enemies. You shall serve your enemy, whom the Lord will send to you, in hunger, and thirst, and nakedness, and in utter want; and he shall put a yoke of iron upon your neck, until he destroys you (Deut 28:48). And he touches on the next cause of punishment, namely, divine wrath, which is the will to punish: for you have kindled a fire. A fire is kindled in my wrath, and it shall burn to the very depths of hell (Deut 32:22). Secundo tangit aliam causam, scilicet fiduciam impunitatis: maledictus homo qui confidit in homine. Promittebant autem sibi impunitatem ex tribus. Second, he touches on another cause, namely, their confidence in their own impunity: cursed is the man who puts his trust in man. They were assuring themselves of their impunity for three reasons. Primo ex potentia amicorum, scilicet Aegyptiorum: et hanc primo tollit. First, because of the power of their friends, namely, the Egyptians; and this he takes away first. Secundo ex multitudine divitiarum, et hanc, ibi, perdix fovit quae non peperit. Second, because of their abundant wealth, and this, at as the partridge has hatched what she did not lay (Jer 17:11). Tertio ex incredulitate divinarum comminationum; et hanc ibi, ecce ipsi dicunt ad me; ubi est verbum Domini? Third, because of their disbelief in God’s threats; and this at, behold, they themselves say unto me, where is the word of the Lord? (Jer 17:15). Circa primum duo. Concerning the first, there are two points. Primo ponit discretionem eorum qui confidunt in Domino et in hominibus; First, he speaks of the distinction between those who trust in the Lord and those who in men; secundo inducit discretionis auctorem ibi, pravum est cor hominis. second, he introduces the author of the distinction, at the heart of man is wicked (Jer 17:9).
