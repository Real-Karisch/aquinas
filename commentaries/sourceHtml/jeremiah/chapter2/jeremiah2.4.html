Mich. 6: judicium Domini cum populo suo, et cum Israel dijudicabitur. First, he sets forth what he intends, namely, judgment: I shall still contend, concerning the enormity, as for ingratitude; with your sons, who imitate the wickedness of their fathers. The Lord will determine judgment with his people, and with Israel (Mic 6:2). Secundo exequitur propositum, ostendens in judicio peccati enormitatem: The argument follows second, when, through judgment, he shows the enormity of their sin: et primo ex comparatione cultorum: transite, per considerationem, Cethim, occidentales ultra Cyprum, vel civitas nominis Cedar, ubi habitant Ismaelitae. first, from a comparison of religion: cross over, by consideration, to the isles of the Cethim—westerners beyond Cyprus; or a city named Cedar, where the Ishmaelites dwelt. Si mutavit gens; quasi dicat, non mutavit. Unde constat quod comparatione aliorum damnati sunt. Ezech. 5: contempsit judicia mea, ut plus esset impia quam gentes, et praecepta mea ultra quam terrae quae in circuitu ejus sunt. If a nation has changed—it is as though he were saying, “They have not changed.” From this it is certain that they are condemned by comparison with others. She has scorned my judgments, that she may be more wicked than the nations, and my precepts more than any land round about her (Ezek 5:6). Secundo ex comparatione deorum prorumpens in stuporem, obstupescite; quasi dicat: si esset possibile, caeli obstupescerent: vel angeli, qui in caelis sunt, sicut civitas regis sui. Portate ejus, principaliores in angelis, qui sunt quasi principes et judices aliorum. Simile Isa. 1: audite, caeli, et auribus percipe terra quoniam Dominus locutus est. Second, when he turns from a comparison of gods and breaks forth in amazement: be astonished—it is as though he were saying, “If it were possible, the heavens would be astonished”—or the angels, who live in the heavens as the city of their king. And its gates—the chief among the angels, who act as princes and judges of the others. As in Isaiah 1:2, hear, O heavens, and give ear, O earth, for the Lord has spoken. Secundo subjungit stuporis rationem. Duo enim, contra duas partes praecepti. Exod. 20: ego sum Dominus Deus tuus, qui eduxi te de terra Aegypti. Non habebis deos alienos coram me, etc. Et iterum: non facietis deos argenteos, nec deos aureos facietis vobis. Fontem aquae, qui veram consolationem eis dare possum. Psal. 35: apud te est fons vitae. Cisternas, idola, quae salvare non possunt, foderunt, quia ipsi ea fecerunt. Psal. 113: simulacra gentium, argentum et aurum opera manuum hominum. Next he gives the reason for his amazement. For two—against two parts of the commandment. I am the Lord your God, who brought you forth from the land of Egypt. You shall have no other gods before me. And again: you shall not make to yourselves gods of silver or gods of gold (Exod 20:3). The source of living water, who am able to give them true consolation. With you is the source of life (Ps 35:10). Cisterns—idols, which are not able to save—they have dug, because they themselves have made them. The idols of the nations are silver and gold—the works of men’s hands (Ps 113:4). Lectio 4 Lecture 4 Israel’s exile is due to guilt 2:14 Numquid servus est Israel, aut vernaculus? Quare ergo factus est in praedam? 2:14 Is Israel not my servant, or my home-born slave? Why, therefore, have you become prey? 2:15 Super eum rugierunt leones, et dederunt vocem suam, posuerunt terram ejus in solitudinem. Civitates ejus exustae sunt, et non est qui habitet in eis. 2:15 The lions have roared over him, and sent forth their voice: they have made his land a desolation. His cities are burned, and there is none who may live in them. 2:16 Filii quoque Mempheos et Taphnes constupraverunt te usque ad verticem. 2:16 The sons of Memphis and Taphnis have ravished you to the top of your head. 2:17 Numquid non istud factum est tibi, quia dereliquisti Dominum Deum tuum eo tempore quo ducebat te per viam? 2:17 Was this not done to you because you abandoned the Lord your God when he led you along the way? 2:18 Et nunc quid tibi vis in via Aegypti, ut bibas aquam turbidam? Et quid tibi cum via Assyriorum, ut bibas aquam fluminis? 2:18 And now what do you wish for yourself in the way of Egypt, that you may drink muddy water? And what do you wish for yourself in the way of the Assyrians, that you may drink the water of the river? 2:19 Arguet te malitia tua, et aversio tua increpabit te. Scito, et vide quia malum et amarum est reliquisse te Dominum Deum tuum, et non esse timorem ejus apud te: dicit Dominus Deus exercituum. 2:19 Your wickedness will reprove you, and your turning aside will chastise you. Know and see that it is an evil and bitter thing to have left the Lord your God, and that there is no fear of him within you, says the Lord God of hosts. 9. Hic exaggerat culpam ex mentis obstinatione: 9. Here, he magnifies their guilt by noting the stubbornness of their minds. et primo quia peccatum non recognoscebant; First, because they do not recognize their sin; secundo quia multiplicabant, ibi, a saeculo confregisti jugum meum. and second, because they add to it. As it says, from of old you have broken my yoke (Jer 2:20). Circa primum tria. Concerning the first, there are three points. Primo sumit ex poenis culpae argumentum, excludens servilem conditionem, ne propter hoc videatur juste in servitutem adducta. Vernaculus, qui nascitur in domo domini: quasi dicat, non. Joan. 8: semen Abrahae sumus, et nemini servivimus unquam. Proponens poenarum diversitatem quantum ad hominum captivitatem: quare ergo factus est in praedam? First, he deduces the proof of their guilt from their punishments, excluding their servile condition, lest because of this they should seem to have been justly led into slavery. My home-born slave, that is, a slave born in the house of his master; it is as though he were saying, “She is not one.” We are of the seed of Abraham and we have never served anyone (John 8:33). Speaking of the variety of punishments with regard to the exile of people, why therefore have you become prey? Leones, principes Chaldaeorum, vocem, praecepta vel bella. Quantum ad regionis vastationem, posuerunt. Isa. 1: terra vestra deserta, civitates vestrae destructae sunt. The lions, the princes of the Chaldeans; their voice, commands or war. With regard to the devastation of the country: they have made. Your land is desolate, your cities have been pulled down (Isa 1:7). Quantum ad mulierum violationem: filii quoque, civitates Aegypti, usque ad verticem, contra naturam abutentes, nulli parcentes membro. Vel spiritualiter per idolatriam usque ad firmos contaminantes. Thren. ult.: mulieres in Sion humiliaverunt, et virgines in civitatibus Juda. With regard to the rape of the women: the sons of Memphis and Taphnis, the cities of Egypt; to the top of your head—abusing her against nature and sparing no limb of her body. Or spiritually, contaminating even the strong through idolatry. They have humbled the women in Zion, and the virgins in the cities of Judah (Lam 5:11). Et concludens veram rationem: non istud factum est tibi, quia dereliquisti Dominum Deum tuum? Ducebat, prospere. Non enim te dereliquit, sed tu ipsum. Dan. 9: quia recessimus a te, et non audivimus vocem Domini Dei nostri, ut ambularemus in lege ejus quam posuit nobis per servos suos prophetas . . . stillavit super nos maledictio. Then concluding with the true reason: was this not done to you because you abandoned the Lord your God? He led you, prosperously. For he did not abandon you, but you him. Because we have turned away from you, and we have not listened to the voice of the Lord our God to walk in his Law which he gave us through his servants the prophets . . . a curse has fallen upon us (Dan 9:10). Secundo ponitur inutile remedium, quod contra hujusmodi poenas adhibebant. Ex quo patet quod peccatum non recognoscebant sperantes in auxilio gentium: unde ponit auxilii inquisitionem, et nunc quid tibi vis? quasi dicat: non sic deberes poenas vitare, sed Deum placando, in via Aegypti, mittens pro auxilio, aquam, Nili, fluminis, Euphratis, qui est Assyriorum. Isa. 30: qui ambulatis ut descendatis in Aegyptum, et os meum non interrogastis. Second, he discusses the useless remedy which they had employed against punishments of this kind. From which it is clear that they did not recognize their sin, but instead hoped in the help of the nations. For this reason, he asks about the remedy: and now what do you wish for yourself? It is as though he were saying, “You should not avoid the punishments in this way, but by pleasing God,” in the way of Egypt, when he mentions the water of the Nile as a remedy, of the river, Euphrates, which belongs to the Assyrians. You who walk that you may go down to Egypt, yet have not asked at my mouth (Isa 30:2). Contra praedictae culpae recognitionem, arguet, malitia, quando pro ea poenas sustinebis. Isa. 3: agnitio vultus eorum respondebit eis, et peccatum suum quasi Sodoma praedicaverunt. Their wickedness will reprove them for not having recognized their guilt, when they suffer punishment for it. The expression of their face will give judgment against them, for they have proclaimed their sin like Sodom (Isa 3:9). Tertio adhibet utile consilium, ut peccatum recognoscant: scito malum, inhonestum, amarum, poenosum. Sophon. 1: vox diei Domini amari, tribulabitur ibi fortis. Third, he offers useful advice to bring them to recognize their guilt: know . . . an evil—that it is disgraceful, bitter, and painful. The sound of the day of the Lord is bitter, then shall the strong man be troubled (Zeph 1:14). Lectio 5 Lecture 5 Obstinance in idolatry 2:20 A saeculo confregisti jugum meum, rupisti vincula mea, et dixisti: non serviam. In omni enim colle sublimi et sub omni ligno frondoso tu prosternebaris meretrix. 2:20 From of old you have broken my yoke, you have burst my chains asunder, and you have said, ‘I will not serve.’ On every high hill, and under every leafy tree you have lain as a harlot. 10. Hic ostendit, quod peccatum obstinata mente multiplicabant: 10. Here, he shows that sin is multiplied by an obstinate mind: et primo quantum ad diuturnitatem temporis. A saeculo; quasi dicat, a principio legis datae. Isa. 48: transgressorem ex utero vocavi te. Job 21: quid est opus ut serviamus ei, et quid nobis proderit, si oraverimus illum? first, with regard to duration: from of old; it is as though he were saying, “From the moment the Law was given.” I have called you a sinner from the womb (Isa 48:8). What need is there that we serve him, and what will it benefit us if we pray to him? (Job 21:15). Secundo quantum ad multitudinem operis, in omni colle: in locis altis et amoenis, idolis immolabant. Isa. 65: sacrificaverunt super montes, et super colles exprobraverunt mihi. Amoena enim et excelsa semper idolis dedicantur. Second, with regard to the number of their misdeeds, on every high hill—on high and beautiful places they would sacrifice to their idols. They have sacrificed upon the mountains, and upon the hills they have reproached me (Isa 65:7). High and beautiful places are always dedicated to idols. Lectio 6 Lecture 6 Removal of excuses 2:21 Ego autem plantavi te vineam electam, omne semen verum. Quomodo ergo conversa es mihi in pravum, vinea aliena? 2:21 But I have planted you as my chosen vine, every seed true: how, then, have you become for me a perversion, a foreign vine? 2:22 Si laveris te nitro, et multiplicaveris tibi herbam berith, maculata es in iniquitate tua coram me, dicit Dominus Deus. 2:22 If you were to wash yourself with natron, and multiplied the herb berith unto yourself, still you would be stained with your iniquity before me, says the Lord. 2:23 Quomodo dicis: non sum polluta, post Baalim non ambulavi? Vide vias tuas in convalle, scito quid feceris. Cursor levis explicans vias suas, 2:23 How do you say, ‘I am not polluted; I have not walked after the Baalim’? Consider the roads in the valley and know what you have done; you are as a light runner running his paths.
