      Lectio 5 Lecture 5 Promise of shepherds 3:14b Et assumam vos, unum de civitate, et duos de cognatione, et introducam vos in Sion. 3:14b And I will take you, one from a city and two from a family, and I will lead you into Zion. 3:15 Et dabo vobis pastores juxta cor meum, et pascent vos scientia et doctrina. 3:15 And I will give you shepherds after my own heart, and they will feed you with knowledge and understanding. 3:16 Cumque multiplicati fueritis, et creveritis in terra in diebus illis, ait Dominus, non dicent ultra: arca testamenti Domini, neque ascendet super cor, neque recordabuntur illius, nec visitabitur, nec fiet ultra. 3:16 And when you have multiplied, and grown numerous in the land in those days, says the Lord, they shall not say anymore, the ark of the covenant of the Lord, neither shall it come into their heart, neither shall there be remembrance of it, neither shall it be visited, or be anymore. 3:17 In tempore illo vocabunt Jerusalem solium Domini, et congregabuntur ad eam omnes gentes in nomine Domini in Jerusalem, et non ambulabunt post pravitatem cordis sui pessimi. 3:17 In that time they will call Jerusalem the throne of the Lord; and all nations shall be gathered together in her in the name of the Lord in Jerusalem, and they will not walk after the depravity of their own wicked hearts. 3:18 In diebus illis ibit domus Juda ad domum Israel, et venient simul de terra aquilonis ad terram quam dedi patribus vestris. 3:18 In those days the house of Judah shall go forth to the house of Israel, and together they shall come from the land of the north to the land which I gave to your fathers. 20. Hic ponit revocatorum responsionem: 20. Here, he takes up the response of those who have been called back: et primo duarum tribuum, promittens eis restitutionem patriae: unum de civitate, quia dispersi erant, vel quia non omnes reversi. Ezech. 36: tollam quippe vos de gentibus, et congregabo vos de universis terris, et adducam vos in terram vestram. first, those of the two tribes, promising them the restitution of their native land: one from a city, because they were scattered, or because not all have come back. Indeed, I will take you from the nations, and I will gather you from every land, and I will lead you to your own land (Ezek 36:24). Et reintegrationem justitiae in emendatione praelatorum: et dabo vobis pastores juxta cor meum, et pascent vos scientia, quantum ad fidem, doctrina, quantum ad mores. Isa. 1: restituam judices tuos ut fuerunt prius, et consiliarios tuos sicut antiquitus. And the restoration of justice by the correction of their rulers: and I will give you shepherds after my own heart, and they will feed you with knowledge, regarding faith, and understanding, regarding morals. I will restore your judges as they were before, and your counsellors as they were of old (Isa 1:26). Tertio quantum ad augmentationem gloriae, quam signat ex praeteritorum oblivione: cumque multiplicati fueritis . . . non dicent ultra: arca testamenti Domini: quasi pro nihilo computabunt illud in quo modo gloriamini: quia arca absconsa fuit nec inventa; sicut dicitur 2 Mach. 1. Third, with regard to the increase of glory, which he signifies by the forgetting of what has come before: when you have multiplied . . . they shall not say anymore, the ark of the covenant of the Lord—as though they will account what they now glory in as nothing, because the ark had been hidden and not found, as it says in 2 Maccabees 1. Ex futurorum promissione: in tempore illo vocabunt Jerusalem, totam, quod completum fuit in fama sanctitatis in qua habebatur, sicut dicitur 1 Mach. 3. Ezech. 43: locus solii mei, et locus vestigiorum pedum meorum, ubi habito in medio filiorum Israel in aeternum. And from the promise of future things: in that time they will call Jerusalem, whole, because it has been completed by the reputation for sanctity in which it was held, as it says in 1 Maccabees 3. The place of my throne, and the place of the prints of my feet, where I dwell in the midst of the children of Israel forever (Ezek 43:7). Et quantum ad cultus ampliationem: et congregabuntur omnes gentes, ex omnibus aliqui: undique enim veniebant orare in Jerusalem. Vel intelligitur de conversione gentium ad fidem Christi. Ps. 85: omnes gentes quascumque fecisti, venient, et adorabunt coram te Domine. And with regard to the increase of worship: and all nations shall be gathered together, some will come from all peoples, for they shall come to pray in Jerusalem. Or it may be understood to speak of the conversion of the nations to the faith of Christ. All the nations, which you have made, will come and worship before you, O Lord (Ps 85:9). Secundo ponit conversionem decem tribuum: in diebus illis ibit domus Juda ad domum Israel: quia et ex illis qui redierunt, duabus tribubus adjuncti sunt. Quod notatur in hoc quod dicit: venient simul. Infra 50: in diebus illis, et in tempore illo, ait Dominus, venient filii Israel, ipsi, et filii Juda simul, ambulantes et flentes properabunt, et Dominum Deum suum quaerent. Quidam tamen exponunt totum hoc de ultimis temporibus. Second, he discusses the conversion of the ten tribes: in those days the house of Judah shall go forth to the house of Israel, because those who returned were joined to the two tribes. This is alluded to by the fact that it is said: together they shall come. As it is said below: in those days, and at that time, says the Lord, the children of Israel shall come, they and the children of Judah together, walking and weeping they shall make haste, and shall seek the Lord their God (Jer 50:4). Some, however, interpret all this to refer to the last times. Lectio 6 Lecture 6 Reason for mercy 3:19 Ego autem dixi: quomodo ponam te in filios, et tribuam tibi terram desiderabilem, haereditatem praeclaram exercituum gentium? Et dixi: patrem vocabis me, et post me ingredi non cessabis. 3:19 But I said: how shall I set you among the sons, and give you a desirable land, an excellent inheritance from among the hosts of the nations? And I said: you will call me ‘Father,’ and you will not cease to walk after me. 21. Hic assignat causam revocationis: 21. Here, he gives the reason for their being called back. et primo ponit quaestionem, quomodo? idest, quid potest movere quod tot bona tibi conferam, cum in tot peccatis sis? In filiis, idest in multitudine filiorum, exercituum gentium, idest ubi ab antiquo exercitus congregabantur, sicut usque hodie, propter ejus delicias. Psal. 105: pro nihilo habuerunt terram desiderabilem. Item Psalm. 15: funes ceciderunt mihi in praeclaris; etenim hereditas mea praeclara est mihi. First, he poses the question: how? That is, how is it possible that I should bestow so many good things upon you, when you languish in so many sins? Among the sons, that is, among a multitude of sons, the hosts of the nations, that is, where, since ancient times, armies have gathered, as they do even to this day, because of its allurements: they set at naught the desirable land (Ps 105:24). The lines have fallen to me in goodly places, for my inheritance is goodly to me (Ps 15:6). Secundo ponit responsionem, et dixi; idest, praevidens bona quae facies, haec tibi promitto. Psalm. 88: ipse invocabit me: pater meus es tu, Deus meus et susceptor salutis meae. Second, he gives the response: and I said, that is, foreseeing the good which you will do, I promise these things to you. He will call upon me: you are my father, my God and the support of my salvation (Ps 88:27). Lectio 7 Lecture 7 Shame after conversion 3:20 Sed quomodo si contemnat mulier amatorem suum, sic contempsit me domus Israel, dicit Dominus. 3:20 But as a woman that despises her lover, so has the house of Israel despised me, says the Lord. 3:21 Vox in viis audita est ploratus, et ululatus filiorum Israel: quoniam iniquam fecerunt viam suam, obliti sunt Domini Dei sui. 3:21 A voice was heard on the roads, the crying and the wailing of the children of Israel, for they have made their way wicked, and have forgotten the Lord their God. 3:22 Convertimini, filii revertentes, et sanabo aversiones vestras. Ecce nos venimus ad te: tu enim es Dominus Deus noster. 3:22 Turn back, wayward children, and I will heal your backslidings. Behold, we have come to you, for you are the Lord our God. 3:23 Vere mendaces erant colles, et multitudo montium. Vere in Domino Deo nostro salus Israel. 3:23 Surely the hills were deceitful, the multitude of the mountains; truly in the Lord our God is the salvation of Israel. 3:24 Confusio comedit laborem patrum nostrorum ab adolescentia nostra, greges eorum et armenta eorum, filios eorum et filias eorum. 3:24 Shame has devoured the labor of our fathers from our youth, their flocks and their herds, their sons and their daughters. 3:25 Dormiemus in confusione nostra, et operiet nos ignominia nostra: quoniam Domino Deo nostro peccavimus nos et patres nostri ab adolescentia nostra usque ad diem hanc, et non audivimus vocem Domini Dei nostri. 3:25 We will sleep in our shame, and our disgrace shall cover us, for we have sinned against the Lord our God, we and our fathers, from our youth until this day, and we have not listened to the voice of the Lord our God. 22. Hic ponit reversionis fructus: 22. Here he discusses the fruit of their return. et primo dicit necessitatem revocandi quantum ad ipsorum culpam, amatorem, non virum. Oseae 3: vade, et dilige mulierem dilectam ab amico, et adulteram. First, he speaks of the necessity of their being called back as regards their own guilt—lover, not husband. Go, and love a woman loved by a lover, an adulteress (Hos 3:1). Quantum ad poenam, vox in viis. Infra 31: vox in excelso audita est, lamentationis, luctus, et fletus, Rachel plorantis filios suos. Regarding the punishment, a voice was heard on the roads. As it says below, a voice was heard on high: lamentation, grief and wailing, Rachel weeping for her children (Jer 31:15). Secundo ponit conversionis utilitatem: convertimini, ut supra, aversiones, quibus a Deo aversi estis. Ps. 102: qui sanat omnes contritiones tuas. Isa. 45: convertimini ad me, et salvi eritis. Second, he discusses the usefulness of conversion: turn back, as it says above, backslidings, the things by which you have been turned away from God. Who heals all your infirmities (Ps 102:3). Turn back to me, and you will be saved (Isa 45:22). Tertio ponit eorum reversionem: Third, he treats of their return. et primo profitetur obedientiam: ecce venimus, sicut tu praecepisti. Oseae 6: venite, revertamur ad Dominum, quia ipse cepit, et sanabit nos; percutiet, et curabit nos. First, he acknowledges their obedience: behold, we have come, as you commanded. Come, let us return to the Lord, because he has taken and healed us; he will strike, and will cure us (Hos 6:1). Secundo confitentur Dei magnificentiam, confitentes ipsius divinitatem: tu enim es. Exod. 15: iste Deus meus, et glorificabo eum. Second, they confess the magnificence of God, professing his divinity: for you are the Lord our God. This is my God, and I will glorify him (Exod 15:2). Idolorum falsitatem: vere mendaces: quia non conferebant idola quae ibi colebamus, auxilium quod sperabamus, nec habebant divinitatem quam praetendebant. Isa. 28: posuimus mendacium spem nostram, et mendacio protecti sumus. Et divini cultus utilitatem: vere in Domino Deo nostro salus Israel. Ps. 61: in Deo salutare meum, Deus auxilii mei, et spes mea in Deo est. And the falsity of their idols: surely the hills were deceitful, because the idols which we worshiped there did not bring the help for which we hoped, nor did they have the divinity which they pretended. We have set a lie as our hope, and by a lie are we protected (Isa 28:15). And the usefulness of worshiping God: truly in the Lord our God is the salvation of Israel. In God is my salvation, the God of my help, and my hope is in God (Ps 61:7).
