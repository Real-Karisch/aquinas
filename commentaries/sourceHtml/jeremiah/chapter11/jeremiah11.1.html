      Caput 11 Chapter 11 Israel’s broken covenant Lectio 1 Lecture 1 The covenant recalled 11:1 Verbum quod factum est a Domino ad Jeremiam, dicens: 11:1 The word which came to Jeremiah from the Lord, saying: 11:2 audite verba pacti hujus, et loquimini ad viros Juda, et ad habitatores Jerusalem, 11:2 Hear the words of this covenant, and speak to the men of Judah, and to the inhabitants of Jerusalem, 11:3 et dices ad eos: haec dicit Dominus Deus Israel: maledictus vir qui non audierit verba pacti hujus, 11:3 and you shall say to them: thus says the Lord God of Israel: cursed is the man who will not hearken to the words of this covenant, 11:4 quod praecepi patribus vestris in die qua eduxi eos de terra Aegypti, de fornace ferrea, dicens: audite vocem meam, et facite omnia quae praecipio vobis, et eritis, mihi in populum, et ego ero vobis in Deum, 11:4 which I commanded to your fathers, on the day when I led them from the land of Egypt, from the furnace of iron, saying: hear my voice, and do all that I command you, and you shall be my people, and I will be your God: 11:5 ut suscitem juramentum quod juravi patribus vestris, daturum me eis terram fluentem lacte et melle, sicut est dies haec. Et respondi, et dixi: amen, Domine. 11:5 That I may stir up the oath which I swore to your fathers, that I would give them a land flowing with milk and honey, as it is today. And I answered, and said: amen, O Lord. 11:6 Et dixit Dominus ad me: vociferare omnia verba haec in civitatibus Juda, et foris Jerusalem, dicens: audite verba pacti hujus, et facite illa. 11:6 And the Lord said to me: proclaim all these words in the cities of Judah, and in the streets of Jerusalem, saying: hear the words of this covenant, and do them. 77. Hic ostendit populi dignitatem ex hoc quod cum Deo pacto inito foederati erant: 77. Here, he shows the dignity of the people from the fact that, having entered into a covenant with God, they were leagued together. et primo ponit pactum, et transgredientibus comminatur poenam; First, he speaks of the covenant, and he threatens punishment to those who transgress it. secundo disputat de punientis justitia, cap. 12: justus quidem tu es, Domine. Second, he examines the justice of punishing them: indeed, you are just, O Lord (Jer 12:1). Circa primum duo. Concerning the first, there are two points. Primo proponit confoederationem; First, he sets forth the covenant; secundo foederis transgressionem, ibi, quia contestans contestatus sum. second, their transgression of the covenant, at for I strongly warned (Jer 11:7). Circa primum duo. Concerning the first, there are two points. Primo inducitur propheta ad annuntiandum, audite tu, et qui tecum sunt: boni sunt, et ita quasi privilegiati. First, the prophet is urged to proclaim: hear, which is addressed to you and those who are with you; they are good, and as such, are in a sense privileged. Et dices ad eos. De hoc pacto Ezech. 16: operui ignominiam tuam, et juravi tibi, et ingressus sum pactum tecum, ait Dominus Deus, et facta es mihi. And you shall say to them. Ezekiel speaks of this covenant: I have covered your shame, and sworn to you, and entered into a covenant with you, says the Lord God, and you are mine (Ezek 16:18). Secundo inducitur populus ad observandum: Second, the people are urged to keep the covenant. et primo proponit rationes duas, First, he sets forth two reasons: quarum primam sumit a poena transgredientium, maledictus vir. Deut. 27: maledictus vir qui non permanserit in sermonibus legis hujus, nec eos opere perfecerit. the first of which he takes from the punishment of those who transgress: cursed is the man. Cursed is the man who will not abide in the words of this law, and not fulfill them in deed (Deut 27:26). Et assignat justitiam: in die qua eduxi eos de terra Aegypti; quasi dicat: justum est puniri illos qui post tot beneficia ingrati sunt: fornace, propter afflictionem, ferrea, propter duram dominationem. Deut. 4: vos autem tulit Dominus, et eduxit de fornace ferrea Aegypti, ut haberet populum hereditarium, sicut est in praesenti die. Et ponit pacti formam: dicens, in quo aliquid petit, scilicet obedientiam: audite, et aliquid promittit, scilicet divinae familiaritatis gloriam: et eritis mihi in populum, et ego ero vobis in Deum. Supra 7: hoc verbum praecepi eis dicens: audite vocem meam, et ero vobis Deus, et vos eritis mihi populus, etc. And he shows the justice of the punishment: on the day when I led them from the land of Egypt—it is as though he were saying, “It is just to punish them, who are ungrateful after having received so many kindnesses”; from the furnace, so called because of their affliction, of iron, because of the cruel domination. But the Lord took you from the iron furnace of Egypt, so that he might have a people of inheritance, as you are today (Deut 4:20). Then, he speaks of the form of the covenant: saying—in which he asks for something, namely, obedience—hear, and he promises something, namely the glory of friendship with God: and you shall be my people, and I will be your God. Above: this thing did I command them, saying: hear my voice, and I will be a God to you, and you shall be my people (Jer 7:23). Secundam rationem sumit ex praemio observantium: ut suscitem, quia jam quasi periit captivitate imminente, lacte, propter abundantiam animalium, melle, propter abundantiam terrae nascentium. Vel hyperbolice in his duobus abundantia omnium bonorum designatur. Deut. 6: multipliceris amplius, sicut pollicitus est Dominus Deus patrum tuorum tibi terram lacte, et melle manantem. The second reason he takes from the reward of those who obey: that I may stir up, what had almost become void, because of the coming captivity; with milk, because of the abundance of animals, and honey, because of the abundance of plants springing from the earth. Alternatively, through hyperbole, an abundance of every good thing is signified by these two. That you may increase yet more in a land flowing with milk and honey, as the Lord God of your fathers has promised you (Deut 6:3). Secundo ponitur prophetae assensio: respondi, amen; idest, vere fecisti quod promisisti; vel fiat hoc quod promittis. Deut. 27: et dicet omnis populus, amen. Second, he gives the assent of the prophet: I answered, amen, that is, you have truly done what you promised: and all the people shall say, amen (Deut 27:26). Tertio inducitur conclusio, ut scilicet observent: et dixit Dominus. Isa. 40: exalta in fortitudine vocem tuam. Third, the conclusion is drawn that they should give heed: and the Lord said. Raise your voice in strength (Isa 40:9). Lectio 2 Lecture 2 The covenant’s transgression 11:7 Quia contestans contestatus sum patres vestros in die qua eduxi eos de terra Aegypti usque ad diem hanc. Mane consurgens contestatus sum, et dixi: audite vocem meam; 11:7 For I strongly warned your fathers, on the day when I brought them forth from the land of Egypt until this day; rising early I warned them, and said: listen to my voice. 11:8 et non audierunt, nec inclinaverunt aurem suam; sed abierunt unusquisque in pravitate cordis sui mali. Et induxi super eos omnia verba pacti hujus quod praecepi ut facerent, et non fecerunt. 11:8 But they did not listen, nor did they incline their ear, but turned away, each one in the depravity of his own evil heart; and I brought all the words of this covenant upon them, which I commanded them to do, and they did not do. 11:9 Et dixit Dominus ad me: inventa est conjuratio in viris Juda, et in habitatoribus Jerusalem. 11:9 And the Lord said to me: a conspiracy has been found among the men of Judah, and the inhabitants of Jerusalem. 11:10 Reversi sunt ad iniquitates patrum suorum priores, qui noluerunt audire verba mea. Et hi ergo abierunt post deos alienos, ut servirent eis, irritum fecerunt domus Israel et domus Juda pactum meum, quod pepigi cum patribus eorum. 11:10 They have turned back to the former iniquities of their fathers, and they refused to listen to my words; therefore, they have gone after strange gods, that they might serve them; the house of Israel and the house of Judah have made void my covenant, which I made with their fathers. 78. Hic ostendit quomodo pactum transgressi sunt: 78. Here, he shows how they have transgressed the covenant: et primo peccando in Deum; first, by sinning against God; secundo peccando in prophetam, ibi, tu autem, Domine, demonstrasti mihi, et cognovi. second, by sinning against the prophet, at but you, O Lord, have shown me, and I have known (Jer 11:18).
