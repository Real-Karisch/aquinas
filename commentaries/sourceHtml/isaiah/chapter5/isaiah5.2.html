      157. Quantum ad electionem palmitum dicitur et plantavit eam electam, id est ex electis palmitibus; unde alia translatio habet vinea soreth, quod est nobilissimum genus vitis, in quo significatur bonitas patrum, Jer. II 21 ego te plantavi vineam electam, omne semen verum. 157. As to choosing of branches is said: and planted it with the choicest, that is, from the choicest branches; hence another translation has, soreth vine, which is the noblest kind of vine, in which the goodness of their fathers is signified: I planted you a chosen vineyard, all true seed (Jer 2:21). Quantum ad sumptum aedificii, quod pertinet ad defensionem, dicit aedificavit in medio ejus turrim; in quo ostenditur regia dignitas, Ez. XVI 13 decora facta es vehementer nimis et profecisti in regnum, Cant. IV 4 turris David aedificata est cum propugnaculis. Vel templum, secundum alios. Usus turris ad conservationem fructus, et prospiciendum ad custodiam. As to the cost of the building, which pertains to defense, he says: and built a tower in the midst thereof; in which is shown royal dignity: you were made exceeding beautiful: and were advanced to be a queen (Ezek 16:13); the tower of David, which is built with bulwarks (Song 4:4). Or the temple, according to others. A tower is used for the preservation of fruit and for looking out to keep guard. Quantum ad fructus collectionem dicit torcular, Prov. III 10 implebuntur horrea tua saturitate, et vino torcularia redundabunt; in quo notatur altare holocaustorum propter effusionem sanguinis. As to the gathering of fruit, he says: and set up a winepress therein: and your barns shall be filled with abundance, and your presses shall run over with wine (Prov 3:10); in which the altar of holocausts is noted because of the outpouring of blood. 158. Et expectavi ut faceret. Hic ponitur malitia fructus. Unde dicit et expectavi, secundum ordinem beneficiorum, ut faceret uvas, fructum bonorum operum, et fecit labruscas, id est uvas sylvestres, ab hoc dictae quia in labris viarum nascuntur, scilicet sepibus: in quo amaritudo vitiorum notatur, Jer. II 21 quomodo conversa es mihi in pravum vinea aliena?, Mich. VII 4 qui optimus est in eis, quasi paliurus, et qui rectus quasi spina de sepe. 158. And he looked that it should bring forth. Here the wickedness of the fruit is set out; hence he says, and he looked, following the order of benefits, that it should bring forth grapes, the fruit of good works, and it brought forth wild grapes (labruscas), so called because in they grow on the lips (labiis) of the roads, namely on fences: in which the bitterness of their vices is noted: how are you turned unto me into that which is good for nothing, O strange vineyard? (Jer 2:21); he that is best among them, is as a brier, and he that is righteous, as the thorn of the hedge (Mic 7:4). 159. Nunc ergo habitatores. Hic ponitur interrogatio judicii; et circa hoc duo facit. 159. And now, O you inhabitants. Here the questioning of the court is set out, and concerning this, he does two things. Primo interpellat judices, dicens: ergo quia feci quod debui, nunc, audita ratione mea, judicate inter me et vineam meam, Oseae II 2 judicate matrem vestram, judicate: quoniam non ipsa uxor mea, et ego non vir ejus. Simile judicium quaerebatur a David de se quasi de alio, II Regum XII, super peccato commisso ab eo, circa scilicet adulterium et homicidium Uriae Cethei. First, he beseeches the judges, saying: therefore, because I did what I ought to have done, now, my reason having been heard, judge between me and my vineyard: judge your mother, judge her: because she is not my wife, and I am not her husband (Hos 2:2). In 2 Samuel 12, a similar judgment is sought by David of himself, as if of another man, upon the sin committed by him, namely, concerning his adultery and murder of Uriah the Hittite. 160. Secundo quaerit judicium; et duo quaerit. 160. Second, he seeks judgment; and he asks two things. Primo utrum peccaverit ex negligentia, unde dicit quid ultra, supra dicta, facere debui? Mich. VI 3 popule meus, quid feci tibi, aut quid molestus fui tibi? Responde mihi. First, whether he had sinned from negligence; hence he says: what is there that I ought to do more, beyond what has been said? O my people, what have I done to you, or in what have I molested you? Answer you me (Mic 6:3). Secundo utrum peccaverit in cultura vineae ex superflua cura, unde dicit: aut forte videor fecisse ultra debitum quod expectavi, bene colendo, ut faceret uvas, et fecit labruscas? Quasi dicat: in hoc videor potius superfluus quod vineae meae tantam culturam adhibui, infra XXVI 15 Domine, indulsisti genti, numquid glorificatus es?, et XLVIII 8 transgressorem ex ventre vocavi te propter nomen meum, Jer. XI 16 ad vocem loquelae grandis exarsit ignis in ea, et combusta sunt frutecta ejus. Second, whether he had sinned in the cultivation of the vineyard from excessive care; hence he says: perhaps I seem to have done contrary to what was due in that I looked, tending it well, that it should bring forth grapes, and it has brought forth wild grapes? As if to say: in this I seem rather to be excessive, that I have applied such cultivation to my vineyard, below: O Lord, you have been favorable to the nation: are you glorified? (Isa 26:15); and: I have called you a transgressor from the womb for my name’s sake (Isa 48:8–9); at the noise of a word, a great fire was kindled in it, and the branches thereof are burnt (Jer 11:16). 161. Nota super illo verbo vinea facta est dilecto quod est vinea multiplex. 161. Note on the words, my beloved had a vineyard (Isa 5:1), that the vineyard is manifold. Primo carnalis concupiscentiae, Deut. XXXII 32 de vinea Sodomorum vinum eorum: First, the vineyard of carnal concupiscence: their vines are of the vineyard of Sodom, and of the suburbs of Gomorrah (Deut 32:32): et hujus est primo vinum irae, Deut. XXXII 33 fel draconum vinum eorum; and the first wine of this vineyard is the wine of wrath: their wine is the gall of dragons (Deut 32:33); secundo luxuriae, Apoc. XVIII 3 de vino fornicationis ejus biberunt omnes gentes; second, the wine of lust: all nations have drunk of the wine . . . of her fornication (Rev 18:3); tertio ineptae laetitiae, II Esdrae IX omnem mentem vertit in securitatem et jocunditatem. third, the wine of unseemly joy: it turns every mind into security and cheerfulness (3 Esdras 3:20). 162. Secundo est vinea fidelis animae, Cant. II 15 vinea floruit: 162. Second is the vineyard of the faithful soul: our vineyard has flourished (Song 2:15): hujus vineae est vinum primo paenitentiae, Lucae X 34 approprians alligavit vulnera ejus, fundens vinum et oleum; the first wine in this vineyard is the wine of penance: going up to him, bound up his wounds, pouring in oil and wine (Luke 10:34); secundo corporalis munditiae, Zach. IX 17 quid enim bonum ejus est, et quid pulchrum ejus, nisi frumentum electorum et vinum germinans virgines?; second, the wine of bodily purity: for what is the good thing of him, and what is his beautiful thing, but the corn of the elect, and wine springing forth virgins? (Zech 9:17); tertio spiritualis laetitiae, Ps. vinum laetificat cor hominis. third, the wine of spiritual joy: and that wine may cheer the heart of man (Ps 103[104]:15). 163. Tertio est vinea militantis Ecclesiae, Cant. ult. vinea fuit pacifico in ea quae habet populos: 163. Third is the vineyard of the Church militant: the peaceable had a vineyard, in that which has people (Song 8:11): et hujus vineae est vinum primo sacrae ablutionis, Num. XV 10 et vinum ad liba fundenda ejusdem mensurae; and the first wine of this vineyard is the wine of the holy washing: and wine for libations of the same measure (Num 15:5); secundo fructuosae praedicationis, Ps. a fructu frumenti, vini et olei multiplicati sunt; second, the wine of fruitful preaching: by the fruit of their corn, their wine, and oil, they rest (Ps 4:8[7]); tertio devotae confessionis, Cant. VII 9 guttur tuum sicut vinum optimum. third, the wine of devout confession: your throat like the best wine (Song 7:9). 164. Quarto est vinea caelestis patriae, III Regum XXI 1 vinea erat Naboth Jezrahelite: 164. Fourth is the vineyard of heaven: Naboth the Jezrahelite had at that time a vineyard (1 Kgs 21:1): et hujus vineae est vinum primo divinae fruitionis, Cant. VIII comedi favum cum melle meo, bibi vinum cum lactae meo; and the first wine of this vineyard is the wine of divine enjoyment: I have eaten the honeycomb with my honey, I have drunk my wine with my milk (Song 5:1); secundo interioris repletionis, Esther I 7 vinum quoque, ut magnificentia regia dignum erat, abundans et praecipuum ponebatur; second, the wine of inner fullness: wine also in abundance and of the best was presented, as was worthy of a king’s magnificence (Esth 1:7); tertio sanctorum congratulationis, Cant. ult. dabo tibi poculum ex vino condito. third, the wine of the fellowship of joy of the saints: I will give you a cup of spiced wine (Song 8:2). 165. Et nunc ostendam vobis. Hic profertur sententia. 165. And now I will show you. Here the sentence is pronounced. Et primo excitat attentionem vel audientiam, dicens nunc, ex quo non vultis sententiam proferre, ego ostendam vobis quid ego faciam vineae meae, infra XLVIII 5–6 praedixi tibi ex tunc, antequam venirent indicavi tibi, ne forte diceres: idola mea fecerunt haec, et sculptilia mea et conflatilia mandaverunt ista quae audisti. And first, he calls for attention or a hearing, saying: now, because you do not want to pronounce sentence, I myself will show you what I will do to my vineyard, below: I foretold you of old, before they came to pass I told you, lest you should say: my idols have done these things, and my graven and molten things have commanded these things which you have heard (Isa 48:5–6). 166. Secundo profert justam sententiam ut ingratis auferat beneficium exhibitum, cum dicit auferam sepem ejus. 166. Second, he pronounces the just sentence that he should take away from the ungrateful the benefits he has furnished, when he says, I will take away the hedge thereof. Et primo aufert beneficium quod pertinet ad custodiam, quae est duplex: angelorum, et quantum ad hoc dicit auferam sepem ejus, id est auxilia angelorum, quibus contra hostes protegebatur, et erit in direptionem, gentibus, Eccli. XXXVI 27 ubi non est sepes, diripietur possessio; est etiam custodia praelatorum, et quantum ad hoc dicit diruam maceriam ejus, id est praesidia regni, de quibus supra II 17 et incurvabitur omnis sublimitas hominum, et erit in conculcationem, id est vilis facta, Ps. destruxisti maceriam ejus. And first, he takes away the benefit which pertains to protection, which is twofold: that of the angels, and as to this he says, I will take away the hedge thereof, that is, the help of the angels, by whom it was protected against enemies, and it shall be wasted, by the gentiles: where there is no hedge, the possession shall be spoiled (Sir 36:37[30]); there is also the protection of superiors, and as to this he says, I will break down the wall thereof, that is, the garrisons of the kingdom, of which it says above: and all loftiness of men shall be bowed down (Isa 2:17): and it shall be trodden down, that is, made base: you have broken down the hedge thereof (Ps 79:13[80:12]). 167. Secundo aufert beneficium quod pertinet ad cultus diligentiam, unde dicit et ponam eam desertam, id est relinquam eam incultam sicut desertum; non putabitur, admonitione aut correptionis verbere quando peccat, et non fodietur, ut auferantur ab eis occasiones mali, sicut malae herbae; et ascendent super eam vepres et spinae, id est majora et minora peccata; vel vepres peccatorum, spinae tribulationum, infra VII 24 vepres enim et spinae erunt in universa terra. 167. Second, he takes away the benefit which pertains to diligence of cultivation; hence he says: and I will make it desolate, that is, I will leave it uncultivated like a desert; it shall not be pruned, by the lash of admonition or correction when they sin, and it shall not be dug, that occasions of evil might be taken away from them like weeds; but briers and thorns shall come up, that is, major and minor sins; or briers of sins, thorns of tribulations, below: for briers and thorns shall be in all the land (Isa 7:24). 168. Tertio aufert beneficium fertilitatis, contra quod ponit defectum pluviae, unde dicit et nubibus mandabo ne pluant, ad litteram; vel nubes: praedicatores, infra LX 8 qui sunt isti qui ut nubes volant, et quasi columbae ad fenestras suas?, Jer. III 3 quamobrem prohibitae sunt stillae pluviarum, et serotinus imber non fuit. 168. Third, he takes away the benefit of fertility, against which he sets out lack of rain; hence he says: and I will command the clouds to rain no rain upon it, literally; or the clouds represent preachers, below: who are these, that fly as clouds, and as doves to their windows? (Isa 60:8); therefore the showers were withholden, and there was no lateward rain (Jer 3:3). 169. Vinea enim Domini exercituum. Hic exponit metaphoram: 169. For the vineyard of the Lord of hosts. Here he explains the metaphor: et primo quantum ad vineam, and first, as to the vineyard, secundo quantum ad vineae causam, ibi expectavi ut faceret judicium. second as to the trial of the vineyard, where it says, I looked that he should do judgment. Circa primum exponit vineam, dicens vinea enim domus Israel est, vel tota, vel quantum ad decem tribus quae dictae sunt Israel post separationem regni propter multitudinem. Concerning the first, he explains the vineyard saying, for the vineyard of the Lord of hosts is the house of Israel, either all of Israel, or as to the ten tribes, which were called Israel after the separation of the kingdom because of their multitude (1 Kgs 12; 2 Chr 10).
