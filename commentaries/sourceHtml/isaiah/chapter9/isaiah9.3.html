      310. Item notandum super illo verbo super humerum ejus, quod Deus posuit super humerum Christi 310. Likewise, it is to be noted on the words, upon his shoulder (Isa 9:6), that God laid upon the shoulder of Christ primo peccata, sicut supra satisfactorem, Is. LIII 6 Dominus posuit super eum iniquitatem omnium nostrum; first, sins, as upon one who makes satisfaction: the Lord has laid upon him the iniquity of us all (Isa 53:6); secundo clavem, sicut supra sacerdotem, Is. XXII 22 et dabo clavem domus David super humerum ejus, et aperiet et non erit qui claudat; second, a key, as upon a priest: and I will lay the key of the house of David upon his shoulder: and he shall open, and none shall shut (Isa 22:22); tertio principatum, sicut supra dominatorem, Is. IX 6 factus est principatus super humerum ejus; third, the government, as upon a ruler: the government is upon his shoulder (Isa 9:6); quarto gloriam, sicut supra triumphatorem, Is. XXII 24 et suspendam super eum omnem gloriam domus patris ejus. fourth, glory, as upon a conqueror: and I shall hang upon him all the glory of his father’s house (Isa 22:24). In omnibus his non est aversus furor ejus, sed adhuc manus ejus extenta. 9:12 The Syrians from the east, and the Philistines from the west: and they shall devour Israel with their whole mouth. For all this his indignation is not turned away, but his hand is stretched out still. 9:13 Et populus non est reversus ad percutientem se, et Dominum exercituum non inquisierunt. 9:13 And the people are not returned to him who has struck them, and have not sought after the Lord of hosts. 9:14 Et disperdet Dominus ab Israel caput et caudam, incurvantem et depravantem in die una. 9:14 And the Lord shall destroy out of Israel the head and the tail, him that bends down, and him that corrupts, in one day. 9:15 Longævus et honorabilis ipse est caput, et propheta docens mendacium ipse cauda est. 9:15 The aged and honorable, he is the head: and the prophet that teaches lies, he is the tail. 9:16 Et erunt qui beatificant populum istum seducentes, et qui beatificantur præcipitati. 9:16 And they that call this people blessed, shall cause them to err: and they that are called blessed, shall be thrown down, headlong. 9:17 Propter hoc super adolescentulis ejus non lætabitur Dominus et pupillorum ejus et viduarum non miserebitur, quia omnis hypocrita est et nequam, et universum os locutum est stultitiam. Verbum misit Dominus in Iacob. Hic exequitur ordinem punitionis secundum diversitatem poenarum. 311. The Lord sent a word into Jacob. Here he describes the order of punishment according to the diversity of punishments. Et primo commemorat culpam, And first, he recalls the fault, secundo exequitur poenam: et elevabit. second, he describes the punishment: and the Lord shall set up (Isa 9:11). Arguit autem in eis culpam dupliciter. And he denounces the fault in them in two ways. Primo quantum ad transgressionem divini praecepti, unde dicit verbum misit Dominus in Jacob, id est in Juda, vel in toto populo, ut de Juda constituerentur duces, Gen. penult. non auferetur sceptrum de Juda, et dux de femore ejus, Judicum IX, I Reg. XV; et cecidit in Israel, id est frustratum est in Israel, id est in decem tribubus quae dimiserunt domum David, III Reg. XII 19–20. Vel verbum legis universaliter, Oseae IV 15 si fornicaris tu, Israel, non delinquat saltem Juda. First, as to the transgression of divine precept; hence he says, the Lord sent a word into Jacob, that is, into Judah, or into the whole people, as the leaders were appointed from Judah: the scepter shall not be taken away from Judah, nor a ruler from his thigh (Gen 49:10; cf. Judg 9; 1 Sam 15). And it has lighted upon Israel, that is, it was frustrated in Israel, that is, in the ten tribes, who rejected the house of David (1 Kgs 12:19–20). Or the word of the law universally: if you play the harlot, O Israel, at least let not Judah offend (Hos 4:15). 312. Secundo quantum ad executionem facti, inquantum dicebant se bene fecisse caecati superbia, quia tumor mentis obstaculum est veritatis, et ideo dicit et omnis populus Ephraim, id est decem tribuum, et habitantes Samariam, quantum ad principes populi, sciet, id est recognoscet se et culpam suam in poena, quia oculos quos culpa claudit, poena aperit. Dicentes in superbia et magnitudine cordis, inquantum in corde suo super alios se aestimabant, et magnitudine, quantum ad praesumptionem magnorum quae proponebant. Lateres, id est reges de Juda qui erant infirmi, ceciderunt a nobis, sed quadris lapidibus, id est fortibus regibus ex nobis, aedificabimus regnum nostrum. Succiderunt, scilicet hostes, ut Syri, sycomoros, id est reges Judae infructuosos; cedros, id est sublimes ex nobis, immutabimus, id est ponemus loco illorum, supra VII 6 ponamus regem in medio ejus filium Tabeel. De superbia eorum infra XXVIII 1 vae coronae superbiae ebriis Ephraim. 312. Second, as to the excusing of the deed, inasmuch as they said that they had done well, being blinded by pride, for swelling of the mind is an obstacle to the truth, and therefore he says, and all the people of Ephraim, that is, the ten tribes, and the inhabitants of Samaria, as to the princes of the people, shall know, that is, they shall recollect themselves and their fault in the punishment, for punishment opens the eyes which fault closes. That say in the pride and haughtiness of their heart, inasmuch as in their heart they supposed that they were above others, and haughtiness, as to the presumption of the great things they proposed. The bricks, that is, the kings of Judah, who were weak, are fallen down, away from us, but we will build our kingdom with square stones, that is, strong kings from among ourselves. They, namely, our enemies, as the Syrians, have cut down the sycamores, that is, the unfruitful kings of Judah; we will change them, that is, set up in their place, cedars, that is, lofty men from among ourselves, above: and make the son of Tabeel king in the midst thereof (Isa 7:6). On their pride, below: woe to the crown of pride, to the drunkards of Ephraim (Isa 28:1). 313. Et elevabit. Hic exequitur diversitatem poenarum quantum ad tria in quibus confidebant. 313. And the Lord shall set up. Here he describes the diversity of punishments as to three things which they trusted in. Primo comminatur contra foederationem vicinorum; First, he threatens against the alliance of neighbors; secundo contra utilitatem personarum quas se aestimabant habere: et disperdet Dominus; second, against the use of persons whom they suppose that they have: and the Lord shall destroy (Isa 9:14); tertio contra multitudinem populorum: succensa est. third, against the multitude of peoples: for wickedness is kindled (Isa 9:18). Circa primum duo facit: Concerning the first, he does two things. primo ponitur punitio, First, the punishment is set out; secundo ulterioris poenae praeparatio: in omnibus. second, the preparation of further punishment: for all this (Isa 9:12). Comminatur autem duo: And he threatens two things: primo futuram destructionem: et levabit Dominus hostes Rasin, id est Assyrios, in tumultum belli, de quo IV Reg. 16 9; first, future destruction: and the Lord shall set up the enemies of Rasin, that is, the Assyrians, in a crowd, of war, concerning which, see 2 Kings 16:9;
