      Caput 45 Chapter 45 45:1 Hæc dicit Dominus christo meo Cyro, cujus apprehendi dexteram ut subjiciam ante faciem ejus gentes et dorsa regum vertam et aperiam ante eum januas et portæ non claudentur: 45:1 Thus says the Lord to my anointed Cyrus, whose right hand I have taken hold of, to subdue nations before his face, and to turn the backs of kings, and to open the doors before him, and the gates shall not be shut. 45:2 Ego ante te ibo et gloriosos terræ humiliabo; portas æreas conteram et vectes ferreos confringam. 45:2 I will go before you, and will humble the great ones of the earth: I will break in pieces the gates of brass, and will burst the bars of iron. 45:3 Et dabo tibi thesauros absconditos et arcana secretorum, ut scias quia ego Dominus qui voco nomen tuum Deus Israel, 45:3 And I will give you hidden treasures, and the concealed riches of secret places: that you may know that I am the Lord who call you by your name, the God of Israel. 45:4 propter servum meum Jacob et Israel electum meum, et vocavi te in nomine tuo, assimilavi te et non cognovisti me. 45:4 For the sake of my servant Jacob, and Israel my elect, I have even called you by your name: I have made you like, and you have not known me. 45:5 Ego Dominus, et non est amplius, extra me non est deus; accinxi te, et non cognovisti me; 45:5 I am the Lord, and there is none else: there is no god besides me: I girded you, and you have not known me: 45:6 ut sciant hi qui ab ortu solis et qui ab occidente, quoniam absque me non est deus. Ego Dominus, et non est alter deus, 45:6 That they may know who are from the rising of the sun, and they who are from the west, that there is none besides me. I am the Lord, and there is no other god: 45:7 formans lucem et creans tenebras, faciens pacem et creans malum: ego Dominus faciens omnia hæc. 45:7 I form the light, and create darkness, I make peace, and create evil: I the Lord that do all these things. 45:8 Rorate cæli desuper et nubes pluant justum, aperiatur terra et germinet salvatorem, et justitia oriatur simul: ego Dominus creavi eum. 45:8 Drop down dew, you heavens, from above, and let the clouds rain the just: let the earth be opened, and bud forth a savior: and let justice spring up together: I the Lord have created him. 45:9 Væ qui contradicit fictori suo, testa de samiis terræ! Numquid dicit lutum figulo suo: quid facis, et opus tuum absque manibus est? 45:9 Woe to him that gainsays his maker, a sherd of the earthen pots: shall the clay say to him that fashions it: what are you making, and your work is without hands? 45:10 Væ qui dicit patri: quid generas? Et mulieri: quid parturis? 45:10 Woe to him that says to his father: why did you beget? And to the woman: why do you bring forth? 45:11 Hæc dicit Dominus Sanctus Israel, plastes ejus: ventura interrogate me super filios meos et super opera manuum mearum mandate mihi. 45:11 Thus says the Lord the Holy One of Israel, his maker: ask me of things to come, concerning my children, and concerning the work of my hands give charge to me. 45:12 Ego feci terram et hominem super eam creavi ego, manus meæ extenderunt cælos, et omni militiæ eorum mandavi. 45:12 I made the earth: and I created man upon it: my hand stretched forth the heavens, and I have commanded all their host. 45:13 Ego suscitavi eum ad justitiam et omnes vias ejus dirigam. Ipse ædificabit civitatem meam et captivitatem meam dimittet, non in pretio neque in muneribus, dicit Dominus exercituum. 45:13 I have raised him up to justice, and I will direct all his ways: he shall build my city, and let go my captives, not for ransom, nor for presents, says the Lord of hosts. 45:14 Hæc dicit Dominus Deus: labor Ægypti et negotiatio Æthiopiæ et Sabaim viri sublimes ad te transibunt et tui erunt; post te ambulabunt, vincti manicis pergent et te adorabunt teque deprecabuntur. Tantum in te est Deus et non est absque te deus; 45:14 Thus says the Lord God: the labor of Egypt, and the business of Ethiopia, and of Sabaim, men of stature shall come over to you, and shall be thine: they shall walk after you, they shall go bound with manacles: and they shall worship you, and shall make supplication to you: only in you is God, and there is no God besides you. 45:15 vere tu es Deus absconditus, Deus Israel salvator. 45:15 Verily you are a hidden God, the God of Israel the savior. 45:16 Confusi sunt et erubuerunt omnes simul, abierunt in confusionem fabricatores errorum. 45:16 They are all confounded and ashamed: the forgers of errors are gone together into confusion. 45:17 Israel salvatus est in Domino salute æterna; non confundemini et non erubescetis usque in sæculum sæculi. 45:17 Israel is saved in the Lord with an eternal salvation: you shall not be confounded, and you shall not be ashamed for ever and ever. 45:18 Quia hæc dicit Dominus creans cælos, ipse est Deus formans terram et faciens eam, ipse plastes ejus. Non in vanum creavit eam, sed ut habitetur formavit eam: ego Dominus, et non est alius. 45:18 For thus says the Lord that created the heavens, God himself that formed the earth, and made it, the very maker thereof: he did not create it in vain: he formed it to be inhabited. I am the Lord, and there is no other. 45:19 Non in abscondito locutus sum, in loco terræ tenebroso. Non dixi semini Jacob: frustra quærite me: ego Dominus loquens justitiam, annuntians recta. 45:19 I have not spoken in secret, in a dark place of the earth: I have not said to the seed of Jacob: seek me in vain. I am the Lord that speak justice, that declare right things. 45:20 Congregamini et venite et accedite simul qui salvati estis ex gentibus: nescierunt qui levant lignum signum sculpturæ suæ et rogant deum non salvantem; 45:20 Assemble yourselves, and come, and draw near together, you that are saved of the Gentiles: they have no knowledge that set up the wood of their graven work, and pray to a god that cannot save. 45:21 annuntiate et venite et consiliamini simul. Quis auditum fecit hoc ab initio, ex tunc prædixit illud? Numquid non ego Dominus, et non est ultra deus absque me? Deus justus et salvans non est præter me. 45:21 Tell, and come, and consult together: who has declared this from the beginning, who has foretold this from that time? Have not I the Lord, and there is no God else besides me? A just God and a savior, there is none besides me. 45:22 Convertimini ad me, et salvi eritis, omnes fines terræ, quia ego Dominus, et non est alius. 45:22 Be converted to me, and you shall be saved, all you ends of the earth: for I am the Lord, and there is no other. 45:23 In memet ipso juravi; egredietur de ore meo justitiæ verbum et non revertetur, 45:23 I have sworn by myself, the word of justice shall go out of my mouth, and shall not return: 45:24 quia mihi curvabitur omne genu, et jurabit omnis lingua. 45:24 For every knee shall be bowed to me, and every tongue shall swear. 45:25 Ergo in Domino dicet: meæ sunt justitiæ et imperium; ad eum venient et confundentur omnes qui repugnant ei. 45:25 Therefore shall he say: in the Lord are my justices and empire: they shall come to him, and all that resist him shall be confounded. 45:26 In Domino justificabitur et laudabitur omne semen Israel. 45:26 In the Lord shall all the seed of Israel be justified and praised. 857. Haec dicit Dominus christo meo etc. Postquam confortavit eos ad firmam expectationem divinorum promissorum, in parte ista incipit prosequi divinas promissiones ad eorum consolationem. Et dividitur in duas: 857. Thus says the Lord to my anointed. After he strengthened them to the firm expectation of divine promises, in this part, he begins to describe the divine promises for their consolation. And this is divided into two parts: in prima promittit liberationem a malis, in the first, he promises liberation from evils; in secunda promittit salvationem in bonis, lvj cap. haec dicit Dominus: custodite judicium etc. in the second, he promises salvation in good things, thus says the Lord: keep judgment (ch. 56). Prima in duas: The first of these is divided into two: in prima promittit eis liberationis beneficium, in the first, he promises them the benefit of liberation; in secunda excludit omne liberationis impedimentum, l cap. haec dicit Dominus: quis est hic libellus etc. in the second, he excludes every impediment to liberation, thus says the Lord: what is this bill of the divorce (ch. 50).
