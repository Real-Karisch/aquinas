      primo describit hostem persequentem, first, he describes the enemy who persecutes them; secundo persecutionem: mulieres. second, the persecution: rise up, you rich women (Isa 32:9). Circa primum tria. Concerning the first, he sets out three things. Primo ipsius regis nequitiam quantum ad blasphemiam oris: stultus, scilicet Senacherib, fatua verba blasphemiae, infra XXXVII, Prov. XV 2 os fatuorum ebullit stultitiam; quantum ad duplicitatem cordis: cor ejus faciet, promittens populo bonam terram, ad Dominum fraudulenter, dicens se illius praecepto venisse, infra XXXVI, Prov. XII 5 cogitationes justorum consilia; quantum ad crudelitatem operis: vacuam, spoliando eos rebus quibus debebant nutriri, contra illud Prov. XXV 21 si esurierit inimicus tuus, ciba illum. First, the wickedness of the king himself, as to the blasphemy of his mouth: the fool, namely, Sennacherib, will speak foolish things, blasphemous words, below (Isa 37:17). The mouth of fools bubbles out folly (Prov 15:2); as to the duplicity of his heart: his heart will work iniquity, promising good land to the people, to the Lord deceitfully, saying he has come by his command, below (Isa 36:10). The thoughts of the just are counsels (Prov 12:5); as to the cruelty of his work: to make empty the soul of the hungry, despoiling them of the things by which they ought to have been nourished, against what it says in Proverbs 25:21: if your enemy be hungry, give him to eat. 702. Secundo principum ejus malitiam: fraudulenti, scilicet Senacherib, vasa, id est nuntii et principes ejus, Prov. XXIX 12 princeps qui libenter audit verba mendacii, omnes ministros habet impios; et malitiae executionem: ipse enim cogitationes, scilicet Rapsaces, de quo infra XXXVI, concinnabit, id est componet,—cinnus: genus potionis ex diversis speciebus compositae—; pauper, Judaeus, Ps. os tuum abundavit malitia et lingua tua concinnabat dolos. 702. Second, the malice of his princes: the vessels, that is, the messengers and princes, of the deceitful, namely, of Sennacherib: a prince that gladly hears lying words, has all his servants wicked (Prov 29:12); and the execution of their malice: for he, namely, Rabsaces, about whom, see below (36), will frame (concinnabit), that is, compose, devices—cinnus is a kind of drink composed of different kinds of things; the poor man, the Jew: your mouth has abounded with evil, and your tongue framed deceits (Ps 49[50]:19). 703. Tertio regnantis Ezechiae prudentiam: princeps, quia praecepit quod non illi aliquis responderet, supra I 26 restituam principes tuos ut fuerunt prius. 703. Third, the prudence of Ezechias in his reign: the prince, for Ezechias commanded that no one should respond to Sennacherib, above: I will restore your princes as they were before (Isa 1:26). 704. Mulieres. Hic describit persecutionem inflictam quantum ad tria. 704. Rich women. Here he describes the persecution inflicted on them as to three things. Primo quantum ad fructuum amissionem, excitans attentionem: mulieres, quae facile potestis plorare, opulentae, quae plorandi materiam habetis ex bonorum perditione; vel mulieres, id est vos Judaei imbecilles, Ps. qui confidunt in virtute sua, et in multitudine divitiarum suarum gloriantur. Et ponit temporis determinationem: post dies aliquot, et unum annum, Ps. quoniam tribulatio proxima est, et non est qui adjuvet. Et ponit fructuum amissionem: consummata, destructa, collectio frugum non veniet in domos vestras ab agris, Jer. XLVIII 33 ablata est laetitia vindemiae. First, as to the loss of fruits, stirring up attention: you women, who cry easily, rich, who have reason to cry from the ruin of their goods; or you women, that is, you feeble Jews: they that trust in their own strength, and glory in the multitude of their riches (Ps 48:7[49:6]). And he sets out the determination of the time: after several days and one year: for tribulation is very near: for there is none to help me (Ps 21:12[22:11]). And he sets out the loss of fruits: the vintage is at an end, destroyed, the gathering, of crops, shall come no more, into our houses from the fields: the joy of the vintage is taken away (Jer 48:33). 705. Secundo quantum ad camporum vastationem, indicens planctum: obstupescite, Baruch IV 20 exui me stola pacis, supra XXII 12 et vocavit Dominus in die illa ad fletum. Et praedicit damnum: super humum, supra V 6 ascendent super eam vepres et spinae. 705. Second, as to the devastation of the fields, he prescribes mourning, be astonished: I have put off the robe of peace (Bar 4:20), above: and the Lord in that day shall call to weeping (Isa 22:12). And he foretells harm: upon the land, above: briers and thorns shall come up (Isa 5:6) over it. 706. Tertio quantum ad desolationem civitatem et domorum, ponens ipsam desolationem: quanto magis, scilicet flendum est, civitatis exultantis, scilicet Jerusalem, cujus inferior pars fuit reddita Assyriis, supra XXII 2 urbs frequens civitas exultans; et desolationis magnitudinem quantum ad domos, quia redacte in perpetuas tenebras, sicut accidit in aedificiis ruinosis: et tenebrae, Job XII 25 palpabunt in tenebris et non in luce; et quantum ad agros: gaudium onagrorum, qui morantur in magnis solitudinibus, Jer. II 24 onager assuetus in solitudine. 706. Third, as to the desolation of their cities and houses, he sets out the desolation itself: how much more, namely, to be wept for, the city that rejoiced, namely, Jerusalem, whose lower part was handed over to the Assyrians, above: a populous city, a joyous city (Isa 22:2). And the magnitude of the desolation as to the houses, for they were reduced to perpetual darkness, as happens to ruined buildings: and darkness: they shall grope in the dark, and not in the light (Job 12:25); and as to their fields: a joy of wild asses, which dwell in great wildernesses: a wild ass accustomed to the wilderness (Jer 2:24). 707. Donec effundatur. Hic prosequitur liberationem. 707. Until the spirit be poured upon us. Here he describes their liberation. Et primo ponit Judaeorum consolationem, And first, he sets out the consolation of the Jews; secundo hostium destructionem: grando autem, second, the destruction of their enemies: but hail (Isa 32:19); tertio prophetae congratulationem: beati qui seminant. third, the prophet’s congratulation: blessed are you that sow (Isa 32:20). Circa primum tria <ponit>. Concerning the first, he sets out three things. Primo divinam consolationem: haec, dico, contingent vastante terram Senacherib, donec spiritus, divina consolatio, Ez. XXXVII 6, 5 scietis quia ego Dominus, quia intromittam in vos spiritum et vivetis. First, divine consolation: these things, I say, will happen, when Sennacherib lays waste the land, until the spirit, divine consolation, be poured upon us: you shall know that I am the Lord, for I will send spirit into you, and you shall live (Ezek 37:5–6). 708. Secundo consolationis modum et ordinem, quantum ad terrae fertilitatem: et erit desertum, id est terra vestra prius deserta erit ita fertilis sicut Carmelus, cujus fertilitas reputabitur sterilitas comparatione terrae vestrae; supra XXIX 17 idem habetur. Quantum ad justitiae observationem: et habitabit, supra I 27 Sion in judicio. Et quantum ad pacis tranquillitatem: et erit opus, id est effectus; silentium, quia sine tumultu contentionum et bellorum, supra XXVI 12 Domine, dabis pacem nobis. 708. Second, the manner and order of the consolation, as to the fertility of the land: and the desert shall be as Carmel, and Carmel shall be counted for a forest; that is, your land, formerly a desert, will be so fertile like Carmel, that Carmel’s fertility will be considered barrenness in comparison to your land; above (Isa 29:19) has the same thing. As the observation of justice: and judgment shall dwell, above: Zion shall be redeemed in judgment (Isa 1:27). And as to the tranquility of peace: and the work, that is, the effect, of justice shall be peace, and the service of justice quietness, for it will be without the tumult of contentions and wars, above: Lord, you will give us peace (Isa 26:12). 709. Tertio ponit divinae consolationis jucundam fruitionem: et sedebit, Prov. I 33 qui autem me audierit, absque terrore requiescet, et abundantia perfruetur. 709. Third, he sets out the pleasant enjoyment of divine consolation: and my people shall sit in the beauty of peace: but he that shall hear me, shall rest without terror, and shall enjoy abundance (Prov 1:33). 710. Grando, scilicet tribulationis, in descensione saltus, id est descendent a Domino super hostes vestros; civitas, Ninive, supra XXV civitatem sublimem humiliabis. 710. Hail, namely, of tribulation, in the descent of the forest, that is, it shall descend from the Lord upon your enemies; the city, Ninive, above: the high city he shall lay low (Isa 26:5). 711. Beati qui seminant. Hic ostenditur prophetae congratulatio: super aquas, id est super loca fertilia ex abundantia aquarum; pedem bovis et asini, non simul quia hoc prohibitum est, Deut. XXII 10 non arabis in bove et asino, Ps. labores manuum tuarum quia manducabis, beatus es, et bene tibi erit. 711. Blessed are you that sow. Here he shows the prophet’s congratulation: upon waters, that is, upon the places that are fertile from the abundance of the waters; sending thither the foot of the ox and the ass, not at the same time, for this was prohibited: you shall not plough with an ox and an ass together (Deut 22:10); for you shall eat the labors of your hands: blessed are you, and it shall be well with you (Ps 127[128]:2). 712. Nota super illo verbo erit opus justitiae pax, quod pax sanctorum in patria erit pulchra, 712. Note on the words, the work of justice shall be peace, that the peace of the saints in heaven will be beautiful, primo quia non erit falsa, Sap. XV; first, because it will not be false (Wis 15); secundo quia non interrupta, supra X et pacis non erit finis; second, because it will not be interrupted, above: and there shall be no end of peace (Isa 9:7); tertio quia plena, Ps. posuit fines tuos pacem. third, because it will be full: he has placed peace in your borders (Ps 147:14). 713. Item nota quod pacem futuram faciunt appetibilem tria: 713. Note also on the peace to come that three things make it desirable: primo firmitas divinae potentiae, Ps. qui confidunt in Domino sicut mons Sion, non commovebitur in aeternum qui habitat in Jerusalem; first, the strength of divine power: they that trust in the Lord shall be as mount Zion: he shall not be moved for ever that dwells in Jerusalem (Ps 124[125]:1–2); secundo puritas propriae conscientiae, Prov. III 23 ambulabis fiducialiter in via tua et pes tuus non impinget; second, the purity of one’s own conscience: you shall walk confidently in your way, and your foot shall not stumble (Prov 3:23); tertio remotio hostilis nequitiae, Matth. ult. confidite, ego vici mundum. third, the removal of hostile wickedness: have confidence. I have overcome the world (John 16:33). 714. Item nota super illo verbo beati qui seminant super aquas, quod doctrina Domini est aqua: 714. Note also on the words, blessed are you that sow upon waters, that the teaching of the Lord is water: primo quia abundat, Eccli. XXIV 40 ego sapientia effudi flumina; first, because it abounds: I, wisdom, have poured out rivers (Sir 24:40); secundo quia refrigerat, Prov. XXV 25 aqua frigida animae sitienti; second, because it cools: as cold water to a thirsty soul (Prov 25:25); tertio quia fecundat, infra LV 10 quomodo descendit imber de caelo et illuc ultra non revertitur, sed inebriat terram et germinare eam facit; sic erit verbum quod egredietur de ore meo; third, because it makes fruitful, below: as the rain comes down from heaven, and returns no more thither, but soaks the earth, and makes it to spring, so shall my word be, which shall go forth from my mouth (Isa 55:10–11); quarto quia velociter portat, Jo. IV 14 fiet in eo fons aquae salientis in vitam aeternam; fourth, because it carries quickly: it shall become in him a fountain of water, springing up into life everlasting (John 4:14); quinto quia singulis se conformat, Prov. V 16 in plateis aquas tuas divide. fifth, because it conforms itself to each individually: in the streets divide your waters (Prov 5:16). Caput 33 Chapter 33
