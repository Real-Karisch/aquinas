      unum sumptum ex divina scientia quae plene omnia novit, praeterita et futura, quam idola non habent: priora quae fuerunt etc., Eccli. xxiii Domino Deo antequam crearentur etc.; one is taken from divine knowledge, which knew all things fully, past and future, and which the idols do not have: tell us the former things what they were: for all things were known to the Lord God, before they were created (Sir 23:29[20]); aliud est sumptum ex divina potentia quae potest punire et benefacere: bene quoque etc., Baruch ult. neque si quid mali patiuntur etc. the other is taken from divine power, which can punish and impart benefits: do also good or evil, if you can: and whether it be evil that one doth unto them, or good, they are not able to recompense it (Bar 6:33). Tertio concludit eorum defectum quantum ad causam materialem: ecce vos, idola, ex nihilo, ex materia creata; quantum ad formalem: opus, id est artificialis forma, quae figurat in vobis divinitatem quae non est; et quantum ad efficientem: abominatio, id est abominabilis. Vel: vos, artifices, opus vestrum, scilicet idola, qui elegit, cultores, prima Cor. viii scimus quia idolum etc., Jer. x confusus omnis artifex etc. Third, he deduces their defect as to material cause: behold, you, idols, are of nothing, created out of matter; as to formal cause, your work, that is, the artificial form, which fashioned in you a divinity, which does not exist; and as to efficient cause: an abomination, that is, abominable. Or: you, artists, your work, namely, the idols, he that has chosen you, worshippers: we know that an idol is nothing (1 Cor 8:4); every artist is confounded in his graven idol (Jer 10:14). 811. Suscitavi. Hic sumit quoddam speciale factum ad arguendum contra idola. 811. I have raised up. Here he takes up a certain particular fact to argue against idols. Et primo praedicit quoddam futurum: ab aquilone, Chaldaeum, ab oriente, Persam, qui subjugaverunt sibi diversas terras et principes, plastes, figulus,—plasma factura—; mystice: per eum qui ab aquilone, gentilem, per eum qui ab oriente, Christum, Ps. ut lutum platearum etc. And first, he foretells something to come: from the north, Chaldea; from the rising of the sun, Persia, which subjugated to itself various lands and princes, potter (plastes)—plasma, a thing produced; mystically: he who comes from the north signifies the gentile; he who comes from the rising of the sun, Christ: I shall bring them to naught, like the dirt in the streets (Ps 17:43[18:42]). Secundo ex hoc sub quaestione proponit argumentum, quod qui hoc potest praedicere est juste dicendus Deus, et qui non, non: quis annuntiavit?, infra xlix congregamini et audite etc. Second, from this, he sets out an argument under the form of a question: that he who can foretell this is justly called God; and he who cannot, is not: who has declared, below: assemble yourselves together, all you, and hear (Isa 48:14). Tertio excludit ab idolis divinitatis indicium: non est annuntians aliquis ex vobis diis, neque aliquis cultorum audiens etc., Ps. oculos habent et non videbunt etc. Third, he excludes proof of divinity from the idols: there is none, any of you gods, that shows nor, any of your worshippers, that hears, etc., they have eyes and see not; they have ears and hear not (Ps 113:13–15[115:5–6]). Quarto ostendit in vero Deo divinitatis signum: primus, id est singularis, Deus noster, ecce adsum in auxilium, evangelistas, annuntiantes futura, Dan. ii vere Deus vester Deus etc. Vel: primus, propheta Domini, ex persona Dei; vel primus, primogenitus Dei Filius, Eph. iv et dedit quosdam quidem etc. Fourth, he shows the sign of divinity in the true God: the first, that is, the only one, our God; behold I am here to help, evangelists, announcing things to come: verily, your God is the God of gods (Dan 2:47); or: the first, prophet of the Lord, in the person of God; or the first, the firstborn Son of God: he gave some apostles, and some prophets, and other some evangelists (Eph 4:11). 812. Et vidi. Hic ex supra posita disputatione concludit sententiae determinationem, et per se patet, Ier. x et lprimo vana sunt et opus risu dignum etc. 812. And I saw. Here from the disputation set out above, he deduces the determination of the sentence, and it is self-evident: they are vain things, and a ridiculous work (Jer 10:15 and 51:18). 813. Christus transivit in pace 813. Christ passed in the peace (see 41:3), carnis ad spiritum quam habuit, Iob v scies quod pacem habeat etc.; of the flesh toward the spirit, which he had: you shall know that your tabernacle is in peace (Job 5:24); hominis ad proximum quam docuit, infra lii quam pulcri super montes etc.; of man toward his neighbor, which he taught, below: how beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that brings good tidings, and that preaches peace (Isa 52:7); mundi ad Deum quam fecit, Eph. ii fecit pacem etc. of the world toward the Lord, which he made: making peace, and reconciling both to God (Eph 2:15). 814. Movet ad praedicandum 814. What moves one to preach (see 52:7), instinctus fidei, Ps. credidi propter etc., II Cor. iv, Apoc. ult.; is the instigation of faith: I have believed, therefore have I spoken (Ps 115[116]:10; 2 Cor 4; Rev 22); stimulus zeli, Ier. xx et factum in corde meo etc.; the spur of zeal: there came in my heart as a burning fire (Jer 20:9); magnitudo praemii, Mt. v qui fecerit et docuerit etc. the greatness of the reward: he that shall do and teach, he shall be called great in the kingdom of heaven (Matt 5:19). 815. Suscepit nos Christus 815. Christ upheld us (see 41:10), quasi victor captivum ad liberandum, Gen. ult. ad praedam fili etc., Ps. educ de custodia; as a conqueror upholds a captive to liberate him: to the prey, my son (Gen 49:9); bring my soul out of prison (Ps 141:8[142:7]); quasi medicus infirmum ad sanandum, Ps. qui propitiatur omnibus etc.; as a physician upholds a sick man to heal him: who forgives all your iniquities: who heals all your diseases (Ps 102[103]:3); quasi advocatus reum ad excusandum, prima Jo. primo advocatum habemus etc.; as an advocate upholds a defendant to plead to excuse him: we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ (1 John 2:1); quasi fortis debilem ad defendendum, infra lxiii ego qui loquor iustitiam etc., Exo. xiv Dominus pugnabit etc.; as a strong man upholds a weak man to defend him, below: I, that speak justice, and am a defender to save (Isa 63:1); the Lord will fight for you (Exod 14:14); quasi sponsus sponsam ad congaudendum, Osee ii sponsabo te mihi etc. as a husband upholds his wife to rejoice with her: I will espouse you to me (Hos 2:19). 816. Aqua 816. There is the water (see 41:18), effusae lacrimae, Ier. ix quis dabit capiti meo etc.; of tears poured out: who will give water to my head, and a fountain of tears to my eyes? (Isa 9:1); baptismalis munditiae, Jo. iii nisi quis renatus etc.; of baptismal purity: unless a man be born again of water and the Holy Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God (John 3:5); spiritualis gratiae, Jo. vii qui in me credit, flumina etc.; of spiritual grace: he that believes in me: out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water (John 7:38); divinae sapientiae, Eccli. xxiv ego sapientia effudi etc.; of divine wisdom: I, wisdom, have poured out rivers (Sir 24:40); internae laetitiae, Prov. v bibe aquam etc. of internal joy: drink water out of your own cistern (Prov 5:15). Caput 42 Chapter 42 42:1 Ecce servus meus, suscipiam eum; electus meus, complacuit sibi in illo anima mea; dedi spiritum meum super eum. Judicium gentibus proferet, 42:1 Behold my servant, I will uphold him: my elect, my soul delights in him: I have given my spirit upon him, he shall bring forth judgment to the Gentiles. 42:2 non clamabit; neque accipiet personam nec audietur foris vox ejus, 42:2 He shall not cry, nor have respect to person, neither shall his voice be heard abroad. 42:3 calamum quassatum non conteret et linum fumigans non extinguet. In veritate educet judicium; 42:3 The bruised reed he shall not break, and smoking flax he shall not quench, he shall bring forth judgment unto truth. 42:4 non erit tristis neque turbulentus. Donec ponat in terra judicium, et legem ejus insulæ exspectabunt. 42:4 He shall not be sad, nor troublesome, till he set judgment in the earth, and the islands shall wait for his law. 42:5 Hæc dicit Dominus Deus creans cælos et extendens eos, firmans terram et quæ germinant ex ea, dans flatum populo qui est super eam et spiritum calcantibus eam. 42:5 Thus says the Lord God that created the heavens, and stretched them out: that established the earth, and the things that spring out of it: that gives breath to the people upon it, and spirit to them that tread thereon.
