      Caput 12 Chapter 12 12:1 Et dices in illa die: confitebor tibi Domine quoniam iratus es mihi; conversus est furor tuus, et consolatus es me. 12:1 And you shall say in that day: I will confess to you, O Lord, for you were angry with me: your wrath is turned away, and you have comforted me. 12:2 Ecce Deus salvator meus: fiducialiter agam et non timebo, quia fortitudo mea et laus mea Dominus Deus, et factus est mihi in salutem. 12:2 Behold, God is my savior, I will deal confidently, and will not fear: because the Lord is my strength, and my praise, and he is become my salvation. 12:3 Haurietis aquas in gaudio de fontibus salvatoris. 12:3 You shall draw waters with joy out of the savior’s fountains: 12:4 Et dicetis in die illa: confitemini Domino et invocate nomen ejus, notas facite in populis adinventiones ejus, mementote quoniam excelsum est nomen ejus. 12:4 And you shall say in that day: confess the Lord, and call upon his name: make his works known among the people: remember that his name is high. 12:5 Cantate Domino quoniam magnifice fecit, annuntiate hoc in universa terra. 12:5 Sing to the Lord, for he has done great things: show this forth in all the earth. 12:6 Exsulta et lauda habitatio Sion, quia magnus in medio tui Sanctus Israel. 12:6 Rejoice, and praise, O you habitation of Zion: for great is he that is in the midst of you, the Holy One of Israel. 384. Confitebor. Hic ponitur gratiarum actio pro suscepto beneficio, quod quidem jam spiritu prophetali propheta videbat et populo promittebat. Unde tria facit: 384. I will confess. Here the thanksgiving for the benefit they received is set out, which indeed the prophet already saw by the prophetic spirit and promised to the people. Hence he does three things: primo decantat canticum, first, he sings a song, secundo promittit populo beneficium: haurietis, second, he promises the benefit to the people: you shall draw waters (Isa 12:3), tertio praedicit decantaturum populum: et dicetis. third, he foretells that the people shall sing: and you shall say (Isa 12:4). 385. Tribus autem malis premebantur tunc, scilicet pro praeteritis culpis divino furore, pro futuris poenis hostium timore, pro praesentibus malis cordis dolore. 385. Now, they were then oppressed by three evils, namely, by divine wrath for past faults, by fear of enemies for future punishments, by sorrow of heart for present evils. Contra primum commemorat Dei misericordiam. Et ideo dicit confitebor tibi ego, Domine, ex nunc, id est laudabo, quia populi nondum videntis beneficium de quo gratias agit, de hoc quod sequitur: quoniam primo juste iratus es mihi, pro peccatis meis, et conversus est furor tuus in misericordiam, et consolatus es me, quantum ad misericordiae effectum, praeteritum pro futuro; et hoc in reditu populi de Babylone, et maxime de consolatione facta per Christum, Zach. VIII 14 sicut cogitavi ut affligerem vos, cum ad iracundiam provocassent patres vestri me, dicit Dominus. Against the first, he recalls the mercy of God. And therefore he says, I will confess to you, I myself, O Lord, from now on, that is, I will praise you, for the people do not yet see the benefit for which he gives thanks, for what follows: for, at first justly, you were angry with me, for my sins, and your wrath is turned, into mercy, and you have comforted me, as to the effect of mercy, using the past tense for the future; and this concerns the return of the people from Babylon, and most of all, the consolation wrought through Christ: as I purposed to afflict you, when your fathers had provoked me to wrath, says the Lord, and I had no mercy: so turning again I have thought in these days to do good to the house of Judah, and Jerusalem (Zech 8:14–15). 386. Contra secundum confitetur Deo fiduciam: ecce Deus salvator meus, liberans populum de servitute Babylonis; vel verius, qui salvum faciet populum suum, Lucae I ipse enim salvum faciet populum suum a peccatis eorum, Jer. XXX 10 ne timeas, serve meus Jacob, ait Dominus, neque paveas, Israel, quia ecce ego salvabo te de terra longinqua. 386. Against the second evil, he confesses confidence in God: behold, God is my savior, freeing the people from the slavery of Babylon, or more truly, who will save his people: for he shall save his people from their sins (Matt 1:21); fear you not, my servant Jacob, says the Lord, neither be dismayed, O Israel: for behold, I will save you from a country afar off (Jer 30:10). Contra tertium, divinae fortitudinis potentiam: fortitudo mea, per quam subsisto, et laus mea, quam laudo, relictis idolis, Ps. diligam te, Domine, fortitudo mea. Against the third evil, the power of divine strength: my strength, by which I subsist, and my praise, which I praise, having abandoned idols: I will love you, O Lord, my strength (Ps 17:2[18:1]). 387. Haurietis. Hic promittit beneficium: aquas consolationis, refrigerantes contra aestum tribulationis. 387. You shall draw. Here he promises the benefit: waters, of comfort, cooling them against the heat of tribulation. 388. Et dicetis. Hic praedicit etiam populum decantaturum post receptum beneficium, et continet mutuam exhortationem populi ad divinam laudem. Ponitur autem exhortatio ad tria: 388. And you shall say. Here he foretells that the people shall also sing, after the benefit has been received, and it contains the mutual exhortation of the people to divine praise. Exhortation to three things is set out: ad laudis divinae confessionem, to the confession of divine praise, ad gratiarum actionem: cantate Domino, to thanksgiving: sing to the Lord (Isa 12:5), ad communem exultationem: exulta. to shared rejoicing: rejoice (Isa 12:6). Circa primum ponuntur quattuor: Concerning the first, four things are set out: aut enim confitemur per fidem: confitemini Domino, Ro. X 10 corde creditur ad justitiam, ore autem confessio fit ad salutem; for we either confess the Lord through faith: confess the Lord: with the heart, we believe unto justice: but, with the mouth, confession is made unto salvation (Rom 10:10); aut invocamus per orationem: invocate, infra LV 6 quaerite Dominum dum inveniri potest, invocate eum dum prope est; or we call upon him through prayer: and call upon, below: seek the Lord, while he may be found: call upon him, while he is near (Isa 55:6); aut annuntiatur per praedicationem: notas facite adinventiones ejus, quia multa adinvenit ad salvandum, Jer. XXXI 10 audite verbum Domini, gentes, et annuntiate in insulis quae procul sunt; or he is announced through preaching: make his works known, for he works many things to save them: hear the word of the Lord, O you nations, and declare it in the islands that are afar off (Jer 31:10); aut memoratur per jugem meditationem: <mementote>, Ps. mementote mirabilium ejus. or he is remembered through continual meditation, remember: remember his marvelous works (Ps 104[105]:5). 389. Cantate. Hic exhortatur ad gratiarum actionem; 389. Sing to the Lord. Here he exhorts them to thanksgiving; et primo ut gratias agant Deo: cantate Domino quoniam magnifice fecit, nos liberando, Ps. cantate ei canticum novum quia mirabilia fecit; and first, that they give thanks to God: sing to the Lord, for he has done great things, in freeing us: sing to the Lord a new canticle: because he has done wonderful things (Ps 97[98]:1); secundo ut annuntietur mundo: annuntiate, Tob. XII 6 benedicite Deum caeli, et coram omnibus viventibus confitemini ei. second, that it be announced to the world: show this forth in all the earth: bless the God of heaven, give glory to him in the sight of all that live (Tob 12:6). 390. Exulta. Hic ponitur exhortatio ad exultationem; 390. Rejoice. Here the exhortation to exultation is set out; et primo ut sit gaudium in corde: exulta; s and first, that there be joy in their hearts: rejoice; ecundo ut sit jubilus in ore: lauda; second, that there be songs of joy in their mouths, praise; tertio ponitur utriusque ratio: quia magnus, infra XXXV 2 exultabit laetabunda et laudans. third, the reason for both is set out, for great is he, below: it shall rejoice with joy and praise (Isa 35:2). 391. Notandum super illo verbo haurietis aquas, quod est aqua 391. It is to be noted on the words, you shall draw waters (Isa 12:3), that there is primo doctrinae, infra LV 1 omnes sitientes venite ad aquas; first, the water of doctrine, below: all you that thirst, come to the waters (Isa 55:1); secundo gratiae, Jo. VII 38 qui in me credit, flumina de ventre ejus fluent aquae vivae; second, of grace: he that believes in me, out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water (John 7:38);
