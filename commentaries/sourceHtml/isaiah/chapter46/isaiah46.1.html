      Caput 46 Chapter 46 46:1 Confractus est Bel, contritus est Nabo. Facta sunt simulacra eorum bestiis et jumentis, onera vestra gravi pondere usque ad lassitudinem; 46:1 Bel is broken, Nebo is destroyed: their idols are put upon beasts and cattle, your burdens of heavy weight even unto weariness. 46:2 contabuerunt et contrita sunt simul, non potuerunt salvare portantem, et anima eorum in captivitatem ibit. 46:2 They are consumed, and are broken together: they could not save him that carried them, and their souls shall go into captivity. 46:3 Audite me domus Jacob et omne residuum domus Israel, qui portamini a meo utero, qui gestamini a mea vulva. 46:3 Hearken unto me, O house of Jacob, all the remnant of the house of Israel who are carried by my bowels, are borne up by my womb. 46:4 Usque ad senectam ego ipse et usque ad canos ego portabo; ego feci et ego feram, et ego portabo et salvabo. 46:4 Even to your old age I am the same, and to your grey hairs I will carry you: I have made you, and I will bear: I will carry and will save. 46:5 Cui assimilastis me et adæquastis et comparastis me et fecistis similem? 46:5 To whom have you likened me, and made me equal, and compared me, and made me like? 46:6 Qui confertis aurum de sacculo et argentum statera ponderatis, conducentes aurificem ut faciat deum et procidunt et adorant. 46:6 You that contribute gold out of the bag, and weigh out silver in the scales: and hire a goldsmith to make a god: and they fall down and worship. 46:7 Et portant illum in humeris gestantes et ponentes in loco suo, et stabit ac de loco non movebitur. Sed et cum clamaverint ad eum non audiet; de tribulatione non salvabit eos. 46:7 They bear him on their shoulders and carry him, and set him in his place, and he shall stand, and shall not stir out of his place. Yea, when they shall cry also unto him, he shall not hear: he shall not save them from tribulation. 46:8 Mementote istud et fundamini; redite prævaricatores ad cor; 46:8 Remember this, and be ashamed: return, you transgressors, to the heart. 46:9 recordamini prioris sæculi quoniam ego sum Deus, et non est ultra me deus, nec est similis mei, 46:9 Remember the former age, for I am God, and there is no God beside, neither is there the like to me: 46:10 annuntians ab exordio novissimum et ab initio quæ necdum facta sunt; dicens: consilium meum stabit, et omnis voluntas mea fiet; 46:10 Who show from the beginning the things that shall be at last, and from ancient times the things that as yet are not done, saying: my counsel shall stand, and all my will shall be done: 46:11 vocans ab oriente avem, et de terra longinqua virum voluntatis meæ, et locutus sum et adducam illud, creavi et faciam illud. 46:11 Who call a bird from the east, and from a far country the man of my own will, and I have spoken, and will bring it to pass: I have created, and I will do it. 46:12 Audite me duro corde, qui longe estis a justitia. 46:12 Hear me, O you hardhearted, who are far from justice. 46:13 Prope feci justitiam meam, non elongabitur, et salus mea non morabitur. Dabo in Sion salutem et in Israel gloriam meam. 46:13 I have brought my justice near, it shall not be afar off: and my salvation shall not tarry. I will give salvation in Zion, and my glory in Israel. 872. Confractus est Bel etc. Hic praedicit destructionem hostium eos captivantium, scilicet Babyloniorum, quantum ad duo in quibus gloriabantur: 872. Bel is broken. Here he foretells the destruction of the enemies holding them captive, namely, the Babylonians, as to two things in which they gloried: primo quantum ad idolorum destructionem, first, as to the destruction of their idols; secundo quantum ad regni cessationem, infra xlvii: descende, sede in pulvere. second, as to the ending of their kingdom, below: come down, sit in the dust (Isa 47:1). Prima in duas. The first of these is divided into two. In prima praedicit idolorum Babylonis destructionem quantum ad confractionem statuarum: confractus est, Ez. vi demoliar aras vestras; et quantum ad asportationem metallorum, quia Persae frusta auri et argenti secum detulerunt, ponens ex parte asportantium lassitudinis incommodum: bestiis et jumentis, scilicet equis et camelis in quibus metalla gravia deferebant, Ier. x portata tollentur, vel cultoribus vestris qui sunt bestiae, imagines bestiarum adorantes, Jer. xvj servietis ibi diis alienis etc.; ponens subventionis defectum ex parte idolorum: contabuerunt, id est defecerunt, scilicet idola, portantem, scilicet bestiam, vel colentem hominem se, Sap. xiv quod factum est cum illo etc.; ponens etiam captivitatis opprobrium: et anima eorum, scilicet idolorum, metaphorice: id est numen eorum, vel gentilium, quia idola sicut animam suam amabant, Oseae x siquidem et ipse in Assur etc. In the first, he foretells the destruction of the idols of Babylon as to the breaking of their statues: Bel is broken: I will throw down your altars (Ezek 6:4); and as to the carrying away of their metal, because the Persians carried off their gold and silver with them in vain, he sets out the disadvantage of weariness on the part of those who carried them off: upon beasts and cattle, namely, horses and camels, on which they carried off heavy metals: they must be carried to be removed (Jer 10:5); or their worshippers, who are beasts, adoring the images of beasts: there you shall serve strange gods (Jer 16:13). He sets out the lack of aid on the part of the idols: they are consumed, that is, they fail, namely, the idols, him that carried them, namely, the beasts, or the men worshipping them: that which is made, together with him that made it, shall suffer torments (Wis 14:10); he sets out also the disgrace of captivity: and their souls, namely, the idols’, metaphorically: that is, their divinity; or the gentiles’, for they loved their idols like their own souls: for itself also is carried into Assyria, a present to the avenging king (Hos 10:6). 873. Secundo revocat Judaeos ad suam venerationem, relictis idolis: audite me. 873. Second, he calls the Jews back to worship of him, the idols having been abandoned: hearken unto me. Et primo revocat eos ad suum cultum, And first, he calls them back to worship of him; secundo revertentibus promittit liberationis beneficium: annuntians ab exordio etc. second, he promises the benefit of liberation to those who turn back to him: who show from the beginning (Isa 46:10). Circa primum tria. Concerning the first, he does three things. Primo ostendit eis suam dilectionem ex affectuosa supportatione: a meo utero, id est ita supportavi vos in mea misericordia affectuose, in deserto et in aliis locis in quibus contra me peccastis, sicut mater filium, Prov. xxx quid dilecte mi etc., Job xxxviii de cujus utero etc., Hebr. primo portans omnia verbo etc.; ex supportationis continuitate: usque ad senectam vestram, id est usque in finem mundi, Ps. usque ad senectam et senium etc.; ex supportationis ratione: ego feci etc., dignum est ut qui fecit conservet, Deut. xxxii expandit alas suas etc. First, he shows them his love from his affectionate carrying of them: borne up by my womb, that is, in my mercy I have carried you so affectionately, in the desert and in the other places where you sinned against me, as a mother carries her child: what, O the beloved of my womb? (Prov 31:2); out of whose womb came the ice? (Job 38:29); upholding all things by the word of his power (Heb 1:3). From the continuation of his carrying them: even to your old age, that is, to the end of the world: unto old age and grey hairs (Ps 70[71]:18); from the reason for his carrying them: I have made you, it is fitting that he who made you should preserve you: he spread his wings, and has taken him and carried him on his shoulders (Deut 32:11). Secundo ostendit idolorum vilitatem, ostendens operantium idola fatuitatem, et quantum ad intentionem, quia volunt aliquid aequale Deo facere: cui assimilastis, supra xl: idem; et quantum ad operationem, quia conducebant artifices ad faciendum deum ex auro quod sicut avari in sacculis reposuerant: qui confertis etc., Jer. x opus manus artificis etc.; ostendens etiam operum, scilicet idolorum ipsorum, imbecillitatem, quia per se nec moveri nec stare possunt: portant illum etc., Baruch ult. videbitis in Babylonia deos etc.; ostendens etiam eorum inutilitatem: sed et cum clamaverint etc., Sap. xiii pro vita quidem mortuum deprecatur etc. Second, he shows the worthlessness of the idols, showing the foolishness of those who serve idols, both as to intention, for they wish to make something equal to God: to whom have you likened me, above (Isa 40:25) has the same thing. And as to their service, for they employed craftsmen to make gods from gold, because they are like misers who store up gold for ages: you that contribute gold: the works of the hand of the workman (Jer 10:3). He also shows the worthlessness of the works, namely, the idols themselves, for they can neither be moved or stand by themselves: they bear him: you shall see in Babylon gods of gold, and of silver, and of stone, and of wood borne upon shoulders (Bar 6:3); and he shows their uselessness: yea, when they shall cry also unto him, he shall not hear: for life he prays to that which is dead, and for help calls upon that which is unprofitable (Wis 13:18). Tertio concludit revocationem: mementote istud praedictum de dilectione mea et vilitate idolorum; prioris saeculi, in quo multa vobis beneficia contuli, Ps. cogitavi dies antiquos etc. Third, he concludes the calling back: remember this, what I have said of my love and the worthlessness of idols. The former age, in which I gave you many benefits: I thought upon the days of old (Ps 76:6[77:5]). 874. Annuntians ab exordio etc. Hic promittit conversis ad ipsum liberationem a captivitate. Et circa hoc duo facit. 874. Who show from the beginning. Here he promises liberation from captivity to those who are converted to him. And concerning this, he does two things. Primo promittit liberationem, ponens praenuntiandi potestatem: annuntians novissimum, quodcumque futurum in novissimis temporibus, Eccli. xlii annuntians quae praeterierunt etc.; ponit etiam praenuntiationis firmitatem: dicens: stabit, implebitur, voluntas beneplaciti, Eccli. xxprimo consilium ejus sicut fons vitae etc., Ps. omnia quaecumque voluit etc.; et ponit liberationis ordinem: vocans ab oriente, avem, Cyrum, propter velocitatem victoriae, vel Christum, supra xlprimo quis suscitavit ab oriente etc. First, he promises liberation, setting out the power of him who announces it: who show the things that shall be at last, whatever is to come in the last times: he declares the things that are past, and the things that are to come (Sir 42:19); he also sets out the firmness of the announcement: saying: my counsel shall stand, it shall be fulfilled; my will, of good pleasure, his counsel continues like a fountain of life (Sir 21:16); he has done all things whatsoever he would (Ps 113:11[115:3]). And he sets out the order of liberation: who call a bird from the east, Cyrus, called a bird because of the swiftness of his victory; or Christ, above: who has raised up the just one from the east? (Isa 41:2). 875. Secundo, ibi audite me, promittit liberationis propinquitatem, 875. Second, where it says, hear me, he promises the nearness of liberation, ponens primo eorum indignitatem, ne suis meritis ascribant: duri corde, ad bene operandum, longe, per multitudinem peccatorum, Eccli. iii cor durum male etc.; setting out first their unworthiness, lest it be ascribed to their merit: O you hardhearted, for acting rightly, who are far from justice, through the multitude of their sins: a hard heart shall fear evil at the last (Sir 3:27[26]); secundo praedicit propinquitatem: prope feci, faciam, praeteritum pro futuro, justitiam de Babyloniis pro vobis per Cyrum, vel justitiam Christi, infra lvj juxta est salus mea etc.; second, he foretells its nearness: I have brought near, I will bring, using the past tense for the future, my justice, concerning Babylon for you through Cyrus, or the justice of Christ, below: my salvation is near to come, and my justice to be revealed (Isa 56:1); tertio ponit loci determinationem: dabo in Sion etc., gloriam, beneficia unde gloriosus apparebo, vel Christum qui est gloria Patris, Aggaei secundo magna erit gloria hujus domus etc. third, he sets out the determination of the place: I will give salvation in Zion, and my glory, my benefits, from which I will appear glorious, or Christ, who is the glory of the Father: great shall be the glory of this last house (Hag 2:10). 876. Peccatum fatigat 876. Sin makes us weary (see 46:1), propter sollicitudinem in excogitando, Prov. iv non enim dormiunt etc.; because of the anxiety in devising it: for they sleep not, except they have done evil (Prov 4:16); propter laborem in exequendo, Sap. lassati sumus in via etc.; because of the labor in carrying it out: we wearied ourselves in the way of iniquity (Wis 5:7);
