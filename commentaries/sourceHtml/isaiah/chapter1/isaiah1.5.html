Audite verbum Domini. Hic ponitur medicina contra poenam. 42. Hear the word of the Lord. Here he gives the medicine for the punishment. Et primo convocat ad audiendum; And first, he calls them together to hear; secundo removet inutile remedium, ibi quo mihi?; second, he removes the useless remedy, where it says, to what purpose?; tertio adhibetur salubre consilium, ibi lavamini, mundi estote. third, salutary counsel is applied, where it says, wash yourselves, be clean (Isa 1:16). Convocat autem ad audiendum primo majores, dicens audite verbum Domini, principes; However, he first calls together the great, saying, hear the word of the Lord, you rulers of Sodom; secundo minores, ibi populus Gomorrhae, Matth. X 15 amen dico vobis etc. second, the lowly, where it says, people of Gomorrah. Amen I say to you, it shall be more tolerable for the land of Sodom and Gomorrah in the day of judgment, than for that city (Matt 10:15). Sed quare invitat majores ad audiendum, minores ad percipiendum? Quia ea quae sunt a majoribus audita, facile percipiunt minores. But why does he invite those of high degree to hear and those of low degree to receive? Because things which are heard by those of high degree are easily received by those of low degree. Item cum relinquat eis semen, quare comparat eos illis? Et dicendum quod quantum ad incorrigibiles comparat propter duo: Likewise, when he leaves seed to them, why does he compare them to Sodom and Gomorrah? And to this is to be said that as to the incorrigible he makes this comparison for two reasons: propter peccati similitudinem, infra II 6 et pueris alienis adhaeserunt, first, because of the similarity of their sin, below: and they have adhered to strange children (Isa 2:6); et propter publicationem, infra III 9 peccatum suum quasi Sodoma praedicaverunt nec absconderunt. and because of its publication, below: they have proclaimed abroad their sin as Sodom, and they have not hid it (Isa 3:9). Et cum fuerint quinque, praecipue comparat istis quia sunt praecipuae inter alias. Et propter hoc etiam principes comparat Sodomis quia civitas illa metropolis erat, sicut patet Gen. XIV; principum autem est audire a Domino verbum suum et populo legem imponere, et ideo invitat illos ad audientiam verbi et populum ad perceptionem legis. And while there were five cities, nonetheless he chiefly compares them to Sodom and Gomorrah, because they are chief among the others. And because of this he also compares their princes to the inhabitants of Sodom, because that city was a metropolis, as is evident from Genesis 14. It belongs to princes, however, to hear from the Lord his word and impose the law on the people; and therefore he invites them to hear the word and the people to receive the law. 43. Quo mihi? Hic ostendit inutilitatem remedii quod apponebant. 43. To what purpose? Here he shows the uselessness of the remedy which they were applying; Et primo quantum ad sacrificiorum oblationes; and first, as to the offering of sacrifices; secundo quantum ad festorum celebrationes, ibi neomenias vestras; second, as to the celebration of feasts, where it says, the new moons (Isa 1:13); tertio quantum ad divinas orationes, ibi et cum extenderitis manus. third, as to divine prayers, where it says, and when you stretch forth your hands (Isa 1:15). Circa primum duo facit: Concerning the first, he does two things: primo reprobat oblationem de animatis; first, he rejects the sacrifice of living things; secundo de inanimatis, ibi incensum abominatio. second, the sacrifice of inanimate things, where it says, incense is an abomination to me (Isa 1:13). In sacrificiis autem animalium erat aliquid quod totum cedebat in cultum Dei, sicut holocaustum, quasi totum incensum—ab olon quod est totum et cauma quod est incendium—, Levi. I; adeps qui totus adolebatur, Levi. III 3; et sanguis, qui totus effundebatur, Levi. XVII 6. In the sacrifices of animals, however, there was one which was allotted whole to the worship of God, as the holocaust, i.e., “all burnt”—from olon, which means “all,” and cauma, which means “fire” (Lev 1); the fat which was all offered in sacrifice (Lev 3:3); and the blood which was all poured out (Lev 17:6). Quaedam autem erant quae partim cedebant in cultum Dei, partim in usum ministri, sicut hostia pro peccato, nisi quando erat pro peccato sacerdotis vel multitudinis, Levi. IV 3; There were certain sacrifices, however, which were allotted partly to the worship of God, partly to the use of the ministers, as the sin offering, except when it was for the sin of a priest or for the multitude (Lev 4:3). quaedam erant de quibus cedebat aliquid in cultum Dei et aliquid in usum ministri et aliquid cedebat ipsi offerenti, sicut in hostiis pacificis quae pro gratiarum actione offerebantur vel pro salute, Levi. IV. There were also certain other sacrifices from which something was offered to the worship of God, something to the use of the ministers, and something to one who offered, as in the peace offerings, which were offered for thanksgiving or for well-being (Lev 4).
