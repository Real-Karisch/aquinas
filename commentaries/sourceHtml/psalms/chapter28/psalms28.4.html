      Num. 11, dicitur quod propter murmur populi ignis invasit partem castrorum, et ad levationem manus Moysi ignis extinctus est: et hoc est quod dicit, vox, id est imperium, domini intercidentis, id est destruentis, flammam ignis. It is said that because of the murmuring of the people, fire entered part of the camp, and at the raising of Moses’s hands the fire was extinguished (Num 11:1). And this is what he says, the voice, that is, the authority, of the Lord divides, that is, destroys, the flames of fire. Consequenter commemorat beneficia quae promovent in bono. Afterwards, he remembers the favors which move towards the good. Et ponit tria. He describes three. Primo beneficia multiplicationis populi. The first favor is the multiplication of the people. Secundo nutritionis parvulorum. Second is the nourishment of the little ones. Tertio ducatus per desertum. Third is being led through the desert. Littera Hieronymi habet, vox domini parturientis desertum. Sic legitur in Josue. Nullus eorum qui exierunt de Aegypto, et venerunt in desertum, intraverunt terram promissionis, nisi Josue et Caleb, ut patet Num. 14. Sed omnes qui intraverunt in eam, nati sunt in deserto. Et ideo commemorat hoc beneficium quod populus non est extinctus; unde dicit, vox domini parturientis, id est parturire facientis, et specialiter desertum Cades: quia quantum ad intentionem Moysi fuit, quod statim exeuntes de Aegypto intrarent terram promissionis, et venirent in Cades. Unde miserunt exploratores, et illi dissuaserunt et timuerunt intrare: et propter illud peccatum omnes mortui sunt. Et quia ex illo dimiserunt intrare, ideo in hoc deserto omnes mortui sunt. Jerome’s text has, the voice of the Lord making the desert fruitful. So it is read in Joshua that none of those who went out from Egypt entered into the promised land except Joshua and Caleb, as is clear in Numbers 14. But all those who entered into it were born in the desert. And so he remembers the favor that the people did not die out, so he says, the voice of the Lord making fruitful, that is, making the desert to bring forth, and in particular the desert of Kadesh, since it was the intention of Moses that they would enter into the promised land immediately after leaving Egypt, and they went to Kadesh. So they sent out scouts and they dissuaded them and feared to enter, and for this sin they all died. And because they were turned aside by this from entering, they all died in the desert. Vel secundum litteram nostram, vox domini concutientis desertum; id est commoventis ad generationem per desertum Cades. Or, according to our text, the voice of the Lord shakes the desert, that is, it moves them to generation in the desert of Kadesh. Vox domini praeparantis cervos. Hieronymus habet, obstetricantis cervos. Hebraeus habet praeparantis mulos. The voice of the Lord prepares the stags. Jerome has, assists the birth of the stags. The Hebrew has, prepares the mules. Cervi in desertis manent. Et quia filii Israel quadraginta annis fuerunt in deserto, ideo dicuntur cervi: Ps. 17: statuit pedes meos tamquam cervorum, quia mulieres habuerunt opportunitatem pariendi et enutriendi. Stags live in desert places. And since the children of Israel were in the desert for forty years, they are called stags. Who has made my feet like the feet of stags (Ps 17:34), since the women were able to give birth and nourish. Et revelabit condensa, vel saltus quia ducatum ei praestitit per columnam ignis per noctem et nubis per diem: Ps. 135: transduxit populum suum per desertum. And he will discover the thick woods, or the narrow passage, because he went before them in the column of fire in the night and of cloud during the day. He led his people through the desert (Ps 135:16). Mystice, in hoc quod dicit, vox domini confringentis, signat donum fortitudinis. Mystically, what is said, the voice of the Lord breaks the cedars, signifies the gift of fortitude. Et ponit hic exclusionem peccati concupiscentiae, cum dicit, vox domini intercidentis flammam ignis, scilicet concupiscentiae: Sap. 6: concupiscentia sapientiae deducet ad ignem perpetuum. Et haec nutrit alia mala de qua 1 Joan. 2: omne quod est in mundo aut est concupiscentia oculorum et cetera. Et haec consumit: Job 31: ignis est usque ad consumptionem devorans. Basilius exponit sic. And he puts it here to exclude the sin of concupiscence, since he says, the voice of the Lord divides the flame of fire, namely, of concupiscence. The desire of wisdom brings to the everlasting fire (Wis 6:21). And this nourishes every evil about which is said, all that is in the world is the concupiscence of the eyes (1 John 2:16). And this consumes. It is a fire that devours even to destruction (Job 31:12). Basil explains it in this way. Vox domini intercidentis, et cetera quia in die judicii virtute divina ignis dividetur, quia ardor sine luce erit in igne involvente reprobos. Ignis splendens sine ardore erit ad gloriam electorum. The voice of the Lord divides, since in the day of judgment, the fire will be divided by divine power because there will be heat without light in the fire enveloping the reprobate. Fire will shine without heat in the glory of the elect. Et hoc quod dicit, pertinet ad donum consilii. And what is said pertains to the gift of counsel. Vox domini concutientis desertum. Hic ponit exclusionem duplicis infidelitatis: scilicet gentilium et Judaeorum. The voice of the Lord shakes the desert. He puts this here to exclude duplicity or unfaithfulness, namely, that of the gentiles and of the Jews. Quantum ad primum dicit, vox domini concutientis desertum, id est gentes ad fidem commovit convertendo ad eam: Isa. 54: multi filii desertae (id est populi gentilis) magis quam ejus quae habet virum, (id est synagogae). In regard to the gentiles, he says, the voice of the Lord shakes the desert, that is, he moves the nations to be converted to faith in him. Many are the children of the desolate, that is, of the gentile people, more than of her who has a husband (Isa 54:1), that is, the synagogue. Quantum ad secundum dicit, desertum cades, quod interpretatur sanctum legis: quia Judaei sanctificati per legislatorem convertentur in fine mundi: Isai. 1: terra nostra deserta et cetera. In regard to the Jews, he says, the desert of Kadesh, which is interpreted as the holiness of the law, since the Jews, sanctified by the lawgiver, will be converted at the end of the world. Our land is desolate (Isa 1:7). Et hoc pertinet ad donum intellectus. And this belongs to the gift of understanding. Consequenter commemorat beneficium quod pertinet ad promotionem in bonis. Afterwards, he remembers the favor which belongs to advancement in goods. Et primo ponit collationem sapientiae. First, he describes the gathering of wisdom; Secundo ejus actum. and second, its act. Dicit ergo, vox domini praeparantis cervos, id est sanctos et perfectos viros: quia sicut cervi repellunt venenosa, ita sancti repellunt omne peccatum: et sicut cervi per spinas incedunt sine laesione, ita sancti per vanitatem mundi sine delectatione. Gen. penul.: Nephtalim cervus emissus, dans eloquia pulchritudinis. Therefore, he says, the voice of the Lord prepares the stags, that is, the holy and perfect men, since as stags repel venomous snakes, the saints repel every sin, and as the deer move through the thorns without being torn, so the saints move through the vanity of the world without delight. Naphtali, a hart let loose, giving words of beauty (Gen 49:21). Et isti praeparantur per Deum, non per se: et ipse Deus, revelabit, eis, condensa, id est ea quae aliis sunt abscondita: Matth. 11: revelasti ea parvulis. And these are prepared by God and not by themselves, and God himself will reveal to them the thick woods, that is, those things which are hidden. You have revealed them to little one (Matt 11:25). Et hoc pertinet ad donum sapientiae. And this belongs to the gift of wisdom. Consequenter concludit gratiarum actionem pro praedictis beneficiis, dicens, in templo ejus omnes dicent gloriam: Ps. 148: juvenes et virgines, senes cum junioribus laudent nomen domini. Afterwards, he concludes by giving thanks for the aforementioned favors, saying, in his temple all shall speak his glory. Young men and maidens, let the old with the younger praise the name of the Lord (Ps 148:12). Alia littera habet, omnis dicet vel loquetur gloriam. Verum est quod quilibet habet dona communia, et etiam specialia. Et pro hoc speciali dono quilibet dicet gloriam: Eccl. ult.: danti mihi justitiam, dabo gloriam. Another text has, everyone shall say or speak of his glory. It is true that some have the common gifts, and some the special. And through this special gift anyone may speak of his glory. To him who gives me wisdom will I give glory (Sir 51:23). 263. Dominus. Hic agit de beneficiis speratis. 263. The Lord makes the flood to dwell. Here he gives thanks for the favors for which he hopes. Et primo commemorat potentiam benefactoris. First, he remembers the power of the benefactor; Secundo ponit sperata beneficia, ibi, dominus virtutem populo suo dabit. second, he describes the hoped-for favors, at the Lord will give strength to his people. In Hebraeo habetur, dominus diluvio sedit, et hoc habet planum sensum; quasi dicat: verum est quia hoc fecit populo Israeli. Numquid non ejusdem potestatis aliquando fuit? Immo a principio mundi judicia ejus manifesta fuerunt. Et commemorat unum manifestum, quod ex judicio pro peccato hominum induxit diluvium. In Hebrew it has, the Lord sits on the flood, and this has a clear sense, as if to say: “It is true that he made the people of Israel. Has there ever been such power at another time? Indeed, his judgments have been made clear from the beginning!” And he remembers one manifestation, because he brought the flood as a judgment for the sin of mankind. Et sedebit dominus rex in aeternum, judicans scilicet populos in aequitate. And the Lord shall sit king forever, judging the people in equity. Hieronymus habet, dominus diluvium inhabitat, vel inhabitare facit. Diluvio inundante, terra evacuata est ab habitatoribus. Postea iterum, inhabitare facit terram, dissipatam tunc per multiplicationem hominum. Jerome has, the Lord dwells in the flood, or makes it to dwell. When the flood poured in, the earth was emptied of inhabitants. Likewise, he made the earth to be inhabited afterwards, through the dispersal from the multiplication of men. Mystice potest tripliciter legi. Mystically, it can be read in three ways. Uno modo, ut ly diluvium sit quasi accusativus appositus ad hoc infinitum, inhabitare: quia in arca Noe soli illi habitaverunt diluvium: et sic per arcam Noe signatur Ecclesia, et sancti qui sunt in ea, securi habitant diluvium tribulationum. In one way, flood is an accusative with the infinitive to dwell, since they alone dwelt in the flood in the ark of Noah, and so, by the ark of Noah, the Church and the saints within her, dwelling securely in the tribulation of the flood, were signified. Alio modo e converso, quasi, diluvium inhabitat in templo suo. Diluvium est mundum, et carnales homines mundi: Nah. 1: in diluvio praetereunte consummationem faciet. Hoc ergo diluvio faciet inhabitare in templo suo, quando convertentur, et sedebit rex in aeternum, ut supra expositum est. In another way, on the contrary, the flood inhabits his temple. For the flood is the world, the carnal men are of the world. With a flood that passes by, he will make an utter end (Nah 1:8). Therefore, he will make the flood dwell in his temple when they are converted, and the Lord shall sit king forever, as has been explained above.
