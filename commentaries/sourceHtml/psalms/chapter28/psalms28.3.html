      Primo ponit beneficium. First, he puts forward the favor; Secundo facilitatem conferendi beneficium, ibi, et comminuet. second, the ease of conferring the favor, at and shall reduce them. Dicit ergo: vox domini confringentis cedros. Cedri sunt homines magni: et signat Amorrhaeos qui erant magni et fortes: Amos 2: exterminavi ante faciem ejus Amorrhaeum, cujus altitudo cedrorum altitudo illius, et fortis ipse quasi quercus. Therefore, he says, the voice of the Lord breaks the cedars. The cedars are great men, and it signifies the Amorites who were great and strong. Yet I cast out the Amorite before their face, whose height was like the cedars and who was as strong as an oak (Amos 2:9). Item in tota terra quae promissa erat eis, erant Amorrhaei et aliae gentes, quae non poterant exterminari et subjugari usque ad tempus David. Omnes Amorrhaei adhuc habitabant circa Libanum, ut dicitur Josue 15. Et ideo dicit, confringet dominus cedros Libani, id est Amorrhaeos, qui adhuc habitabant ibi, et comminuet eos tamquam vitulum Libani, et Saron tamquam filium unicornium. Likewise, throughout the whole land which was promised to them, there were Amorites and other peoples who could not be exterminated and subjugated even up to the time of David. All the Amorites then lived around Lebanon, as Joshua 15 says. And so he says, the Lord shall break the cedars of Lebanon, that is, the Amorites who then lived there, and shall reduce them to pieces as a calf of Lebanon, and Sharon as the son of unicorns. Hebraei habent sic, et comminuet eas tamquam vitulum Libani et Saron sicut filium bubalorum. Et est sensus litteralis: quia differentia est inter bubalos et boves; quia bubali in paludibus nutriuntur, boves autem in montibus. Erant enim in monte Libani multa pascua in quo crescebant magni cedri. Item erant ibi vituli, et boves. Dicit ergo, ita erit facile Deo comminueret cedros Libani, sicut si comminueret vitulum Saron. Saron est locus quidam: Isa. 35: decor Carmeli et Saron. Hic locus est aquosus, ubi bubali pascuntur; quasi dicat, etiam Saron comminuet sicut filium bubali, vel vitulum. The Hebrew has, and shall tear them to pieces as a calf of Lebanon and Sharon as the son of buffalo. And the literal sense is that there is a difference between buffalo and cattle, since buffalo are nourished in marshes but cattle in the mountains. For there are many pastures on the mountain of Lebanon in which great cedars grow. There were also calves there and cows. Therefore, he says that it is as easy for the Lord to break the cedars of Lebanon as to break the calf of Sharon. Sharon is a certain place: the beauty of Carmel and Sharon (Isa 35:2). This place is wet, where buffalo are pastured, as if to say, “For he shall break Sharon as the son of buffalo or a calf.” Mystice vox domini super aquas. Duplex beneficium innuit, scilicet conversionis et donorum quae dantur conversis. Mystically, the voice of the Lord is upon the waters implies two favors: namely, conversion and the gifts which are given to the one converted. Vox domini in virtute. Secundum mysterium potest dupliciter exponi. The voice of the Lord is in power. This can be explained in two ways according to the mystical sense. Uno modo, ut referatur ad praedicationem Christi; et sic agitur de conversione Judaeorum et gentium: In one way, it refers to the preaching of Christ, and so the conversion of the Jews and the gentiles is treated. Judaeorum, cum dicit, super aquas. Homines assimilantur aquis: quia sicut aquae homines fluunt, et non revertuntur, 2 Reg. 14. Unde super aquas dicitur vox, id est praedicatio domini, id est super populum Judaeorum, quia ex doctrina Dei nondum incarnati, sed expectati, Judaei conversi sunt ad Deum. The Jews are indicated when he says, upon the waters. Men are like water, since men flow like water and return no more (2 Sam 14:14). So the voice, that is, the preaching of the Lord, is said to be upon the waters, that is, upon the people of the Jews, because the Jews were converted to God by the teaching of God not yet incarnate but expected. De conversione gentium sequitur cum dicit, Deus majestatis intonuit. Tonitruum formatur in nube, in qua signatur ipsa incarnatio quae est sicut nubes; Isa. 19: ecce dominus ascendit super nubem levem. Deus ergo majestatis intonuit, id est omnipotens intonuit per praedicationem de nube carnis suae: Job 37: tonabit voce sua mirabiliter. Et dicit, super aquas multas; quia vox domini incarnati fuit non tantum super Judaeos, sed super gentiles: Isa. 49: dedi te in lucem gentium et cetera. The conversion of the gentiles follows when he says, the God of majesty has thundered. Thunder is formed in a cloud, in which the Incarnation, which is like a cloud, is signified. Behold, the Lord will ascend upon a swift cloud (Isa 19:1). Therefore, the God of majesty has thundered, that is, the Omnipotent has thundered by preaching from the cloud of his flesh. He shall thunder wonderfully with his voice (Job 37:5). And he says, upon many waters because the voice of the incarnate Lord was not only upon the Jews but upon the gentiles. I have given you to be a light to the gentiles (Isa 49:6). Vel, super aquas, Baptismi: unde ex tempore quo Christus est baptizatus, iste psalmus cantatur, Or it is upon the waters of baptism, so from the time when Christ was baptized, this song was sung. vox domini in virtute. Supra psalmista secundum mysticum intellectum tetigit mysterium conversionis Judaeorum et gentium; hic autem ponit beneficium temporalium donorum: The voice of the Lord is in power. Above, the psalmist touched upon the mystery of the conversion of the Jews and gentiles in the mystical understanding; now here he describes the favors that are temporal gifts. et circa hoc tria facit. He makes three points about this. Primo proponit spiritualia dona. First, he describes the spiritual gifts. Secundum ponit vitiorum extirpationem quae sequitur ex eis, ibi, vox domini confringentis. Second, he describes the rooting out of the vices, which follows from them, at the voice of the Lord breaks the cedars. Tertio profectionem seu promotionem ad bona ibi, vox domini praeparantis. Third, he describes the accomplishment or progress to goods, at the voice of the Lord prepares the stags. In spiritualibus donis haec est differentia, quia quibus dantur spiritualia dona, quibusdam dantur ad ea quae conveniunt communiter, et agenda sunt, et sunt de necessitate salutis: quibusdam ad quaedam ardua, sicut ad miracula facienda, et hujusmodi. A difference among spiritual goods is that, of those to whom spiritual gifts are given, they are given to some in order to accomplish what is generally fitting and must be done, and are necessary for salvation. To others they are given for certain difficult tasks, such as working miracles and such things. Quantum ad primum dicit, vox domini in virtute; id est ex imperio domini datur virtus ad implendum praecepta. In regard to the necessary works, he says, the voice of the Lord is in power, that is, the power to fulfill his precepts is given from the authority of the Lord. Quantum ad secundum dicit, vox domini in magnificentia, id est ex imperio domini datur virtus sanctis suis magna faciendi: Ps. 67: super Israel magnificentia ejus. In regard to the more difficult, he says, the voice of the Lord in magnificence, that is, power is given from the authority of the Lord for his saints to do great things. Above Israel is his magnificence (Ps 67:35). Glossa haec adaptat donis spiritus sancti. The gloss applies this to the gifts of the Holy Spirit. Et primum, scilicet conversionem fidelium ad donum timoris qui fit divina virtute, ad quam pertinet timores admittere. First, the conversion of the faithful is attributed to the gift of fear, which is a divine power that urges on the fearful. Magnificentia pertinet ad donum scientiae, quia ad scientiam pertinet magna agere: Eccl. 25: quam magnus est qui invenit sapientiam. Magnificence belongs to the gift of knowledge, to which belongs the knowledge to do great things. How great is he who finds wisdom (Sir 25:13). Vox domini confringentis. Hic agit de exclusione vitiorum, The voice of the Lord breaks the cedars. Here he treats the exclusion of the vices; et tangit primo vitium superbiae. and he first touches on the vice of pride; Secundo concupiscentiae, ibi, vox domini intercidentis flammam ignis. second, that of concupiscence, at the voice of the Lord divides the flame of fire; Tertio vitium infidelitatis sive contemptus, ibi, vox domini concutientis desertum. third, the vice of infidelity or contempt, at the voice of the Lord shakes the desert. Ostendit ergo primo exclusionem superbiae. Unde notandum est quod sicut abietes sunt magnae, ita et cedri; et ideo per eas signatur superbia. Dicit ergo, vox domini confringentis cedros, id est virtus imperii divini super omnes superbos: Isa. 14: detracta est ad Inferos superbia tua. Et comminuet cedros Libani, id est vox domini super arrogantes et superbos ipsos comminuendo sua potentia: quia omnes reges per hoc conversi sunt ad Christum; et finaliter super cedros Libani, quia majores Judaeorum conversi sunt, sicut de Nicodemo, Joan. 3. Therefore, he first shows the exclusion of pride. So it should be noted that, as fir trees are great, so are cedars, and pride is signified by them. Therefore, he says, the voice of the Lord breaks the cedars, that is, the power of the divine authority is over all the proud. Their pride is brought down to hell (Isa 14:11). Yea, the Lord shall break the cedars of Lebanon, that is, the voice of the Lord is over the arrogant and proud by reducing their power, since through this all kings were converted to Christ. Finally, it is over the cedars of Lebanon, since the majority of the Jews were converted, such as Nicodemus (John 3). 261. Item, et comminuet eos tamquam vitulum. Hic ponitur perfectio conversionis. 261. Likewise: and shall reduce them to pieces as a calf of Lebanon. Here the completion of their conversion is described. Mons Libani est valde pascuosus, et sacerdotes propter copiam victimae faciebant pascere ibi boves: et ideo, comminuet eos tamquam vitulum Libani, id est humiliabit eos sicut vitulum Libani, ut scilicet se ipsos in sacrificium offerant, sicut offertur vitulus qui nutritur in monte Libani. Et hoc patet, quia multi magni se martyrio exposuerunt pro Christo: Matth. 22: tauri mei et altilia occisa sunt. Mount Lebanon is excellent for pasture, and the priests would pasture cows there because of the abundance of the victims. And so, he shall reduce them to pieces as a calf of Lebanon, that is, he will humiliate them like a calf of Lebanon so that they offer themselves in sacrifice like a calf which was nourished on Mount Lebanon. And this is clear because many martyrs exposed themselves for the sake of Christ. My calves and fatlings are killed (Matt 22:4). Et dilectus quemadmodum filius unicornium. Hoc potest dupliciter legi. And as the beloved son of unicorns. This can be read in two ways. Primo, ut significet auctoritatem comminutionis; quasi dicat; dilectus faciet ea. Et secundum hoc dilectus ponitur singulariter, quasi auctoritatem habens. Hic est filius meus et cetera. Matth. 3. Et haec faciet, quemadmodum filius unicornium. Id est filius Judaeorum, quia mysterium incarnationis haec operabatur; et Judaei unicornes dicuntur, in quantum gloriantur in cultu unius Dei. The first is so that it signifies the authority to reduce, as if to say, “He will make the beloved like this.” And according to this, beloved is put in the singular to mean the one having authority. This is my beloved son (Matt 3:17). And he will do this as the son of unicorns. That is, the son of the Jews, since the mystery of the Incarnation does this, and the Jews are called unicorns insofar as they gloried in the worship of one God. Vel unicornis dicitur, quia secundum generationem aeternam est sine matre, et secundum temporalem generationem fuit filius sine patre. Or he is said to be a unicorn because the son is without a mother in his eternal generation and without a father in his temporal generation. Aliter, dilectus et cetera id est Christus in exemplum hujus comminutionis patietur, dabit exemplum aliis patiendi: 1 Petr. 2: Christus passus est pro nobis, vobis relinquens exemplum, ut sequamini vestigia ejus. Et patietur non in quantum Deus, sed in quantum filius unicornium Judaeorum. In another way, the beloved, that is, Christ, who, as an example of one suffering this reduction, gave an example of suffering to others. Christ suffered for us, leaving you an example that you should follow his steps (1 Pet 2:21). And he suffered this not insofar as he is God, but insofar as he is the son of the unicorns, the Jews. Revertamur ergo ad sensum litteralem. Therefore, now let us return to the literal sense. 262. Vox domini intercidentis flammam. Hic commemorat beneficium, quo liberati sunt a malo inflicto divinitus. 262. The voice of the Lord divides the flame of fire. Here he remembers the favors by which they were delivered from the evil inflicted by God. Num. 11, dicitur quod propter murmur populi ignis invasit partem castrorum, et ad levationem manus Moysi ignis extinctus est: et hoc est quod dicit, vox, id est imperium, domini intercidentis, id est destruentis, flammam ignis. It is said that because of the murmuring of the people, fire entered part of the camp, and at the raising of Moses’s hands the fire was extinguished (Num 11:1). And this is what he says, the voice, that is, the authority, of the Lord divides, that is, destroys, the flames of fire.
