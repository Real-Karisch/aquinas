      Super Psalmo 28 Psalm 28 Afferte Domino, filii Dei Bring to the Lord, O you children of God 28:1a Psalmus David, in consummatione tabernaculi. Afferte Domino, filii Dei, 28:1a A psalm for David, at the finishing of the tabernacle. Bring to the Lord, O you children of God, 28:1b afferte Domino filios arietum. 28:1b bring to the Lord the offspring of rams. 28:2 Afferte Domino gloriam et honorem; afferte Domino gloriam nomini ejus; adorate Dominum in atrio sancto ejus. 28:2 Bring to the Lord glory and honor; bring to the Lord glory to his name; adore the Lord in his holy court. 28:3 Vox Domini super aquas; Deus majestatis intonuit: Dominus super aquas multas. 28:4 Vox Domini in virtute; vox Domini in magnificentia. 28:3 The voice of the Lord is upon the waters; the God of majesty has thundered; the Lord is upon many waters. 28:4 The voice of the Lord is in power; the voice of the Lord in magnificence. 28:5 Vox Domini confringentis cedros, et confringet Dominus cedros Libani: 28:5 The voice of the Lord breaks the cedars: yea, the Lord shall break the cedars of Lebanon. 28:6 et comminuet eas, tamquam vitulum Libani, et dilectus quemadmodum filius unicornium. 28:6 And shall reduce them to pieces, as a calf of Lebanon, and as the beloved son of unicorns. 28:7 Vox Domini intercidentis flammam ignis; 28:8 vox Domini concutientis desertum: et commovebit Dominus desertum Cades. 28:9 Vox Domini praeparantis cervos: et revelabit condensa, et in templo ejus omnes dicent gloriam. 28:7 The voice of the Lord divides the flame of fire; 28:8 the voice of the Lord shakes the desert, and the Lord shall shake the desert of Kadesh. 28:9 The voice of the Lord prepares the stags, and he will discover the thick woods, and in his temple all shall speak his glory. 28:10 Dominus diluvium inhabitare facit, et sedebit Dominus rex in aeternum. Dominus virtutem populo suo dabit; Dominus benedicet populo suo in pace. 28:10 The Lord makes the flood to dwell, and the Lord shall sit king forever. The Lord will give strength to his people; the Lord will bless his people with peace. 256. In aliis psalmis commemoravit psalmista fiduciam quam habet de Deo; hic autem quasi liberatus gratias agit. 256. In the other psalms, the psalmist recalled the trust that he had in God; now here he gives thanks as one who has been delivered. Et primo ponuntur psalmi pertinentes ad gratiarum actionem. First are mentioned those parts of the psalm which belong to giving thanks; Secundo commemorat beneficia praestita, ibi, in te domine speravi. second, he remembers the favors he has received, at in you, O Lord, have I hoped (Ps 30:2). Circa primum duo facit. He makes two points regarding the first. Primo invitat alios ad gratiarum actiones. First, he invites others to give thanks. Secundo ipse gratias agit, ibi, exaltabo te. Second, he himself gives thanks: I will extol you (Ps 29:2). Titulus, psalmus David in consummatione tabernaculi: The title is a psalm for David at the finishing of the tabernacle. et nota historiam 1 Reg. 4, ubi filii Israel tempore Heli pugnaverunt contra Philistaeos, et arca Dei capta est, et fuit in terra illa sex mensibus, tandem propter plagam remiserunt eam in Gabaa: et mortuo Saule, David tulit eam, et portavit in Hierusalem, et fecit ei tabernaculum, et lusit ante eam, ut habetur 2 Reg. 6; et tunc videtur factus fuisse iste psalmus. And note the history in 1 Samuel 4, where the sons of Israel in the time of Eli fought against the Philistines, and the ark of God was captured and was in their land for six months until they returned it to Gibeah. And once Saul had died, David brought it and carried it into Jerusalem and built a tabernacle for it and danced before it, as found in 2 Samuel 6. It seems that this psalm was made then. Mystice per tabernaculum designatur sancta Ecclesia: Apoc. 21: ecce tabernaculum Dei cum hominibus. Hoc tabernaculum, scilicet Ecclesia, ereptum est de manibus Philistiim, id est Daemonum: et quae in psalmo isto dicuntur, pertinent ad dona spiritus sancti, per quae istud tabernaculum consummatur. Mystically, the holy Church is signified by the tabernacle. Behold, the tabernacle of God with men (Rev 21:3). This tabernacle, namely the Church, was torn from the hands of the Philistines, that is, the demons, and those things said in the psalm pertain to the gifts of the Holy Spirit, through which this tabernacle is finished. Dividitur ergo in partes duas. Therefore, it is divided into two parts. In prima invitat alios ad offerendum Deo per gratiarum actionem. In the first part, he invites others to offer thanksgiving to God. Secundo commemorat beneficia, ibi, vox domini super aquas. In the second, he remembers the favors: the voice of the Lord is upon the waters. Circa primum tria facit. He makes three points about thanksgiving. Primo ostendit cui sit offerendum. First, he shows to whom it should be offered; Secundo qui offerat. second, who offers; Tertio quid debeant offerre. third, what should be offered. Dicit ergo, afferte. Sed cui? Domino, soli, non alii: Exod. 22. Quod soli Deo debeat offerri, dicitur 1 Paral. ult.: tua sunt omnia; et quae de manu tua accepimus, dedimus tibi. Therefore he says, bring. But to whom? To the Lord alone, not to others (Exod 22:20). That it should be offered to God alone is said, all things are yours and we have given you what we received of your hand (1 Chr 29:14). Qui debebant offerre ostendit, filii Dei: Eccl. 34: dona iniquorum non probat altissimus: Gen. 4: Deus primo respexit ad Abel, et ad munera ejus postea, quia debet homo primo Deo placere, et postea ei oblationem facere; He shows who should offer: O you children of God. The Most High approves not the gifts of the wicked (Sir 34:23). The Lord first had respect to Abel and his offerings (Gen 4:4), since a man should first please God and afterwards bring an offering. et ideo dicit, filii Dei, per fidem: Joan. 1: quotquot autem receperunt eum, dedit eis potestatem filios Dei fieri, his qui credunt in nomine ejus. And so he says they are children of God through faith. But as many as received him, he gave them power to be made the sons of God to those who believe in his name (John 1:12). Item per caritatem: 1 Jo. 3: videte qualem caritatem dedit nobis Deus et cetera. Per bona opera: Rom. 8: qui spiritu Dei aguntur, hi filii Dei sunt. They are also children of God through charity. Behold what manner of charity the Father has bestowed upon us (1 John 3:1). They are also children of God through good works. Who are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God (Rom 8:14). 257. Afferte. Hic ostendit quid debeat offerri. 257. Bring to the Lord glory and honor. Here he shows what should be offered. Et primo ponit corporalem sacrificium. First, he describes the bodily sacrifice; Secundo spirituale, ibi, afferte domino gloriam. second, the spiritual, at bring to the Lord glory. De tribus generibus animalium fiebat oblatio: scilicet de bobus, capris, et arietibus: et super cetera animalia agnus maxime consuetum sacrificium erat. Unde Exod. 29, dicitur, quod quolibet mane et vespere unus agnus immolabatur: quia per agnum maxime et expressius figurabatur Christus: Joan. 1: ecce agnus Dei. Et ideo dicit, afferte domino filios arietum, id est agnos. Offering was made from three kinds of animals: from the cattle, the goats, and the sheep. And more than the rest, the lamb was the most usual sacrifice. So it is said that a lamb was offered every morning and evening (Exod 29:39), since Christ is figured most excellently and distinctly by a lamb. Behold the Lamb of God (John 1:29). And so he says, bring to the Lord the offspring of rams, that is, lambs. Mystice arietes sunt principes gregis, id est apostoli: Psalm. 46: principes populorum congregati sunt. Filii eorum sunt fideles: 1 Cor. 4: in Christo Jesu per Evangelium ego vos genui. Afferte, ergo vos ipsos Deo, qui estis filii arietum. Mystically, the rams are the leaders of the flock, that is, the apostles. The princes of the people are gathered together (Ps 46:10). Their offspring are the faithful. In Jesus Christ, through the Gospel, I have begotten you (1 Cor 4:15). Bring to God, therefore, yourselves, who are the offspring of rams. 258. Afferte. Consequenter ponit spirituale sacrificium. 258. Bring to the Lord glory to his name. Afterwards, he describes the spiritual sacrifice.
