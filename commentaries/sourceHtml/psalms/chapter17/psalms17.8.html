      Et dissipavit eos, scilicet peccatores, qui aliquando ex eis moventur: secundum diversitatem enim ventorum est diversitas motus fulguris: nam sicut superius cum de modo ventorum agebatur dixit, volavit volavit et cetera ut ostenderet diversum modum ventorum, ita hic dicit, fulgura et cetera ut ostendat diversum motum fulgurum. And he scattered them, namely, sinners, who sometimes are moved by these. For there are different movements of lightning which correspond to the different winds. For just as above he said, he flew, he flew, about the ways in which the wind is agitated to show the different forms of the winds, so here he says, lightnings, to show the different types of lightning. Dicit, conturbavit eos, quia dicit Plinius (Lib. 2, c. 12), quod secundum fulgura sunt augurationes; quia quandoque est bonum signum, scilicet quando fiunt ab oriente: aliquando non est bonum; et ideo homines augurantes conturbantur propter praesagia futurorum. He says, he troubled them, since Pliny says that augury is based on lightning, since sometimes it is a good sign, such as when it is in the east, and sometimes it is not good; and so men making these predictions are disturbed by these signs of future things. 131. Et apparuerunt. Hic agit de generatione aquarum, quae ex aliquibus principiis emanant, quae fontes dicuntur, ex quibus est omnis generatio aquarum. 131. Then the fountains of waters appeared. Here he treats the generation of waters which come forth from certain beginnings, which are called fountains from which all water is generated. Hi autem dupliciter generantur. Now these are generated in two ways. Aliquando ex causa consueta et naturali: sicut cum vapores super terram elevantur, et ex hac elevatione infrigidantur superius, et descendunt et fiunt pluviae: ita etiam ex calore terrae interius, et quando vapores non exeunt, congregantur et resolvuntur in aquam, et fiunt fontes aquarum. Sicut pluviae generantur in aere, ita fontes in terra; et ideo circa montes a quibus vapores non exeunt, fiunt fontes. Et hoc est quod dicit, apparuerunt fontes aquarum. Sometimes they arise from a fitting and natural cause, and it is like when the vapors above the earth are lifted up, and, as a result of this elevation, they congeal higher up and descend and become rain. So also when vapors rising from the interior heat of the earth do not exit, but gather together and condense into water and become fountains of water. Just as rain is generated in the air, so fountains are generated in the earth, and so fountains arise around mountains from which vapors cannot exit. And this is what he says, the fountains of waters appeared. Aliquando generantur fontes ex subversione terrae ex terraemotu, ex cujus commotione apparent venae aquae in profundo terrae submersae; et ideo dicit, et revelata sunt fundamenta orbis terrarum. Sometimes fountains are generated from the overturning of the earth during an earthquake, from which shaking veins of water submerged deep in the earth appear, and so he says, the foundations of the world were discovered. Philosophus. Subversio est a vento intus incluso, sicut ventus in aere commovet aerem. Sed quando retinetur ventus fit terraemotus: et uterque ventus videtur ira Dei. Et ideo dicit facta per terram, sicut terraemotum. The Philosopher thinks that overturning is caused by the enclosed interior wind, like when the wind in the air moves the air. But when the wind is held in, there is an earthquake, and both winds seem to come from the wrath of God. And so he says it was done by the earth, as in an earthquake. Mystice secundum spirituales effectus: et sicut supra ostensum est mysterium incarnationis signans ipsam incarnationem per quam descendit, et ascensionem; ita hic designantur ea quae secuta sunt post. Mystically, this describes spiritual effects, and just as above, the mystery of the Incarnation was shown, signifying that Incarnation through which he descended and the Ascension, so here those things which came afterwards are signified. Primo ergo ostendit ejus occultationem. First, therefore, he shows his concealment; Secundo Ecclesiae congregationem, ibi, in circuitu ejus. second, the congregation of the Church, at his pavilion round about him; Tertio apostolorum praedicationem, ibi, tenebrosa aqua. and third, the preaching of the apostles, at dark waters. Quantum ad primum dicit, posuit tenebras. Glossa distinguit quadrupliciter tenebras. In regard to the first, he says, he made darkness. The gloss distinguishes four kinds of darkness. Primo humanitatem: Ezech. 32: solem nube tegam. Isa. 45: vere tu es Deus absconditus. The first is his humanity. I will cover heaven with a cloud (Ezek 32:7). Verity, you are a hidden God (Isa 45:15). Secundo species sacramentales, sicut Baptismus, et alia sacramenta, in quibus divina virtus operatur secrete. The second is the sacramental appearances, as in baptism, and the other sacraments in which the divine power works secretly. Tertio latuit in fide fidelium: 2 Cor. 5: quamdiu sumus in corpore, peregrinamur a domino. Third, he is hidden in the faith of the faithful. While we are in the body, we are absent from the Lord (2 Cor 5:6). Quarto latenter operatur aliquid per malos, qui sunt tenebrae: Jo. 1: lux in tenebris lucet, et tenebrae eam non comprehenderunt. Fourth is when he accomplishes something in a hidden way through the evil, who are darkness. The light shines in the darkness and the darkness did not comprehend it (John 1:5). Aliquando mali permittuntur aliquid facere contra sanctos; sed his tenebris existentibus, tabernaculum ejus, id est Ecclesia, est in circuitu ejus: Ps. 45: sanctificavit tabernaculum suum altissimus: Apo. 21: ecce tabernaculum Dei cum hominibus et cetera per fidem et caritatem, in quantum sibi inhaerent tamquam medio, qui eis aequaliter favet, ut dicit glossa. Sometimes the evil are permitted to do something against the saints, but in the midst of this darkness his pavilion, that is, his Church, is round about him. The Most High has sanctified his own tabernacle (Ps 45:5). Behold, the tabernacle of God with men (Rev 21:3), through faith and charity, insofar as they cling to him as a medium, who equally supports them, as the gloss says. Tenebrosa aqua in nubibus aeris. Hic agit de praedicatione Apostolorum. Dark waters in the clouds of the air. Here he treats the preaching of the apostles. Et primo ponit qualitatem praedicationis. First he describes the quality of their preaching; Secundo conditionem praedicantium, ibi, nubes. second, the condition of those preaching, at clouds; Tertio praedicationis effectum, apparuerunt fontes aquarum. and third, the effect of preaching: the fountains of waters appeared. Dicit ergo, tenebrosa aqua, id est doctrina, in nubibus, id est in prophetis et praedicatoribus. Hos vocat nubes, quia a terrenis elevati in nubibus compluunt verbum Dei: Isa. 60: qui sunt isti qui ut nubes volant et cetera. Et 45: rorate caeli desuper, et nubes pluant justum. Therefore, he says, dark water, that is, doctrine, in the clouds, that is, in the prophets and preachers. He calls them clouds because, lifted up above the earth as clouds, they rain down the word of God. Who are these, who fly as clouds? (Isa 60:8). Drop down dew, ye heavens, from above, and let the clouds rain on the just (Isa 45:8). Vel dicit, in nubibus aeris, id est apostolis elevatis a terra: Isa. 5: mandabo nubibus ne pluant super eam imbrem. Or he says, in the clouds of the air, that is, the apostles lifted up above the earth. I will command the clouds to rain no rain upon it (Isa 5:6). Et dicuntur apostoli aqua tenebrosa in comparatione ad fulgorem, id est Christum, qui apparebit videntibus eum; 1 Cor. 13: videmus nunc per speculum in aenigmate, tunc autem facie ad faciem. And the apostles are called dark waters in comparison to the light, that is, Christ who appeared to those seeing him. We see now through a glass in a dark manner; but then face to face (1 Cor 13:12). Vel aliter, et sic punctetur: tenebrosa aqua in nubibus aeris: prae fulgore in conspectu ejus nubes transierunt: postea sequitur, grando et carbones ignis et cetera. Or it can be punctuated in another way: dark waters in the clouds of the air, at the brightness that was before him the clouds passed. Afterwards it follows: hail and coals of fire. Et distinguitur duplex doctrina: And a twofold teaching is distinguished: scilicet prophetarum, et haec est obscura, quia velamen habet, ut dicitur 2 Cor. 3: usque in hodiernum diem idipsum velamen in lectione veteris testamenti manet non revelatum, quoniam in Christo evacuatur. Ideo dicitur, tenebrosa aqua in prophetis, id est doctrina. that of the prophets, which is obscure because it is veiled, as is said: for, until this present day, the selfsame veil, in the reading of the Old Testament, remains not taken away, because in Christ it is made void (2 Cor 3:14). And so the doctrine is said to be dark waters in the prophets. Sed doctrina novi testamenti est clara; et ideo dicit, prae fulgore; tota est una dictio, id est fulgida quia, ut dicitur Eph. 3: aliis in generationibus non est agnitum: Ps. 147: non fecit taliter omni nationi. But the doctrine of the New Testament is clear, and so he says, at the brightness. The whole is one saying: that is, it is brightness, as was said: in other generations it was not known (Eph 3:5). He has not done in like manner to every nation (Ps 147:9). Consequenter agit de ipsis doctoribus, et comparantur nubibus, sagittis et fulgoribus: Afterwards, he treats the doctors, and they are compared to the clouds, arrows, and lightning. nubibus pro praedicatoribus. The clouds are the preachers. Et dicit tria. And he says three things about them. Primo eorum transitum; nubes. First is their passing: clouds. Qualitatem praedicationis, grando et carbones ignis. Then is the quality of the preaching: hail and coals of fire. Auctoritatem praedicandi, intonuit. Then is the authority of their preaching: thundered. Nubes, id est apostoli, transierunt, de Judaeis ad gentes: Job. 37: nubes spargunt lumen suum, quae lustrant per circuitum. Act. 13: vobis oportebat primum loqui verbum Dei; sed quia et cetera. The clouds, that is, the apostles, passed, from the Jews to the gentiles. The clouds spread their light which go round about (Job 37:11–12). To you it behooved us first to speak the word of God, but because you reject it . . . we turn to the gentiles (Acts 13:46). Grando nocet multum fructibus et floribus, et eorum praedicatio fuit quasi grando comminationis. Et carbones ignis, id est verba inflammantia; et auctoritas, quia dominus per eos loquebatur. Hail damages many fruits and flowers, and their preaching was like a hail of threats. And coals of fire, that is, burning words, and they had authority because the Lord spoke through them.
