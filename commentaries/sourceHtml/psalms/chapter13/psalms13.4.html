      Movet primo quaestionem, First, he brings the question forward; et interponit in se culpam impiorum, and then, he interposes the guilt of the wicked. et loquitur sic. Dico quod sunt sepulcrum patens, et quod non est Deus in corde eorum: sed numquid cognoscent, quod dominus est in generatione justa? Quasi dicat: hoc debent cognoscere, et quod dominus sit in ea: Hier. 14: tu in nobis es, domine, et nomen sanctum tuum et cetera. Ps. 21: tu autem in sancto habitas, laus Israel. Est ergo dominus in eo sicut in templo. He speaks in this way, “I say that you are an open sepulcher, and that God is not in your heart, but surely you know that the Lord is in the just generation?” It is as if he were to say that they ought to know and that the Lord is in it. You, O Lord, are among us, and your holy name (Jer 14:9). But you dwell in the holy place, the praise of Israel (Ps 21:4). Therefore, the Lord is in it just as he is in the temple. Et omnes qui operantur iniquitatem, scilicet quantum ad Deum: et devorant plebem meam sicut escam panis, quantum ad proximos, quos devorant auferendo bona eorum: Zach. 11: voret unusquisque carnem proximi sui: Michaeae 3: comederunt carnem populi mei, et pellem eorum desuper excoriaverunt: Eccles. 34: panis egentium vita pauperis: qui defraudat illum, homo sanguinis est. And all those who work iniquity, namely, in regard to God, and who devour my people as they eat bread, in regard to their neighbors, who devour them by taking away their goods. Let everyone devour the flesh of his neighbor (Zech 11:9). Who have eaten the flesh of my people, and have flayed the skin off of them (Mic 3:3). The bread of the needy is the life of the poor; he who defrauds them of it is a man of blood (Sir 34:25). Non invocaverunt Deum, id est non habent spem de Deo: Deum non invocaverunt. Ps. 52: Et sequitur ex hoc, quod non habent securitatem: ideo dicit: illic trepidaverunt et cetera. Prov. 28: fugit impius nemine persequente: Job 15: sonitus terroris semper in auribus illius, et pax cum sit illi, semper insidias suspicatur. They have not called upon the Lord, that is, they do not have hope from God. They have not called upon God (Ps 52:6), and it follows from this that they do not have security, and so he says, they have trembled (Ps 52:6). The wicked man flees, when no one pursues (Prov 28:1). The sound of dread is always in his ears and when there is peace, he always suspects treason (Job 15:21). Sed qui invocant Deum salvantur: Joel. 2: omnes qui invocaverunt nomen domini, salvi erunt: Prov. 18: turris fortissima nomen domini. But those who call upon God are saved. Those who call upon the Lord shall be saved (Joel 2:32). The name of the Lord is a strong tower (Prov 18:10). Non ergo tales cognoscent, quia dominus in generatione justa est. Et ostendit quod non, per signum; quia confudistis consilium inopis, id est confusibilem reputastis, et blasphemastis quantum in vobis est: Therefore, this sort do not know that the Lord is in the just generation. And he shows that they do not, through a sign, since you have confounded the counsel of the poor man, that is, you considered him as one able to be shamed and blasphemed as much as you could. et hoc est consilium inopis, ut sciat quoniam dominus spes ejus est: Ps. 21: speravit in domino, eripiat eum, salvum faciat eum, quoniam vult eum. Matth. 27. Qui dicit: si vis perfectus esse et cetera. Item 19. Hoc consilium semper divites contemnunt: Prov. 1: despexistis omne consilium meum, et increpationes meas neglexistis. And the counsel of the poor is that he knows that the Lord is his hope. He hoped in the Lord, let him deliver him. Let him save him, seeing he delights in him (Ps 21:9; Matt 27:43). It is he who says, if you want to be perfect, go, sell what you have and give to the poor and come, follow me (Matt 19:21). The rich always despise this counsel. You have despised all my counsel, and have neglected my reproofs (Prov 1:25). Et unde? Quoniam dominus spes ejus est: quia non habent in mundo unde sperent nisi in Deo, qui spes sanctorum est. And where does the counsel of the poor come from? Because the Lord is his hope, since he has nothing in this world in which he can hope except in God, who is the hope of the saints. 102. Omnes antiqui expectabant hoc. Gen. 49: non auferetur sceptrum de Juda et cetera. Sed hoc expectabatur ex Sion, id est ex Judaeis: Jo. 4: salus ex Judaeis est. 102. All of the ancients looked for this. The scepter shall not be taken away from Judah (Gen 49:10). But they looked for this from Zion, that is, from the Jews. Salvation is of the Jews (John 4:22). Sed quando? Responsio: hoc habebimus, cum averterit dominus captivitatem plebis suae, qui sunt in captivitate peccati et carcere inferni: Isa. 49: equidem captivitas a forte tolletur, et quod captum fuerit a robusto. Et tunc laetetur Jacob, id est populus Dei interius: et exultet Israel exterius. But when? I respond: we will have this when the Lord shall have turned away the captivity of his people, who are in captivity to sin and the prison of hell. Verily, the captivity will be taken away from the strong, and what was taken by the mighty (Isa 49:25). And then Jacob, that is, the people of God, shall be glad interiorly, and Israel shall rejoice exteriorly. Super Psalmo 14 Psalm 14 Domine, quis habitabit in tabernaculo tuo? Lord, who shall dwell in your tabernacle? 14:1 Psalmus David. Domine, quis habitabit in tabernaculo tuo? Aut quis requiescet in monte sancto tuo? 14:1 A psalm of David. Lord, who shall dwell in your tabernacle? Or who shall rest in your holy hill? 14:2 Qui ingreditur sine macula, et operatur justitiam; 14:3a qui loquitur veritatem in corde suo: qui non egit dolum in lingua sua, 14:2 He who walks without blemish, and works justice; 14:3a he who speaks truth in his heart, who has not used deceit in his tongue, 14:3b nec fecit proximo suo malum, et opprobrium non accepit adversus proximos suos. 14:3b nor has done evil to his neighbor, nor taken up a reproach against his neighbors. 14:4a Ad nihilum deductus est in conspectu ejus malignus; timentes autem Dominum glorificat. 14:4a In his sight the malignant is brought to nothing; but he glorifies those who fear the Lord. 14:4b Qui jurat proximo suo, et non decipit; 14:5 qui pecuniam suam non dedit ad usuram, et munera super innocentem non accepit. Qui facit haec, non movebitur in aeternum. 14:4b He who swears to his neighbor, and deceives not; 14:5 he who has not put out his money to usury, nor taken bribes against the innocent; he who does these things shall not be moved forever. 103. Supra psalmista egit de malitia et dolo adversariorum; hic agit de propria justitia. 103. Above, the psalmist treated the malice and deceit of his adversaries; here he treats individual justice. Et primo ostendit qualis sit justitia quam Deus acceptat. First, he shows what sort of justice God accepts. Secundo, quasi gratias agens, ostendit qualis sit sua justitia, ibi, conserva me. Second, as though giving thanks, he shows what his own justice is like, at preserve me (Ps 15:1). Titulus, psalmus David. The title is a psalm of David. In psalmo isto duo facit: nam quasi sacerdos existens coram Deo consulit Deum. In this psalm, he does two things, for he takes counsel with God like a priest standing before God. Primo ponitur quaestio. First, he puts forward a question. Secundo responsio, ibi, qui ingreditur sine macula. Second, he gives the response: he who walks without blemish. Praemittit ergo duplicem quaestionem: quia duplex est status praesentis Ecclesiae et futurae. And he asks a double question, since the state of the Church is twofold: that of the present and that of the future. Primus est militantium: Apoc. 14: amodo jam dicit spiritus ut requiescant. First is the time of fighting. From henceforth the Spirit says that they may rest (Rev 14:13). Et hi duo status signati sunt in veteri testamento. Quia primo habuerunt tabernaculum, Exod. 26, quamdiu habuerunt bella et labores. 2 Reg. 7: factum est cum dedisset dominus requiem David ab universis inimicis suis, dixit ad Nathan: vides quod ego habitem in domo cedrina, et arca Dei posita est in medio pellium? Postea fecit templum, quando habuit pacem. And these two states are signified in the Old Testament because they first had the tabernacle, as in Exodus 26, when they were involved in war and labors. It came to pass when the Lord had given rest to David from all his enemies, he said to Nathan, “Do you see that I dwell in a house of cedar, and the ark of God is lodged within skins?” (2 Sam 7:1–2). Afterwards, they built the temple when they had peace. Per tabernaculum designatur Ecclesia militans, per templum in monte factum status futurae vitae: et ideo dicit, quis habitabit in tabernaculo tuo? id est in praesenti Ecclesia; quasi dicat: quis est dignus habitare? Peccatores enim habitant numero, non merito. The Church Militant is signified by the tabernacle, and the state of the future life by the temple built on the mountain. And so he says, who shall dwell in your tabernacle, that is, in the present Church, as though to say, “Who is worthy to dwell in it?” For sinners dwell there numerically, but not by merit. Hieronymus habet, quis peregrinabitur? Ps. 67: habitare facit unanimes in domo. Jerome has, who will walk around? Who makes men of one manner to dwell in a house (Ps 67:7). Secunda quaestio, quis requiescet in monte sancto tuo? Et dicitur mons sanctus, quia nihil est ibi coinquinatum: Hier. 31: benedicat tibi dominus, pulchritudo justitiae, mons sanctus: Isa. 35: via sancta vocabitur: Exod. 15: introduces eos, et plantabis in monte hereditatis tuae, firmissimo habitaculo tuo, quod operatus es, domine. The second question is, who shall rest in your holy hill? And it is called his holy hill since there is nothing tainted there. The Lord bless you, the beauty of justice, the holy mountain (Jer 31:23). It shall be called the holy way (Isa 35:8). You shall bring them in, and plant them in the mountain of your inheritance, in your most firm habitation which you have made, O Lord (Exod 15:17). 104. Qui. Hic ponitur responsio: et circa hoc duo facit. 104. He who walks without blemish. Here, he gives the response, and he makes two points. Primo commemorat merita habitantium in praedictis locis, scilicet in tabernaculo et in monte sancto Dei. First, he calls to mind the merits of those living in the places that were mentioned, that is, in the tabernacle and on the holy mountain of God. Secundo praemium, ibi, qui facit haec, non commovebitur in aeternum. Second, he brings to mind their reward, at he who does these things shall not be moved forever.
