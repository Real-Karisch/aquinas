      Super Psalmo 51 Psalm 51 Quid gloriaris in malitia Why do you glory in malice? 51:1 In finem. Intellectus David, 51:2 cum venit Doeg Idumaeus, et nuntiavit Sauli: Venit David in domum Abimelech. 51:3 Quid gloriaris in malitia, qui potens es in iniquitate? 51:4 Tota die injustitiam cogitavit lingua tua; sicut novacula acuta fecisti dolum. 51:1 Unto the end, understanding for David, 51:2 when Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, “David went to the house of Abimelech.” 51:3 Why do you glory in malice, you who are mighty in iniquity? 51:4 All the day long your tongue has devised injustice: as a sharp razor, you have wrought deceit. 51:5 Dilexisti malitiam super benignitatem; iniquitatem magis quam loqui aequitatem. 51:6 Dilexisti omnia verba praecipitationis; lingua dolosa. 51:5 You have loved malice more than goodness, and iniquity rather than to speak righteousness. 51:6 You have loved all the words of ruin, O deceitful tongue. 51:7 Propterea Deus destruet te in finem; evellet te, et emigrabit te de tabernaculo tuo, et radicem tuam de terra viventium. 51:7 Therefore will God destroy you forever: he will pluck you out, and remove you from your dwelling place, and your root out of the land of the living. 51:8 Videbunt justi, et timebunt; et super eum ridebunt, et dicent: 51:9 ‘Ecce homo qui non posuit Deum adjutorem suum; sed speravit in multitudine divitiarum suarum, et praevaluit in vanitate sua.’ 51:8 The just shall see and fear, and shall laugh at him, and say: 51:9 “Behold the man who made not God his helper, but trusted in the abundance of his riches, and prevailed in his vanity.” 51:10 Ego autem, sicut oliva fructifera in domo Dei; speravi in misericordia Dei, in aeternum et in saeculum saeculi. 51:11 Confitebor tibi in saeculum, quia fecisti; et exspectabo nomen tuum, quoniam bonum est in conspectu sanctorum tuorum. 51:10 But I, as a fruitful olive tree in the house of God, have hoped in the mercy of God forever, yea, forever and ever. 51:11 I will praise you forever, because you have done it: and I will wait on your name, for it is good in the sight of your saints. 510. Hic incipit secunda quinquagena. Et sicut dictum est in principio libri, prima quinquagena pertinet ad poenitentes; ista secunda quinquagena pertinet ad proficientes. 510. Here begins the second set of fifty psalms. And as was said in the beginning of the book, the first fifty pertain to penitents; this second fifty to the proficient. Circa quos sunt tria per ordinem videnda: About these, three things should be examined in order: nam primus gradus proficientium est justificatio: for the first stage of the proficient is justification; secundus gradus est exercitium bonorum operum: the second, the doing of good works; tertius est consideratio divinorum operum. the third, the consideration of divine works. Et sic ista quinquagena dividitur in tres partes. And thus this group of fifty is divided into three parts: Primo tangit de his quae pertinent ad justificationem; the first touches on what pertains to justification; secundo agit de his quae pertinent ad executionem bonorum operum, ibi, Deus judicium; the second is about what pertains to the doing of good works, at give to the king your judgment, O God (Ps 71:2); tertio agit de his quae pertinent ad considerationem divinorum operum, ibi, Deus stetit. the third is about what pertains to the consideration of divine works, at God stood (Ps 81:1). In justificatione duo sunt necessaria: scilicet aversio a peccato, et conversio ad Deum. Et sic duo sunt in peccato per oppositum; scilicet aversio a Deo, et conversio ad peccatum. In justification two things are necessary: namely, turning away from sin and conversion to God. And thus there are in sin conversely two things: turning away from God and conversion to sin. Primo ergo ponit psalmos qui pertinent ad aversionem et detestationem peccati; Therefore, he puts first the psalms that pertain to the turning away from and abhorrence of sin; secundo ponit psalmos qui faciunt sive continent subjectionem Deo, ibi, Ps. 61: nonne Deo subjecta. second, the psalms that effect or contain subjection to God, at Psalm 61: shall not my soul be subject to God? (verse 2). Circa primum duo facit. Regarding the first, he makes two points: Primo ostendit malitiam peccatorum; first, he shows the evil of sinners; secundo ostendit mala quae a peccatoribus fiunt. second, the evils that happen because of sinners. Ex duobus aggravatur peccatum: Sin is worsened by two things, scilicet ex affectione peccandi, namely, affection for sinning et ex contemptu Dei. and contempt of God. Primo ergo aggravat malitiam peccatorum ex primo; First, therefore, he dramatizes the evil of sinners from the first; secundo aggravat eorum malitiam ex secundo, ibi, Ps. 52: dixit insipiens. second, from the second, at the fool said (Ps 52:1). Titulus: in finem intellectus David, cum venisset Doeg Idumaeus, et nuntiasset Sauli, venit David in domum Abimelech, haec historia habetur 1 Reg. 21 et 22, quando scilicet David fugiens a Saule, venit in Zebe ubi erant sacerdotes, et accepit gladium Goliae et panes propositionis, et Doeg Idumaeus interfuit, qui inter pastores erat potens; et conquerente Saule quod nullus pugnaret pro eo contra David, iste Doeg enarravit quomodo Abimelech sacerdos receperat David, et dederat ei gladium Goliae, et panes propositionis: et sic iratus Saul mandavit occidi omnes sacerdotes. Cumque illi nollent mittere manus ad occidendum sacerdotes, iste Doeg interfecit omnes sacerdotes. The title is unto the end, understanding for David, when Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, “David went to the house of Abimelech.” This history is in 1 Samuel 21 and 22, when David, fleeing from Saul, came to Nob (where the priests were) and received Goliath’s sword and the bread of proposition, and Doeg was present, who was influential among the shepherds; and when Saul complained that no one was fighting on his behalf against David, this same Doeg detailed how Abimelech the priest received David and gave him Goliath’s sword and the bread of proposition; and, angered thereby, Saul ordered that all the priests be killed. Sed tamen diversitas est in titulo et in historia, quia princeps sacerdotum in historia vocatur Achimelech, in titulo Abimelech. But there is a discrepancy in the title and in the history, because historically the high priest is called Ahimelech but in the title Abimelech. Et dicit glossa quod hoc factum est vitio scriptorum, vel propter mysterium: nam per David signatur Christus: tum quia usus est officio regiae dignitatis, tum etiam quia usus fuit dignitate sacerdotali, comedendo panes propositionis. And the gloss says that this happened either because of a scribal error or because of the underlying mystery that Christ is signified through David, both because he exercised the status of kingly dignity and because, in eating the bread of proposition, he exercised the priestly dignity. Doeg interpretatur motus: Idumaeus terrenus; Saul petitio; Abimelech patris mei regnum. Per Doeg ergo qui fuit motus a terrenis, cum venisset David, scilicet Christus ad Abimelech, id est ad Judaeos qui est patris mei regnum, nuntiavit Sauli, id est morti, quod impii manibus et verbis accersierunt eum et occiderunt eum. Doeg means “moved,” Idumean “earthly,” Saul “petition,” and Abimelech “the kingdom of my father.” It was through Doeg, then, who was moved by earthly things, when David (that is, Christ) came to Abimelech, that is, to the Jews who are “the kingdom of my father,” he told Saul, that is, death, because those impious of hand and word accused him and killed him. Vel, Doeg, id est Antichristus qui commovebit in fine mundi terrenos. Cum venisset Christus in Ecclesiam, nuntiavit Diabolo, quod persequetur Ecclesiam. Et principaliter intelligitur totus iste psalmus de malis qui persequuntur Christum, vel in se, vel in membris. Or Doeg is the antichrist who will cause the upheaval of earthly things at the end of the world. When Christ came to his Church, he spoke to the devil, because he was persecuting the Church. And this psalm refers principally to the evil people who persecute Christ, either in himself or in his members. Dividitur autem psalmus iste in partes duas. The psalm itself is divided into two parts. In prima agit de malitia peccatoris, qui ad iniquitatem afficitur. In the first part, it deals with the evil of the sinner, who is drawn to iniquity. Secundo agit de justitia sanctorum, ibi, ego autem sicut oliva. In the second, it deals with the righteousness of the holy ones, at but I, as a fruitful olive tree. Circa primum duo facit. Regarding the first, it makes two points.
