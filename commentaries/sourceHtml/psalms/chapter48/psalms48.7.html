      Hieronymus: laudabunt te cum bene sibi fuerit, id est homines laudant et serviunt divitibus, quamdiu suppetunt et prosperantur in divitiis; sed si mutetur fortuna, mutantur, necdum non laudant, sed detrahunt eis. Jerome has, they praised you while you did good for them, that is, men praise riches and slave after them, as long as the riches are at hand and they prosper in them, but if fortune changes, men change, and not only no longer praise but rather disparage them. Introibit. Hic ponit defectum spiritualium. He shall go. Here, he describes the loss of spiritual goods. Et primo ostendit defectum; And first he notes the loss; secundo ponit ordinem perveniendi ad ipsum defectum, ibi, homo. second, he describes the path of arriving at this loss, at man. Et primo ponit defectum culpae; First, he describes the loss that consists in guilt; secundo poenae. second, that which consists in punishment. Quantum ad primum dicit, introibit, scilicet iste peccator sic dives, et quem laudant omnes, dum bene est ei, introibit dico, usque ad progeniem patrum suorum, scilicet per imitationem, dum omnia mala quae tota sua progenies facit, ipse operatur. Matth. 23: et vos implete mensuram patrum vestrorum. Jer. 11: reversi sunt ad iniquitates patrum suorum priores. In regard to the first he says, he will go, namely, this sinner—while he is rich and everyone praises him, while he is prospering—he will go in, I say, to the generations of his fathers, namely, by imitating them, doing himself all the evils which all his ancestors did. And fill up then the measure of your fathers (Matt 23:32). They are returned to the former iniquities of their fathers (Jer 11:10). Vel, introibit et cetera quia patres sui impii sunt in inferno, et iste ibit illuc. Or, he shall go in means that his wicked fathers are in hell, and he will go there. Quantum ad secundum dicit: et usque in aeternum non videbit lumen: quia quasi, scilicet hic in suo aeterno, scilicet in vita, noluit sequi lumen rationis, ideo, in aeternum non videbit lumen. Job 15: non credit quod reverti possit de tenebris ad lumen. Matth. 25: inutilem servum mittite in tenebras exteriores et cetera. In regard to the second, he says, and he shall never see the light, since, just as he refused to follow the light of reason during life and so will be the same in eternity, he shall therefore never see the light. He believes not that he may return from darkness to light (Job 15:22). The unprofitable servant cast out into the outer darkness (Matt 25:30). Et quo ordine? Quia cum esset praeditus lumine rationis, sicut homo in honore constitutus, noluit illo lumine regi, assimilatus est jumentis insipientibus, et ideo facit sicut jumenta, debet deputari occisioni. And in what order? Because, although man was endowed with the light of reason and so established in honor, he refused to be ruled by the light and has been made like to senseless beasts, and so he acts like a beast and ought to be marked for slaughter. Deus deorum Dominus locutus est, et vocavit terram a solis ortu usque ad occasum. 49:2 Ex Sion species decoris ejus: 49:1 A psalm for Asaph. The God of gods, the Lord, has spoken and he has called the earth from the rising of the sun, to the going down thereof. 49:2 Out of Zion, the loveliness of his beauty, 49:3 Deus manifeste veniet; Deus noster, et non silebit. Ignis in conspectu ejus exardescet; et in circuitu ejus tempestas valida. 49:3 God shall come manifestly; our God shall come, and shall not keep silence. A fire shall burn before him and a mighty tempest shall be round about him. 49:4 Advocabit caelum desursum, et terram, discernere populum suum. 49:5 Congregate illi sanctos ejus, qui ordinant testamentum ejus super sacrificia. 49:6 Et annuntiabunt caeli justitiam ejus, quoniam Deus judex est. 49:4 He shall call heaven from above, and the earth to judge his people. 49:5 Gather together his saints to him, who set his covenant before sacrifices. 49:6 And the heavens shall declare his justice, for God is judge. 49:7 Audi, populus meus, et loquar; Israel, et testificabor tibi: Deus, Deus tuus ego sum. 49:7 Hear, O my people, and I will speak; O Israel, and I will testify to you: I am God, your God. 49:8 Non in sacrificiis tuis arguam te; holocausta autem tua in conspectu meo sunt semper. 49:9 Non accipiam de domo tua vitulos, neque de gregibus tuis hircos. 49:10 Quoniam meae sunt omnes ferae silvarum, jumenta in montibus, et boves. 49:11 Cognovi omnia volatilia caeli, et pulchritudo agri mecum est. 49:12 Si esuriero, non dicam tibi: meus est enim orbis terrae et plenitudo ejus. 49:8 I will not reprove you for your sacrifices, and your burnt offerings are always in my sight. 49:9 I will not take calves out of your house, nor he-goats out of your flocks. 49:10 For all the beasts of the woods are mine: the cattle on the hills, and the oxen. 49:11 I know all the fowls of the air, and with me is the beauty of the field. 49:12 If I should be hungry, I would not tell you, for the world is mine, and the fullness thereof. 49:13 Numquid manducabo carnes taurorum? Aut sanguinem hircorum potabo? 49:13 Shall I eat the flesh of bullocks? or shall I drink the blood of goats? 49:14 Immola Deo sacrificium laudis, et redde Altissimo vota tua. 49:14 Offer to God the sacrifice of praise and pay your vows to the Most High. 49:15 Et invoca me in die tribulationis: eruam te, et honorificabis me. 49:15 And call upon me in the day of trouble. I will deliver you, and you shall glorify me. 49:16 Peccatori autem dixit Deus: Quare tu enarras justitias meas? Et assumis testamentum meum per os tuum? 49:17 Tu vero odisti disciplinam, et projecisti sermones meos retrorsum. 49:18 Si videbas furem, currebas cum eo; et cum adulteris portionem tuam ponebas. 49:16 But to the sinner God has said, “Why do you declare my justices, and take my covenant in your mouth?” 49:17 Seeing you have hated discipline and have cast my words behind you. 49:18 If you saw a thief, you ran with him, and with adulterers you have been a partaker. 49:19 Os tuum abundavit malitia, et lingua tua concinnabat dolos. 49:20 Sedens adversus fratrem tuum loquebaris, et adversus filium matris tuae ponebas scandalum. 49:21 Haec fecisti, et tacui. Existimasti inique quod ero tui similis: arguam te, et statuam contra faciem tuam. 49:19 Your mouth has abounded with evil, and your tongue framed deceits. 49:20 Sitting you did speak against your brother, and did lay a scandal against your mother’s son. 49:21 These things you have done, and I was silent. You thought unjustly that I should be like to you: but I will reprove you, and set before your face. 49:22 Intelligite haec, qui obliviscimini Deum, nequando rapiat, et non sit qui eripiat. 49:23 Sacrificium laudis honorificabit me, et illic iter quo ostendam illi salutare Dei. 49:22 Understand these things, you that forget God, lest he snatch you away, and there be none to deliver you. 49:23 The sacrifice of praise shall glorify me, and there is the way by which I will show him the salvation of God. 490. Supra psalmista invitavit gentes ad confidendum de Deo; hic instruit eas ad cultum Dei. 490. Above, the psalmist invited the nations to trust in God; here, he instructs them in the worship of God. Titulus, psalmus Asaph. The title is a psalm for Asaph. Iste Asaph unus fuit de praefectis super cantores populorum, et super illos qui cantabant et laudabant in cymbalis, sicut 1 Par. 15 et 16 et 25, dicitur. Et dicitur psalmus Asaph quia cantatur ministerio Asaph. Asaph was one of the leaders over the singers of the people, and he was over those who sang and praised with cymbals, as in 1 Chronicles 15, 16, and 25. And it is called a psalm of Asaph because it was sung during the ministry of Asaph. Et congruit mysterio, quia Asaph interpretatur synagoga, et sic legitur synagogae persona. And this is also fitting in the mystical sense, since the name Asaph means “synagogue,” so it is read as spoken in the person of the synagogue. Ubi instructus de sacrificiis, duo proponit de cultu Dei, quae antecedunt divinum judicium: In the instruction about sacrifices, he puts forward two things regarding the worship of God, things which precede the divine judgment. et est primum. And the first part of the psalm is the first thing. Secundum est disceptatio Dei cum populo de cultu suo, ibi, audi populus meus. The second is the discussion of God with his people about his worship: hear, O my people. Ante judicium erunt tria. Before the judgment, there will be three things: Citatio judicis, the judge’s summoning, adventus judicis his coming, et apparatus. and his appearance. Primo ergo ponit citationem; Therefore, he first puts down the summoning; secundo adventum, ibi, Deus noster manifeste veniet; second, the coming, at our God shall come manifestly;
