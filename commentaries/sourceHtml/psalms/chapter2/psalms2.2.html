      In secunda ponitur eorum repressio, ibi, qui habitat in caelis irridebit eos. In the second, he describes their suppression: he who dwells in heaven shall laugh at them. Circa primum tria facit. Regarding the first, he narrates three things: Primo narrat machinantium rebellionem. first, the rebellion of those plotting; Secundo contra quem machinatur. second, whom they are plotting against; Tertio propositum machinantium. third, the purpose of those plotting. Secundum ibi, adversus dominum. The second begins at against the Lord. Tertium ibi, dirumpamus vincula eorum. The third begins at let us break their bonds apart. Primo ergo historialiter sciendum, quod quando populus molitur rebellionem, primo surgit murmur in populo, post accedit auxilium magnatum ad perficiendum. First, therefore, we should understand that historically, when a people undertakes a rebellion, murmuring first arises among the people, and afterwards the help of powerful men is brought to bear to accomplish it. Primo ergo ponit conatum populi murmurantis. Therefore, he first describes the efforts of the people who are murmuring; Secundo auxilium magnatum, ibi, astiterunt reges terrae, et principes convenerunt in unum. second, he describes the help of powerful men: the kings of the earth stood up, and the princes met together. In populo autem sunt quidam minus habentes de ratione, qui sunt impetuosi: quidam plus, qui cauti dicuntur. Within a people there are some who have less reason—the impetuous ones—and some who have more, whom we call “cautious.” Primi non moventur sensu ad rebellandum, sed magis impetu; et ideo dicit de his, fremuerunt, quod est bestiarum: Prov. 19: sicut fremitus leonis, ita et regis ira. The first are moved to rebel not by thought but rather by impulse, and therefore he says about these that they raged, which is bestial: as the roaring of a lion, so also is the anger of a king (Prov 19:12). Secundi moventur consilio; et ideo de his dicit: meditati sunt inania. Quia vanae sunt cogitationes hominum, Ps. 93. The second are moved by counsel, and so therefore he says about these, they devised vain things, since the thoughts of men . . . are vain (Ps 93:11). Populus est multitudo hominum juris consensu sociata. Et ideo Judaei dicuntur populus, quia cum lege et sub lege Dei sunt. Alii dicuntur gentes, quia non sunt sub lege Dei. Vel ad litteram. In regno David erant gentes subjugatae, et Judaei fideles; et utrique moliebantur contra eum; ideo dicit: quare fremuerunt gentes, et populi meditati sunt inania? Non interrogat sed increpat, sicut ibi Sap. 5: quid nobis profuit superbia, aut divitiarum jactantia quid contulit nobis? A people is a multitude of men associated by agreement in law. And so the Jews are called a people since they are with and under the law of God. Others are called gentiles because they are not under the law of God. (Or, literally, in the kingdom of David, there were gentile subjects and faithful Jews, and both exerted themselves against him.) So he says, why have the gentiles raged, and the people devised vain things? He does not question, but rebukes, as in Wisdom 5:8: what has pride profited us or what advantage has the boasting of riches brought us? Item minores nihil per se facere possent, nisi haberent auxilium majorum: unde ponit quosdam praebentes auxilium: Also, the lesser could do nothing themselves unless they had the help of the greater, and so he describes those providing help. primo per potentiam adjuvando et quantum ad hoc dicit: astiterunt reges terrae, et principes convenerunt in unum adversus dominum, et adversus Christum ejus; quasi dicat, illi fremuerunt, sed alii astiterunt, id est affuerunt huic malitiae. First, through the power to help, and about this he says, the kings of the earth stood up, and the princes met together, against the Lord and against his Christ, as if to say, “Some raged but others stood up,” that is, they were party to this wickedness. Item quidam praebuerunt auxilium per sapientiam consulendo; et quantum ad hoc dicit, convenerunt in unum, scilicet ad consiliandum. Littera Hieronymi habet tractabant pariter. Hier. 5: ibo igitur ad optimates, et loquar eis: ipsi enim cognoverunt viam domini, et judicium Dei sui et cetera. Also, some provided help by counseling wisdom, and about these he says that they met together, namely to consult. Jerome’s text has, they negotiated with each other. As Jeremiah 5:5 says, I will go therefore to the great men, and I will speak to them: for they know the way of the Lord, the judgment of their God. Deinde cum dicit, adversus dominum, et adversus Christum ejus et cetera ponit patientes rebellionem. Ostendit enim contra quos fuit rebellio, quia contra dominum, et contra regem ejus: reges enim dicuntur Christi, id est uncti: Ps. 104: nolite tangere Christos meos. Qui ergo rebellat regi instituto per Deum, rebellat etiam Deo: Rom. 13: qui potestati resistit, Dei ordinationi resistit. Et ideo dicit, adversus dominum, et adversus Christum ejus. Rom. 8: non te abjecerunt, sed me. Then, when he says, against the Lord and against his Christ, he is describing those suffering the rebellion. For he is setting out those against whom the rebellion was made—the Lord and his king—because kings are called “Christs,” that is, anointed ones: do not touch my Christs (Ps 104:15). Therefore, he who rebels against the king instituted by God rebels also against God. He that resists the power, resists the ordinance of God (Rom 13:2). And so he says, against the Lord and against his Christ. For they have not rejected you, but me (1 Sam 8:7). Mystice haec dicta sunt sub similitudine David de Christo: Act. 4: domine, tu dixisti per os patris nostri pueri tui David, quare fremuerunt gentes et cetera. Convenerunt enim vere in civitate ista adversus sanctum puerum tuum Jesum, quem unxisti et cetera. Et secundum hoc intelligendum est, quod gentes, scilicet milites, convenerunt contra Christum: et populi, scilicet Judaei, meditati sunt inania, credentes eum occidere totaliter, scilicet quod non resurgeret: et reges terrae, scilicet Herodes Ascalonita prior qui occidit infantes: et posterius Herodes Antipas ejusdem filius qui Pilato consensit: et principes, id est Pilatus, ut ponatur plurale pro singulari per synecdochem. Vel principes sacerdotum convenerunt in unum, id est unam pravam voluntatem, adversus dominum et adversus Christum ejus. Mystically, these things are said about Christ under the likeness of David. Lord, you said by the mouth of our father David, your servant, “Why did the gentiles rage, and the people meditate vain things? . . . For truly there assembled together in this city against your holy child Jesus, whom you have anointed” (Acts 4:25, 27). And, in accordance with this, it should be understood that the gentiles, namely, the soldiers, met together against Christ, and the people, namely the Jews, devised vain things, believing they would kill him completely (that is, that he would not rise again). And the kings of the earth indicates the first Herod of Ascalon, who killed the infants, and the later Herod Antipas, his son, who agreed with Pilate. And the princes indicates Pilate; he uses the plural in place of the singular through a synecdoche. Or the princes could be the leaders of the priests, who met together, that is, in the same depraved intention against the Lord and against his Christ. 5. Consequenter ponit propositum machinantium: unde dicit: dirumpamus vincula eorum et cetera. Quod proprie dicitur: nam regis dominium dicitur jugum. 3 Reg. 12, dicitur ad Roboam, quod alleviaret de jugo quod imposuerat eis Salomon. Sicut enim boves junguntur jugo ad opus, ita homines ad dominium regni. Jugum autem removeri non potest nisi solvantur vincula. Vincula autem sunt in regno illa quibus firmatur potestas regia in regno, sicut milites, castra, et arma. Primo ergo oportet ista dissolvere, et tunc removere jugum. 5. Next, he describes the purpose of those plotting: let us break their bonds apart. This is fittingly phrased, for the dominion of the king is called a yoke. It is said to Rehoboam that he should lighten the yoke which Solomon had imposed on them (1 Kgs 12:4). For as oxen are harnessed for work with a yoke, so are men for the king’s lordship. Now a yoke cannot be removed unless the bonds are broken apart. But the bonds in a kingdom are those things by which the royal power is made firm in the kingdom, such as soldiers, fortresses, and arms. Therefore, these must first be broken apart before the yoke can be removed. Spiritualiter in Christo est jugum lex caritatis: Matth. 11: jugum meum suave est et cetera. Vincula sunt virtutes, spes, fides, caritas: Ephes. 4: soliciti servare unitatem spiritus, in vinculo pacis. Eccl. 6: vincula illius alligatura salutaris. Quod ergo conscientia hominis non sit sub jugo legis Christi, non potest esse nisi prius rumpantur haec vincula: quod faciunt qui dicunt Deo, recede a nobis, scientiam viarum tuarum nolumus et cetera. Job 12 Hier. 2: a saeculo confregisti jugum, dirupisti vincula, et dixisti, non serviam. Spiritually, in Christ, the yoke is the law of charity: for my yoke is sweet (Matt 11:30). The bonds are the virtues of faith, hope, and charity: careful to keep the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace (Eph 4:3); her bonds are a healthful binding (Sir 6:31). The human conscience cannot be under the yoke of Christ’s law unless these bonds are first broken apart. He does this who says to God, depart from us, we do not desire the knowledge of your ways (Job 21:14). From of old you have broken my yoke, you have broken my bonds apart, and you said, “I will not serve” (Jer 2:20). Vel hoc dicitur in persona David a Christo ad suos servos: glossa quasi ipsi ita moliuntur: sed o mei dirumpamus et cetera sed non facit ad propositum. Or this is said by Christ in the person of David to his servants. The gloss says, it is as though they themselves are struggling: but O my people, let us break apart; but this is not to the point. 6. Deinde cum dicit, qui habitat, ponitur oppressio molientium in regnum David. 6. Afterwards, when he says, he who dwells, the overthrow of those rebelling against the kingdom of David is described. Et circa hoc duo facit. And regarding this, he shows how they are overthrown: Primo ostenditur quomodo opprimuntur a domino. first, by the Lord; Secundo quomodo a Christo ejus, ibi, ego autem constitutus et cetera. second, by his Christ, at I am appointed. Contra hos, scilicet dominum et contra Christum ejus moliti sunt, ut dictum est. They struggled against these, namely the Lord and his Christ, as has been said. Circa primum nota quatuor: scilicet irrisionem, subsannationem, iratam locutionem, et conturbationem. About the first, four things should be noted: laughter, derision, angry speech, and disturbance. Nam sicut aliquis puer nullius virtutis et potestatis, si pugnat contra gigantem, irridetur a gigante: ita si aliquis nullius potestatis moliri vult contra eum qui habitat in caelis, irridetur ab eo. Job 35: suspice caelum et intuere et contemplare aethera, quod altior te sit. Si peccaveris quid ei nocebit? For just as a boy, lacking strength and power, would be laughed at by a giant if he fought against a giant, so if anyone lacking power desires to rebel against he who dwells in heaven, he is laughed at by him. Look up to heaven and see, and behold the sky, that it is higher than you. If you sin, in what shall you hurt him? (Job 35:5–6). Et si perseveret impotens, tunc ille qui est potentior reprehendit et subsannat. Irrisio namque fit bucca, secundum Hieronymum in glossa; sed subsannatio rugato naso atque contracto, ex quadam scilicet levi indignatione. Prov. 1: ego quoque in interitu vestro ridebo, et subsannabo, cum vobis id quod timebatis advenerit. And if he, powerless, persists, then the more powerful one rebukes and derides him. Laughter is in the mouth, according to Jerome in the gloss, but derision is in the nose, wrinkled and contracted in petty indignation. I also will laugh in your destruction, and will mock when that which you feared comes to you (Prov 1:26). Si autem nullo modo desistat, procedit ad vindictam; et ideo dicit, tunc loquetur ad eos in ira sua, id est proferet sententiam vindictae contra eos: nam in Deum non cadit ira: sed quod est creaturae, aliquando attribuitur creatori per antropospatos, quod est humana propassio. Ps. 6: domine ne in ira tua et cetera. Now, if he does not stop, he will continue on to vengeance, and so he says, then he shall speak to them in his anger, that is, he will pronounce the sentence of vengeance against them. For anger does not come upon God, but what belongs to creatures is sometimes attributed to the Creator through anthropomorphism in regard to the emotions, which is like a human propassion. O Lord, rebuke me not in your anger (Ps 6:2). Ultimo autem sententia executioni mandatur; et ideo dicit, et in furore suo conturbabit eos, in corde et in anima in aeterna poena: hoc est sua virtute puniet eos. Job 15: cum se moverit ad quaerendum panem, novit quod paratus sit in manu ejus tenebrarum dies: terrebit eum tribulatio, et angustia vallabit eum. Finally, it is commanded that the sentence be carried out. And so he says, and he shall trouble them in his rage, with an eternal punishment in heart and soul—that is, he will punish them with his power. When he moves himself to seek bread, he knows that the day of darkness is ready at his hand. Tribulation shall terrify him, and distress shall surround him (Job 15:23–24). Haec quatuor erunt in judicio. Quia irridebit statuens eos a sinistris: Matth. 25: subsannabit, dicens: esurivi et cetera improperando. Loquetur in ira, sententiando: ite maledicti in ignem aeternum et cetera. Conturbabit, sententiam exequendo: ibunt hi in supplicium aeternum et cetera. These four things are found in the judgment: for he will laugh at them, setting them on his left: he shall deride them, saying, for I was hungry (Matt 25:42); rebuking: he shall speak in his anger; sentencing: go, you cursed, into everlasting fire (Matt 25:41); he shall trouble them, by carrying out the sentence: these shall go into eternal punishment (Matt 25:46). 7. Sequitur consequenter ostendere, quomodo comprimantur a Christo ejus, cum dicit, ego autem. Insurrexerunt autem contra Christum David, populus, gentes, et principes. 7. Next, he shows how they shall be subdued by his Christ, when he says, now, I am appointed king. Now, the people, the gentiles, and the princes rose up against David the Christ. Primo ergo ostendit, quomodo Christus se habeat ad populum. Therefore, he shows first how the Christ conducts himself in regard to the people. Secundo quomodo ad gentes, ibi, dominus dixit ad me. Second, how he conducts himself in regard to the gentiles: the Lord said to me.
