      Primo ergo ostendit, quomodo Christus se habeat ad populum. Therefore, he shows first how the Christ conducts himself in regard to the people. Secundo quomodo ad gentes, ibi, dominus dixit ad me. Second, how he conducts himself in regard to the gentiles: the Lord said to me. Tertio quomodo ad reges, ibi, et nunc reges intelligite et cetera. Third, how he conducts himself in regard to the kings: and now, O kings, understand. Dicit ergo: ego autem constitutus sum rex ab eo super Sion montem sanctum ejus et cetera. Sciendum est autem, quod constitutus est rex a Deo in Hierusalem, et sua praedicatione reduxit populum; quasi dicat: illi sic faciunt, sed intentum suum habere non possunt, quia sum constitutus, id est stabilitus rex super Sion, id est super populum Judaeorum, qui erat in Hierusalem, cujus arx est Sion: ab eo, scilicet a Deo: Ps. 117: dominus mihi adjutor, non timebo quid faciat mihi homo. Job 17: pone me Domine juxta te, et cujusvis manus pugnet contra me. Sum autem constitutus rex super Sion montem sanctum ejus, non propter me, sed ut regam populum secundum legem Dei; et ideo dicit, praedicans praeceptum ejus. Therefore, he says, I am appointed king by him over Zion his holy mountain, preaching his commandment. Now, it should be noted that he was appointed king by God in Jerusalem, and he brought the people back by his preaching, as if to say, “They act in this way, but they cannot fulfill their intent, since I am appointed, that is, made firm, as king over Zion, that is, over the people of the Jews who were in Jerusalem, the stronghold of which is Zion, by him, namely, by God.” The Lord is my helper, I will not fear what man can do to me (Ps 117:6). O Lord, set me beside you, and let any man’s hand fight against me (Job 17:3). Now, I am appointed king over Zion, his holy mountain, not for my own sake, but to rule the people according to God’s law, and so he says, preaching his commandment. Mystice autem constitutus est rex, secundum illud Hier. 23: regnabit rex, et sapiens erit et cetera. In the mystical sense, he was appointed king according to the following principle: a king shall reign, and shall be wise, and shall execute judgment and justice in the earth (Jer 23:5). Sion, id est ecclesiam Judaeorum, quae dicitur mons sanctus, quia prius recepit radios solis: Matth. 14: non sum missus nisi ad oves et cetera. 2 Reg. 19: an ignoro me hodie regem factum super Israel? Zion indicates the assembly of the Jews, which is called the holy mountain since it received the rays of the sun first. I was not sent except to the lost sheep of the house of Israel (Matt 15:24). Do not I know that this day I am made king over Israel? (2 Sam 19:22). Praedicans praeceptum, id est Evangelium. Vel illud speciale praeceptum de quo dicitur Joan. 13: mandatum novum do vobis, ut diligatis invicem; et ejusdem 15: hoc est praeceptum meum et cetera. Hoc autem praeceptum personaliter praedicavit Judaeis, in persona scilicet propria: Matth. 4: circuibat Jesus totam Galilaeam docens in synagogis eorum, et praedicans evangelium regni. Rom. 15: dico autem Christum Jesum ministrum fuisse circumcisionis propter veritatem Dei et cetera. Preaching his commandment means the Gospel, or the special commandment which is mentioned in John 13:34, a new commandment I give to you: love one another, and John 15:12, this is my commandment, that you love one another. Now, he preached this commandment to the Jews personally, that is, in his own person. Jesus went about all Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, and preaching the Gospel of the kingdom (Matt 4:23). Now I say that Christ Jesus was minister of the circumcision for the truth of God (Rom 15:8). 8. Deinde cum dicit, dominus, ex eadem historia ostenditur, quomodo se habet ad gentes: 8. Afterwards, when he says, the Lord, the same historical account shows how it relates to the gentiles. et circa hoc duo facit. Primo ostendit quod Christo convenit habere potestatem super gentes. Regarding this, he first shows that it is appropriate for Christ to have power over the gentiles; Secundo ponit usum potestatis, reges eos. second, he mentions the use of this power: you shall rule them. Circa primum duo facit. Primo ostendit quo jure potestas sibi competit super gentes. Regarding the appropriateness of Christ’s having power over the gentiles, he first shows by what right Christ holds power over the gentiles; Secundo ponit acceptionem ipsius potestatis, ibi, dabo tibi gentes. second, he shows the reception of this power: I will give you the gentiles. Dicit ergo, dominus dixit ad me. Hoc non usquequaque completur de David; et ideo intelligitur de Christo cui competit dominium super gentes duplici jure: scilicet hereditario, et meritorio. Therefore, he says, the Lord said to me. This is not completely fulfilled in David, and so it is understood about Christ, to whom dominion over the gentiles belongs by a double right: heredity and merit. Primo ergo ponit jus. First, he describes the right; Secundo meritum, ibi, postula et cetera. second, he describes the merit: ask of me, and I will give. Est autem Christus rex universorum, sicut dicitur Hebr. 1: et hoc competit ei, quia Filius: Gal. 4: si filius, et heres per Deum: et ideo agit de aeterna generatione Christi: in qua tria notantur. Christ is the king of all things, as Hebrews 1 notes, and this is fitting to him because he is the Son. If a son, an heir also, through God (Gal 4:7). And so this passage treats of the eternal generation of Christ, in which three things should be noted. Primo modus generationis. First, the mode of generation; Secundo proprietas filiationis. second, the nature of his sonship; Tertio aeternitas Filii generati. third, the eternity of the Son who is generated. Modus ostenditur in hoc quod dicit: dominus dixit: quia scilicet processit per modum intellectus. Uniuscujusque generatio est per modum ejus. Modus divinae naturae non est carnalis, sed intellectualis, immo est ipsum intelligere. He indicates the mode where he says, the Lord said, since the Son proceeds from the Father through an intellectual mode. The generation of any entity occurs through that entity’s mode. The mode of the divine nature is not carnal, but intellectual; indeed, it is the very act of intellection. Secundo generatio est processio secundum originem quae invenitur in re intelligibili, quae est secundum conceptionem verbi procedens ab intellectu; et hoc est dicere verbum in corde; et ideo dicit, dominus dixit, quasi, dicendo me generavit. Unde Filius est Verbum quod Pater dixit, id est gignendo produxit. Second, this generation is a procession according to the origin which is found in intelligible realities, which is like the conception of the word proceeding from the intellect. And this is to speak a word in the heart, and so he says, the Lord said, as though, by speaking, he generated me. So the Son is the Word which the Father spoke, that is, which he produced by begetting. Proprietas vero ostenditur in hoc, quod dicit, filius meus, non adoptivus, sicut illi, de quibus dicitur Joan. 1: dedit eis potestatem filios Dei fieri et cetera; sed proprietate naturae. Unde Filius meus es tu naturalis, singularis, consubstantialis: Matth. 17: hic est Filius meus dilectus. Having treated the mode of generation, he shows the nature of his sonship where he says, my son—not adopted, as those about whom it is said, he gave them power to be made the sons of God (John 1:12), but by nature. So, you are my natural, only, and consubstantial son. This is my beloved son (Matt 17:5). Aeternitas ponitur in hoc, quod subjungit: ego hodie genui te, id est aeternaliter: non enim est nova, sed aeterna generatio; et ideo dicit: hodie genui te: quia hodie praesentiam signat, et quod aeternum est, semper est. Dicit etiam, genui te, et non genero, ad designandum generationis perfectionem: cum enim generatio sit sine motu, simul est generari et generatum esse. Dicit etiam hodie, ut designet praesentialitatem cum claritate quae conveniunt Christo, qui et lucem inhabitat inaccessibilem, 1 Tim. 6; et qui vere est, in quo nihil est praeteritum, vel futurum, vel obscurum, sed clarum. Finally, he expresses the Son’s eternity when he adds, this day I have begotten you, that is, eternally. For it is not a new but an eternal generation, and so he says, this day I have begotten you because this day signifies the present, and what is eternal always is. He says, I have begotten you, and not “I will have begotten you” in order to express the perfection of the generation; for when generation is without motion, to generate and to be generated are simultaneous. He says, this day, to express the presentness and clarity appropriate to Christ, who both inhabits inaccessible light (1 Tim 6:16) and who truly is, in whom nothing is past or future or obscure, but clear. 9. Supra positum est privilegium aeternae generationis, ex quo Christo competit dominium gentium jure hereditario; hic ostendit, quomodo acquisivit per suum meritum. Ubi considerandum est: quod sicut in naturalibus formae infunduntur secundum dispositionem materiae; ita Deus gratuita dona largitur: Philip. 2: Deus est qui operatur in nobis velle et perficere et cetera; et ideo vult ut recipiamus dona petendo et orando: hoc exemplum voluit ostendere per Christum, quia voluit quod peteret, quod sibi jure hereditario competebat. 9. Above was described the privilege of eternal generation, by which dominion over the gentiles belongs to Christ by hereditary right; here he shows how he came to possess it by merit. Here it should be considered that just as forms are infused into natural things according to the disposition of the matter, in the same way does God grant his free gifts: it is God who works in you, both to will and to accomplish (Phil 2:13). And so he wills that we receive gifts by asking and praying. He wanted to give us the example of this in Christ, since he willed him to ask for what belonged to him by hereditary right. Haec autem postulatio pro gentibus vocandis potest intelligi dupliciter. Now, this petition for the calling of the gentiles can be understood in two ways. Primo per orationem, quia pro eis oravit: Joan. 17: non pro eis rogo tantum, sed pro eis qui credituri sunt per verbum eorum in me. First as a prayer, because he prayed for them. Not for them only do I pray, but for those also who through their word shall believe in me (John 17:20). Item per passionem: Hebr. 9: ut morte intercedente in redemptionem earum praevaricationum quae erant sub priori testamento, repromissionem recipiant, qui vocati sunt aeternae hereditatis: quae quidem postulatio non fuit vacua, quia in omnibus exauditus est pro sua reverentia Heb. 6. Unde subditur concessio, cum subditur, et dabo tibi gentes. This petition can also be understood as the Passion: that by means of his death, for the redemption of those transgressions, which were under the former testament, they that are called may receive the promise of eternal inheritance (Heb 9:15). And this petition was not in vain, since in all things he was heard for his reverence (Heb 5:7). So the next section follows, I will give you the gentiles. Ubi notandum est, quod ad Christum nullus venit nisi dono Patris: Joan. 6: nemo potest venire ad me nisi Pater qui misit me traxerit eum. Datio autem gentilium est pure donum: nam Judaei quasi redditi sunt, quia ante dati erant: Rom. 15: dico Jesum Christum ministrum fuisse circumcisionis et cetera: et ideo dicit: dabo tibi gentes, ut scilicet subjiciantur tibi, et sint tua hereditas: Phil. 2: ut in nomine Jesu omne genuflectatur, caelestium, terrestrium et infernorum: Ps. 15: hereditas mea praeclara est mihi. Here it should be noted that no one comes to Christ except by the gift of the Father: no one can come to me, unless the Father who sent me draw him (John 6:44). The giving of the gentiles is a pure gift, for the Jews were, in a sense, returned, since they had been given already. I say that Christ Jesus was minister of the circumcision (Rom 15:8). And so he says, I will give you the gentiles so that they will be subject to you, and shall be your inheritance. That in the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those in heaven, on earth, and under the earth (Phil 2:10). For my inheritance is goodly to me (Ps 15:6). Item non habet eas sicut ministri habent, ut Petrus vel Paulus, sed sicut dominus: Heb. 3: et Moyses quidem fidelis erat in tota domo ejus tamquam famulus, in testimonium eorum quae dicenda erant: Christus vero tamquam Filius in domo sua, quae domus sumus nos. Et ideo dicit, possessionem tuam: Isa. 49: ut possideres haereditates dissipatas, ut diceres his qui vincti sunt, exite, et his qui sunt in tenebris, revelamini: terminos terrae: quia per totum mundum aedificata est Ecclesia. Sed postmodum per Nicolaum haereticum, et Mahumetum ad infidelitatem redierunt. He possesses them not as ministers like Peter and Paul do, but as the Lord. And Moses indeed was faithful in all his house as a servant, for a testimony of those things which were to be said, but Christ as the Son in his own house, which house we are (Heb 3:5–6). And so he says, for your possession. That you might possess the inheritances that were destroyed, that you might say to those who are bound, “Come forth,” and to those who are in darkness “Show yourselves . . . to the ends of the earth” (Isa 49:8–9; 40:28): since the Church has been built up throughout the whole world. But afterwards they returned to unbelief because of Nicholas the heretic and Mohammed. Vel expectatur fundanda: Isa. 49: parum est, ut sis mihi servus ad suscitandas tribus Jacob, et faeces Israel convertendas. Dedi te in lucem gentium, ut sis salus mea usque ad extremum terrae: Heb. 1: quem constituit heredem universorum et cetera. Alternatively, this means that its foundation is still in the future. It is a small thing for you to be my servant to raise up the tribes of Jacob, and to convert the dregs of Israel. Behold, I have given you to be the light of the gentiles, that you may be my salvation even to the farthest part of the earth (Isa 49:6). Whom he has appointed heir of all things (Heb 1:2). 10. Deinde cum dicit, reges, ponitur executio potestatis: et secundum historiam posset exponi, quia erat constitutus rex Judaeorum, ideo et dominabatur gentibus aliquibus quas subjugaverat in figura universalis dominii Christi. Sed quia aliter reguntur cives, nam cives reguntur regimine misericordiae, aliter hostes subjugati, scilicet regimine severae justitiae; ideo dicit: in virga ferrea. 10. Afterwards, when he says, you shall rule, he describes the exercise of power; and it can be explained historically, since he was appointed king of the Jews, and he also ruled the other nations which he subjugated, as a figure of the universal rule of Christ. But because citizens are ruled in one way (for citizens are ruled with the rule of mercy), and subjugated enemies in another way (that is, the rule of severe justice), he says, with a rod of iron. Sed melius est ut referatur ad dominium spirituale Christi: necesse est enim quod qui regit, habeat virgam: Ps. 44: virga directionis, virga regni tui. Ad hoc enim necessarii sunt reges, ut virgam habeant disciplinae qua puniant delinquentes. Et quia Christus constitutus est rex a Deo ad populum regendum, ideo dicit: reges eos in virga ferrea. Et addit, ferrea, ad designandum inflexibilem justitiae disciplinam. Virga namque qua regebantur Judaei, non fuit ferrea, quia frequenter excusserunt se adorando idola. Sed haec est virga ferrea qua regit gentes, quia non recedent amplius a dominio Christi, quando plenitudo gentium intraverit: Apoc. 12: mulier peperit masculum, qui recturus erat omnes gentes in virga ferrea. But it is better that this be referred to the spiritual rule of Christ, for it is necessary that he who rules should have a scepter: the scepter of your kingdom is a scepter of uprightness (Ps 44:7). For this reason it is necessary that there be kings, since they have the scepter of discipline by which they punish those doing wrong. And since Christ is appointed king by God to rule the peoples, he says, you shall rule them with a rod of iron. And he adds iron to specify the unbending discipline of justice. For the rod by which the Jews were ruled was not iron; they frequently threw it off by worshipping idols. But the rod by which he rules the gentiles is a rod of iron, since once the multitude of gentiles has entered in, they will not again withdraw themselves from Christ’s dominion: the woman brought forth a man, who was to rule all nations with an iron rod (Rev 12:12). Et tamquam vas figuli confringes eos; quod exponitur per illud Hier. 18: descendi in domum figuli, et ecce ipse faciebat opus super rotam, et dissipatum est vas quod ipse faciebat e luto manibus suis. Conversusque fecit illud vas alterum. Et post, sicut lutum in manu figuli, sic vos in manu mea. Quando enim vas figuli est recens, frangitur de facili a mala forma, et restituitur in bonam. Judaei conversi erant, unde non erant confringendi: eadem enim est fides eorum et nostra. Gentiles autem erant idolatrae: et ideo erant confringendi, ut aliam formam acciperent, id est aliam fidem veram. And shall break them in pieces like a potter’s vessel, which is explained in Jeremiah: I went down into the potter’s house, and behold! he was doing a work on the wheel. And the vessel was broken which he was making of clay with his hands, and turning he made another vessel, as it seemed good in his eyes to make it (Jer 18:3–4). And afterward, behold as clay is in the hand of the potter, so are you in my hand (Jer 18:6). For when a potter’s vessel is newly made, if it is badly formed, it can be easily broken and restored to a good form. The Jews had been converted, so they did not need to be broken, for their faith is the same as ours. Now the gentiles were idolaters, and so they needed to be broken so that they might receive another form, that is, another—the true—faith. Vel aliter: in virga ferrea, bonos scilicet, et tamquam vas figuli, malos qui finaliter conterendi sunt: Luc. 2: ecce positus est hic in ruinam, et in resurrectionem multorum: Isa. 30: subito dum non speratur, veniet contritio ejus, et comminuentur sicut conteritur lagena figuli contritione pervalida et cetera ut sic, qui justus est, justificetur adhuc, et qui in sordibus est, sordescat adhuc. Apoc. ult. Or with a rod of iron could be understood in another way, that is, as referring to good people, and like a potter’s vessel as referring to evil people, who ultimately must be crushed. Behold this one is set for the fall, and for the resurrection of many (Luke 2:34). Its destruction shall come on a sudden, when it is not looked for, and it shall be broken small, as the potter’s vessel is broken all to pieces with mighty breaking (Isa 30:34–35). And so he who is just, let him be justified still, and he who is filthy, let him be filthy still (Rev 22:11). 11. Deinde cum dicit, et nunc, ostenditur quomodo se habeat ad reges. Reprimit autem eos admonendo et attrahendo ad servitutem Dei. 11. Afterwards, when he says, and now, he describes how he conducts himself in regard to kings.
