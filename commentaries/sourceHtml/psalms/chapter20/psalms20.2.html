Quantum ad primum dicit, desiderium cordis ejus tribuisti ei: Luc. 22: desiderio desideravi hoc Pascha manducare vobiscum; et dicitur de quolibet viro justo. In regard to the first, he says, you have given him his heart’s desire. With desire I have desired to eat this passover with you (Luke 22:15). This is said about any just man. Quantum ad secundum dicit, et voluntate labiorum ejus, id est quam expressit per labia: non fraudasti eum, quia exaudisti eum: Heb. 5: exauditus est pro sua reverentia: Is. 46: consilium meum stabit, et omnis voluntas mea implebitur. In regard to the second desire, he says, and the will of his lips, that is, what is expressed by the lips, you have not withheld from him, since you heard him. He was heard for his reverence (Heb 5:7). My counsel shall stand, and all my will shall be done (Isa 46:10). 168. Quoniam. Hic ponit in quibus impletur desiderium. 168. For you have preceded him. Here he describes the things by which desire is fulfilled. Et primo dicit de impletione desiderii cordis. First, he speaks about the fulfillment of the desire of the heart; Secundo de impletione desiderii orationis, quod idem est quod desiderium labiorum, ibi, vitam. second, about the fulfillment of the desire of the mouth, which is the same as the desire of the lips: he asked life of you. Est autem duplex beneficium; Now this involves a twofold favor. et ideo primo ponit beneficium spirituale interius, quod est beneficium gratiae: et quantum ad hoc dicit, quoniam praevenisti eum in benedictionibus dulcedinis. Benedicere est bonum dicere; et dicere Dei est ejus facere. Unde in benedictione Dei intelligitur infusio bonitatis: Gen. 26. Benedicam tibi, et multiplicabo semen tuum et cetera. Et 17: benedicam ei, et multiplicabo eum, et augebo eum valde. Haec ergo benedictio est dulcis: Sap. 12: o quam bonus et suavis est spiritus tuus in nobis. And so he first describes the interior spiritual favor, which is the gift of grace, and in regard to this he says, for you have preceded him with blessings of sweetness. To bless is to speak what is good, and for God, to speak is to act. So by the blessing of God should be understood the infusion of goodness. I will bless you and multiply your seed (Gen 22:17). I will bless him, and multiply him, and increase him exceedingly (Gen 17:20). Therefore, this blessing is sweet. O how good and sweet is your spirit in us (Wis 12:1). Ergo, in benedictionibus dulcedinis, id est in bonis gratiae, et in immunitate a peccatis, praevenisti eum, id est Christum, tempore: quia a principio conceptionis fuit plenus omni gratia: Jo. 1: vidimus eum plenum gratia et veritate, quasi unigenitum a Patre: quia quam cito conceptus fuit unitus divinae naturae, et tam cito fuit plenus gratia. Therefore, with blessings of sweetness, that is, with the goods of grace, and in freedom from sin, you have preceded him, that is, Christ, in time, since he was full of every grace from the beginning of his conception. We saw him . . . full of grace and truth, as the only begotten of the Father (John 1:14), since as soon as he was conceived he was united to the divine nature, and as just quickly he was full of grace. Item, praevenisti eum, quantitate: quia prae omnibus aliis gratiam accepit, et non ad mensuram. Sancti etiam dicuntur praeveniri; 1 Jo. 4: non quasi nos dilexerimus Deum, sed quoniam ipse prior dilexit nos: Sap. 6: praeoccupat eos qui se concupiscunt. Likewise, you have preceded him in quantity, since he received grace before all others and not according to a measure. For the saints are said to have been preceded. Not as though we had loved God, but because he has first loved us (1 John 4:10). She precedes those who covet her (Wis 6:14). Et sic in figura Christi David speciali gratia praeventus est: quia unctus fuit in regem, cum adhuc esset puer, antequam ipse cogitaret de regno. And so David, in the figure of Christ, preceded us by a special grace, since he was anointed as king while he was still a boy, before he himself had thought about the kingship. Beneficium exterius ostendit dicens, posuisti in capite ejus coronam de lapide pretioso. He shows the exterior favor, saying, you have set on his head a crown of precious stones. Si haec referuntur ad Christum, tunc est sensus. Posuisti in capite ejus coronam de lapide et cetera id est fecisti eum regem. Corona autem est signum regiae dignitatis: quia, ut dicitur Isa. 33: regem in decore suo videbunt: Cant. 3: egredimini filiae Sion, et videte regem in diademate, quo coronavit eum mater sua: id est divinitas. Propter hoc dicit, de lapide pretioso, id est de divinitate: quia regnum suum non est de hoc mundo, Jo. 18: Hieron. de auro obrizo, id est purissimo: Apoc. 6: data est ei corona. If this refers to Christ, this is the sense: you have set on his head a crown of precious stones, that is, you have made him king. Now a crown is a sign of royal dignity, since, as is said, they shall see the king in his beauty (Isa 33:17). Go forth daughters of Zion, and see the king . . . in the diadem, wherewith his mother crowned him (Song 3:11), that is, the divinity. Because of this, he says it is of precious stones, that is, of the divinity, since my kingdom is not of this world (John 18:36). Jerome has, of fine, that is, most pure, gold. There was a crown given him (Rev 6:2). Vel corona de lapide pretioso sunt apostoli: qui dicuntur lapides pretiosi propter pretiositatem doctrinae: Apoc. 12: in capite ejus scilicet Christi, corona stellarum duodecim. Eccl. 25: corona senum multa peritia. Unde coetus apostolorum est quasi corona Christi. Or, the crown of precious stones is the apostles, since they are called precious stones because of the preciousness of their doctrine. On his, namely Christ’s, head a crown of twelve stars (Rev 12:1). Much experience is the crown of old men (Sir 25:8). So the company of apostles is like the crown of Christ. Vel, posuisti in capite ejus coronam, id est Ecclesiam: Prov. 12: mulier, id est Ecclesia, diligens corona est viro suo id est Christo. Vel potest dici hoc de quolibet sancto: quia corona, sive merces, est ipse Deus: Isa. 28: in die illa erit dominus corona gloriae. De lapide, propter soliditatem, pretioso, quia David ad litteram ab ipso Deo coronatus est. Or, you have set on his head a crown, that is, the Church. A diligent woman, that is, the Church, is a crown to her husband (Prov 12:4), that is, Christ. Or this can be said about any saint, since the crown, or reward, is God himself. In that day the Lord of hosts shall be a crown of glory (Isa 28:5). It is of stones because of its solidity, which are precious since David was crowned by God himself according to the literal sense. Consequenter ostendit, quomodo impletur petitio labiorum ejus: unde dicit, vitam petiit a te, quando petiit a te resurrectionem: Ps. 40: tu autem domine miserere mei, et resuscita me, et retribuam eis. Et hoc datum est ei: Ps. 2: postula a me, et dabo tibi gentes hereditatem: Luc. 11: omnis qui petit accipit. Afterwards, he shows how the petition of his lips is fulfilled, so he says, he asked life of you when he asked for the resurrection from you. But you, O Lord, have mercy on me, and raise me up again and I will requite them (Ps 40:11). And this is given to him. Ask of me and I will give you the gentiles for your inheritance (Ps 2:9). Everyone who asks receives (Luke 11:10). Similiter etiam quilibet sanctus petit hoc, scilicet vitam, et maxime vitam aeternam: Ps. 26: unam petii a domino, hanc requiram et cetera et David similiter hoc petit. Similarly, any of the saints asks for this, namely life, and especially eternal life. One thing I have asked of the Lord, this will I seek after (Ps 26:4), and David also asks for this. Secundo ostenditur quomodo impletur petitio quoad alios; unde dicit, tribuisti ei et cetera. Afterwards he shows how the petition is fulfilled for others, so he says, you have given him length of days forever and ever. Petiit quod corpus ejus, scilicet Ecclesia, servetur in longitudinem dierum: et hoc ei tribuit Deus. Matth. ult. ecce ego vobiscum sum usque ad consummationem saeculi. Hoc etiam petebat David 2 Reg. 7: insuper locutus est de domo servi sui in longinquum. He asked that his body, the Church, be preserved for length of days, and God gave this to him. Behold I am with you all days, even to the consummation of the world (Matt 28:20). For David asked for this. In addition, you did speak of the house of your servant for a long time to come (2 Sam 7:19). 169. Magna. Hic ponitur consummatio bonorum: et circa hoc duo facit. 169. His glory is great in your salvation. Here the accomplishment of these goods is described, and he makes two points about this. Primo ponit magnificationem istius regis praeventi, in quo inchoata est benedictio. First, he describes the exaltation of this king who is foretold, in which the blessing is begun; Secundo exponit magnitudinem, ibi, gloriam et magnum decorem. second, he describes its greatness, at glory and great beauty. Dico quod praevenisti eum et cetera sed adhuc plus: quia, gloria ejus, scilicet Christi et hominis est magna in salutari tuo, id est in verbo cui unita est natura humana. I say that you have made him excel, but even more than this, since his glory, namely of Christ and of man, is great in your salvation, that is, in the Word to which human nature is united. Vel, magna est gloria ejus, apud homines: et hoc, in salutari tuo, quia suscitasti eum: Hebr. 5: in diebus carnis suae preces supplicationesque et cetera. Or, his glory is great among men, and in your salvation, since you have raised him up. In the days of his flesh, with a strong cry and tears . . . and supplications (Heb 5:7). Vel magna est gloria ejus in salutari tuo, id est in auxilio tuo: in quo institutum est, ut habeat magnam gloriam; Or, his glory is great in your salvation, that is, in your help, in which you established him so that he would have great glory. et hanc exponit quantum ad tria. He explains this in three ways. Primo per comparationem ad se ipsum; et ideo dicit, gloriam et magnum decorem imposuisti super eum, qui quidem decor impositus est super Christum in sua resurrectione, et gloria in aliorum admiratione. First, in relation to himself, and so he says, glory and great beauty you have laid upon him. This beauty was indeed laid upon Christ in his Resurrection, as was glory in the admiration of others. Vel ut accipiatur pro eodem, gloriam et magnum decorem imposuisti super eum: Job 40: circumda tibi decorem, et in sublime erigere, et esto gloriosus. Or, they can be taken as meaning the same thing, glory and great beauty you have laid upon him. Clothe yourself with beauty, and set yourself up on high and be glorious (Job 40:5). Secundo in comparatione ad alios; unde dicit, quoniam dabis eum in benedictionem in saeculum saeculi, quia per ipsum derivata est benedictio in omnes alios: Gen. 22: in semine tuo benedicentur omnes gentes. Second, he considers his glory in relation to others; so he says, for you shall give him to be a blessing forever and ever, since the blessing for all others is derived from him. In your seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed (Gen 22:18). Tertio in comparatione ad Deum; unde dicit, laetificabis eum in gaudio cum vultu tuo, id est laetificat Christum hominem laetitia sempiterna: et in vultu tuo, id est in visione vultus tui: Ps. 15: adimplebis me laetitia cum vultu tuo. Third, he considers it in relation to God, so he says, you shall make him joyful in gladness with your countenance, that is, you shall make Christ as man joyful with everlasting joy, and in your countenance, that is, in the vision of your countenance. You shall fill me with joy with your countenance (Ps 15:11). Vel gloriam imposuit super eum, quando sanctis suis dedit facere miracula: et hoc super Christum, quia totum ad gloriam Christi factum est, scilicet quod benedixit eis, et quod perduxit eos, ad visionem suam: Isa. 66: videbitis, et gaudebit cor vestrum: Joan. 16: gaudium vestrum nemo tollet a vobis. Or he laid glory upon him when he gave his saints the ability to work miracles, and this is upon Christ since the whole Christ is brought to glory, because he blessed them and because he led them to his vision. You shall see and your heart shall rejoice (Isa 66:14). Your joy no man shall take from you (John 16:22). 170. Quoniam. Supra psalmista nuntiavit regis Christi exaltationem; hic autem meritum hujus declarat: 170. For the king hopes in the Lord. Above, the psalmist announced the exaltation of Christ the king; now here he declares his worthiness of this. et hoc meritum est, quia Christus secundum quod homo, et quilibet justus non ponit nisi in Deo spem suam: Hiere. 17: benedictus vir qui sperat in domino, et erit dominus fiducia ejus. Et ideo quia speravit in domino, non commovebitur: Ps. 124: qui confidunt in domino et cetera. Quia spes in Deo est firmamentum: 1 Mach. 2: omnes qui sperant in eo, non infirmantur. Ideo haec comparatur anchorae. Now this was merited by Christ, insofar as he is man, and no just man places his hope in anyone except God. Blessed be the man who hopes in the Lord, and the Lord shall be his confidence (Jer 17:7). And so, because he has hoped in the Lord he shall not be moved. Those who trust in the Lord shall be like Mount Zion (Ps 124:1) because hope in God is the foundation. None who trust in him fail in strength (1 Macc 2:61). And so it is compared to an anchor. Sed notandum, quod tria sunt quae debent movere ad sperandum in domino. But it should be noted that there are three things which should move someone to hope in the Lord. Primo divina providentia. Homo non consuevit sperare in illis ad quos sui cura non pertinet. Ad Deum autem pertinet cura nostra; ideo dicit, sperat in domino, cujus est gubernare. First is divine providence. A man does not tend to hope in regard to those things which do not pertain to his care; however, our care does pertain to God, so he says, he hopes in the Lord, to whom governance belongs.
