      Item fuit etiam in sanctis: Isa. 66: super quem respiciam nisi ad pauperculum et contritum spiritu, et trementem sermones meos? Likewise, it was in the saints. Upon whom shall I look, but upon him who is poor and little and of a contrite spirit and who trembles at my words? (Isa 66:2). Aloes est succus herbae; sed non sumitur hic sic, sed pro quodam ligno, et vocatur aloes, quod est odoriferum. The aloe is a succulent herb, but it is not taken here in that way, but to mean a certain wood called aloes, which means “fragrant.” Stactes est gumma myrrhae, quod est speciosius quam myrrha. Et haec idem est quod myrrha quantum ad odorem. Stactes is the gum of myrrh, which is more precious than myrrh. And it has the same fragrance as myrrh. Casia est triplex. Quaedam est casia quae est sicut fistula; quaedam est fructus arboris, et de hac non intendit hic, quia non est aromatica; sed est quaedam casia virga quae est aromatica, et ad istam refertur quod dicitur hic. Cassia can be three things. There is cassia which is like a pipe, and one which is the fruit of a tree, and neither of these is meant here, since they are not aromatic, but there is a certain cassia branch which is aromatic, and this is referred to and meant here. Vel secundum glossam est quaedam arbor casiata, quae nascitur in locis aquaticis: et per hoc signatur aqua lacrymarum, vel aqua baptismalis; quasi dicat, odor omnium istorum procedit a sanctis, et corpore tuo: 2 Cor. 2: Christi bonus odor sumus. Or, according to the gloss, there is a certain cassia tree which grows in watery places, and it signifies the water of tears, or the water of baptism, as if to say, “The odor of all these things comes forth from the saints and from your body.” We are the good odor of Christ (2 Cor 2:15). Ex secundo dicit, a domibus eburneis; quasi dicat: redolet etiam a domibus tuis eburneis, quae redolent propter ista aromata. Antiqui parietes erant tabulati, apud nos sunt marmorei. Et sic apud Hebraeos et Orientales, parietes erant tecti ebore: Amos 3: peribunt domus eburneae. In regard to the second, he says, from the ivory houses, as if to say, “It wafts from your ivory houses, which are scented by these aromatic spices.” Ancient house walls were made of wood, although among us they are marble. And so among the Hebrews and those in the East, walls were built with ivory. The houses of ivory will perish (Amos 3:15). Domus signat fideles: 1 Pet. 2: et ipsi tamquam lapides vivi aedificamini in domos spirituales et cetera. Eburneae, frigidae propter castitatem: Cant. 5: venter illius eburneus. Item candidae propter puritatem, rubicundae propter castitatem: Thren. 4: rubicundiores ebore antiquo. The house signifies the faithful. Be you also as living stones built up in spiritual houses (1 Pet 2:5). Ivory signifies the coolness of chastity. His belly as of ivory (Song 5:14). So also, white is for purity and ruddiness for chastity. More ruddy than old ivory (Lam 4:7). Ex tertio, ex quibus delectaverunt te filiae regum in honore tuo; quasi dicat: ita est deliciosus quod filias regum habet in ministerio; quasi dicat: vestimenta tua paraverunt filias regum quae serviunt tibi. In regard to the third, out of which the daughters of kings have delighted you in your glory, as if to say, “It is delightful that he has the daughters of kings to minister to him.” As if to say, “Your garments prepared the daughters of kings who serve you.” Ad litteram. Filiae regum mundanorum delectant nos ad honorem Christi, quia dedicaverunt se Christo, et mortuae sunt pro Christo, et hoc, in honore tuo, id est ad tuum honorem: quasi scilicet non solum unius regis, sed etiam filiae regum serviunt Christo. In the literal sense, the daughters of earthly kings delight us to Christ’s glory, since they dedicated themselves to Christ and died for Christ, and this is in your glory, meaning, for your honor, as if to say, the daughters not only of one king, but of many kings, serve Christ. Vel aliter. Reges sunt apostoli: filii eorum sunt animae fideles. Or the kings can be understood as the apostles, and their daughters as faithful souls. Vel reges sunt doctores: Apocal. 5: fecisti nos Deo nostro regnum, et sacerdotes. Filiae eorum sunt populus Christianus et fidelis: 1 Corinth. 4: in Christo Jesu per Evangelium ego vos genui: Or the kings are the doctors. You have made us to our God a kingdom of priests (Rev 5:10). Their daughters are the faithful Christian people. For in Christ Jesus, by the Gospel, I have begotten you (1 Cor 4:15). hae sunt filiae in honore Christi, non in honore regum, scilicet Petri et Pauli, sed Christi: 1 Cor. 1: nos praedicamus Christum crucifixum. Istae delectaverunt istis aromatibus. These daughters are in Christ’s glory, not in the glory of kings—namely, not of Peter and of Paul, but of Christ. We preach Christ crucified (1 Cor 1:23). These delight in his fragrances. 457. Astitit regina. Supra psalmista laudavit Christum ex gratiositate, ex virtute bellica, et ex deliciis; hic autem laudat eumdem ex sponsa: 457. The queen stood. Above, the psalmist praised Christ for his graciousness, his warlike strength, and his delights; now here, he praises him in his bride. quam describit ex quatuor: He describes her in four ways: scilicet quantum ad sponsi praesentiam, in regard to her presence to the groom, quantum ad dignitatem, her dignity, quantum ad gloriam, her glory, et quantum ad ornatum. and her ornaments. Sponsa Christi est Ecclesia; sponsae regis dicuntur reginae: Esther 2: ista est Esther ordinata regina. Et haec regina est Ecclesia: 2 Corinth. 11: despondi vos uni viro virginem castam et cetera. Dignitas ejus est, quod est regina. The spouse of Christ is the Church; the spouses of a king are called queens. For example, Esther was appointed queen (cf. Esth 2). And this queen is the Church. I have espoused you to one husband that I may present you as a chaste virgin to Christ (2 Cor 11:2). Her dignity is that she is a queen. Haec astitit semper inhaerens Deo et conjuncta. Unde angeli qui non mittuntur, dicuntur assistentes: Daniel. 7: millia millium et cetera. Ps. 5: mane astabo tibi et cetera. She stands, always clinging to God and joined to him. So the angels who are not sent as messengers are said to stand. Thousands of thousands ministered to him and ten thousand times a hundred thousand stood before him (Dan 7:10). In the morning I will stand before you (Ps 5:5). Dicit Gregorius: videt quidam per fidem, erigitur per spem, unitur per caritatem. Gregory says, one sees by faith, is roused up by hope, and is united by charity. Gloria istius reginae est praerogativa quam habet, quia, a dextris, id est in potioribus bonis. Unde et Filius, in quantum est in potioribus bonis Patris, secundum quod homo, dicitur esse a dextris. Marc. ult.: dominus quidem Jesus sedet a dextris Dei. Et haec meliora sunt; sed si comparentur spiritualia temporalibus, spiritualia sunt potiora. Et haec regina astitit in spiritualibus. Prov. 3: longitudo dierum in dextera ejus. The glory of this queen is the prerogative which she has, since she is on your right hand, that is, among the greater goods. So also the Son, as man, insofar as he is among the Father’s greater goods, is said to be on the right hand. The Lord Jesus sits at the right hand of God (Mark 16:19). And these are better, but if spiritual things are compared to temporal, the spiritual are better. And this queen stands among the spiritual goods. Length of days is in her right hand (Prov 3:16). Item si per dexteram significentur bona opera, haec sunt potiora quam peccata. Prov. 4: vias quae a dextris sunt, novit dominus. Et in his astitit haec regina. Likewise, if the right hand is signifying good works, it means that these are better than sins. The Lord knows the ways that are on the right hand (Prov 4:27). And the queen stands among these. Ornatus describitur cum dicit, in vestitu deaurato. Her ornaments are described when he says, in gilded clothing. Hieronymus non habet, circumdata varietate, nec Hebraei: sed Hieronymus habet, in diademate deaurato. In Hebraeo, in massa auri. Jerome does not have, surrounded with variety, nor does the Hebrew, but Jerome has, in a golden diadem. In Hebrew, it is, in a weight of gold. Et secundum nostram litteram est duplex vestimentum Ecclesiae. Our text is indicating that the Church has two kinds of vesture. Unum est doctrina duorum testamentorum. Prover. ult.: omnes domestici ejus vestiti sunt duplicibus. Et iste vestitus non est aurum solum, sed deauratus, quia est refulgens divina sapientia, qua haec doctrina est plena. One is the teaching of the two testaments. All her domestics are clothed with double garments (Prov 31:21). And this garment is not only of gold, but is gilded, since it is resplendent with the divine wisdom, with which this teaching is full. Tamen est, circumamicta varietate. Et potest referri quantum ad diversa genera linguarum, vel quantum ad profundiorem modum sapientiae. Nevertheless, she is surrounded with variety. And this can refer to the various types of languages, or to a more profound mode of wisdom. Alius vestitus est operatio virtuosa. Ps. 131: sacerdotes ejus et cetera. Per aurum autem significatur caritas. Genes. 2: aurum terrae illius optimum. Est enim caritas fulgida et rubea. Et ideo dicitur deauratus, quia informatus caritate. 1 Cor. ult. omnia opera vestra in caritate fiant. Another vesture is virtuous work. I will clothe her priests with salvation (Ps 131:16). Now, gold signifies charity. The gold of that land is very good (Gen 2:12). For charity is shining and ruddy. And so something informed by charity is said to be gilded. Let all your things be done in charity (1 Cor 16:14). Vel, circumamicta varietate, id est diversis virtutum operibus; quia alii fuerunt aurei per martyrium, alii rosei per gemitum poenitentiae. Colos. 3: induite vos sicut electi Dei sancti et dilecti viscera misericordiae et cetera. Or surrounded with variety means that she is surrounded with the various works of the virtues, since some of her faithful were of gold in martyrdom, others were rosy with the tears of penitence. Put on therefore, as the elect of God, holy and beloved, the bowels of mercy, benignity, humility, modesty, patience (Col 3:12). Et potest exponi totum hoc de beata virgine, quae regina et mater regis est, quae astat super omnes choros in vestitu deaurato, id est deaurata divinitate: non quod sit Deus, sed quia est mater Dei. And all of this can be applied to the Blessed Virgin, who is the queen and the mother of the king, who stands above all the choirs of angels in gilded vesture, that is, gilded with the divinity—not that she is God, but because she is the mother of God. 458. Audi filia. Hic commendatur sponsa quadrupliciter: 458. Hearken, O daughter. Here, the bride is praised in four ways: scilicet a decore, in regard to her demeanor; ab excellentia gloriae, ibi, omnis gloria. the excellence of her glory, at all the glory; A societate, ibi, adducentur. her companions, at virgins shall be brought; Et a prole, ibi, pro patribus tuis. and her offspring, at instead of your fathers.
