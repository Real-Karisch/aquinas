      Vel patres boni fuerunt prophetae. Eccl. 44: laudemus viros gloriosos, et parentes nostros. Et loco istorum nati sunt Ecclesiae filii qui funguntur dignitate illorum; et sic patet proles et origo sponsae. Or the good fathers were the prophets. Let us now praise men of renown, and our fathers (Sir 44:1). And in their place are born to the Church sons who equal their dignity, and so the spouse’s lineage and the offspring are clear. Sequitur dignitas, constitues eos principes super omnem terram, id est primo capientes, primum capies. Then, he treats their dignity: you shall make them princes over all the earth, that is, you will take first those who are themselves taking first. Isti ergo principes dicuntur, quia primi dona Spiritus Sancti acceperunt. Rom. 8: non solum autem illi, sed nos ipsi primitias spiritus habentes. Tempore prius, ceteris abundantius, dicit glossa. Therefore, those who received first the gifts of the Holy Spirit are called princes. Not only it, but ourselves also who have the first fruits of the Spirit (Rom 8:23). First in time, more abundantly than others, says the gloss. Unde sicut beatae virgini nulla mulier comparatur, ita nec aliquis sanctus potest apostolis comparari nec adaequari. So, just as no woman can be compared to the Blessed Virgin, so no other saint can be compared to, or can equal, the apostles. Item dicuntur principes, quia fuerunt et sunt Ecclesias gubernantes. Likewise, princes are those who were, and are, governing the churches. Item quia primi post Christum doctores nostri fuerunt. Ps. 67: praevenerunt principes et cetera. Isti sunt diligendi. Judges 5: cor vestrum diligit principes Israel. Likewise, those who were first after Christ were our teachers. Princes went before (Ps 67:26). They should be loved. My heart loves the princes of Israel (Jdg 5:9). Istos constitues principes, qui non sunt per se ipsos constituti, sed per Christum. Joan. 15: non vos me elegistis, sed ego elegi vos et cetera. You shall make them princes who are not constituted as such by themselves but by Christ. You have not chosen me, but I have chosen you (John 15:16). Item alii praelati constituuntur per summum pontificem. Hebr. 5: nemo assumat sibi honorem. Likewise, the other prelates are constituted as such by the high priest. No one takes this honor to himself (Heb 5:4). Et non in aliqua parte, sed super omnem terram. Ps. 18: in omnem terram exivit sonus eorum et cetera. Et nimis honorati sunt et cetera. Ps. 138. Et hoc specialiter convenit Petro et Paulo; nam Petrus obtinuit principatum universalem Ecclesiae. Joan. 21: pasce oves meas. Paulus super totum mundum, quantum ad gentes gentiles. Isa. 49: posui te in lucem gentium, ut sis salus mea usque ad extremum terrae. Et hoc etiam dicit ipse, Act. 13. And they are not over just one part of the earth but over all of it. Their sound has gone forth into all the earth (cf. Ps 18:5). And they are made exceedingly honorable (Ps 138:17). And this especially befits Peter and Paul, for Peter received authority over the whole Church—feed my sheep (John 21:17)—and Paul over the whole earth, in regard to the gentile nations. I have set you to be the light of the gentiles, that you may be my salvation even to the farthest part of the earth (Isa 49:6). And he says this himself in Acts 13:47. Memores erunt nominis tui, domine. Hic ponitur officium apostolorum, quod est praedicare nomen Dei. Marc. ult.: euntes in mundum universum, praedicate Evangelium omni creaturae; et ideo dicit, memores erunt, id est facient habere memoriam. Nominis tui in omni generatione. They shall remember your name, O Lord. Here, the office of the apostles is described, which is to preach the name of God. Go into the whole world and preach the Gospel to every creature (Mark 16:15). And so he says, they shall remember, that is, they shall make others to remember, your name throughout all generations. Quantum ad locum, quia in omni parte mundi, et generatione, quantum ad tempus, quia caelum et terra transibunt et cetera. Marc. 13. Matth. ult.: ecce ego vobiscum sum et cetera. The place where this shall happen is every part of the world, and the era is throughout all generations, since heaven and earth shall pass away, but my word shall not pass away (Mark 13:31). Behold, I am with you all days, even to the consummation of the world (Matt 28:20). Propterea populi. Hic ponitur fructus, qui est, quia omnes populi confitebuntur tibi Christe: Therefore shall peoples praise. Here, he describes the fruit of the Church’s offspring, because all people shall confess you, Christ. et dicit, populi, quia non solum unus populus, sed omnes. Ps. 66: confiteantur tibi populi, Deus, confiteantur tibi populi omnes. Philip. 2: omnis lingua confiteatur, quia et cetera. And he says, peoples, since not one people alone, but all shall praise you. Let people confess to you, O God: let all people give praise to you (Ps 66:4). Every tongue should confess that the Lord Jesus Christ is in the glory of God the Father (Phil 2:11). Isti confitebuntur tibi in aeternum. In Graeco idem est aeternum quod saeculum, vel aevum; unde glossa, in aeternum, scilicet praesentis saeculi, et in saeculum saeculi, id est in futuro. Et haec memoria durabit in aeternum. Isa. 35: gaudium et laetitiam obtinebunt. They shall praise you forever. In Greek, “eternity” and “age” or “aeon” are the same, so the gloss says, forever, namely, in the present age, and forever and ever, that is, in the future. And this memory will endure forever. They shall obtain joy and gladness (Isa 35:10). Alia littera habet, memor ero nominis tui et cetera. Et haec est melior: et tunc refertur ad fructum apostolorum, et erit vox populi conversi; quasi dicat: dico quod constitues eos principes: et ego populus Christianus memor ero nominis tui et cetera: in quo designatur fides, quae est in corde; et post laus, quae est post fidem. Another text has, I will remember your name. And this is better, and then it refers to the fruit of the apostles, and the voice will be that of the converted people, as if to say, “I say that you will make them princes, and I, the Christian people, will remember your name for ever.” This signifies faith, which is in the heart, and afterwards praise, which comes after faith. Et hic psalmus cantatur in festo natalis domini propter sponsi laudem quae tangitur. And this psalm was sung on the feast of the birth of the Lord because of its praise of the bridegroom. Item cantatur in festis virginis, propter ejus laudem quae tangitur. Likewise, it is sung on the feasts of a virgin because of its praise of her. De primo, ibi, speciosus. It treats the first when it says, you are beautiful above the sons of men, De secundo, ibi, astitit regina. and the second when it says, the queen stood. Item cantatur in festivitatibus virginum, quia adducentur. It is also sung on the feasts of virgins because of they shall be led. Item in festis apostolorum, pro patribus tuis. Likewise, on the feasts of the apostles, because of instead of your fathers, sons. Super Psalmo 45 Psalm 45 Deus noster refugium et virtus God is our refuge and strength 45:1 In finem, filiis Core, pro arcanis. Psalmus. 45:2 Deus noster refugium et virtus; adjutor in tribulationibus quae invenerunt nos nimis. 45:3 Propterea non timebimus dum turbabitur terra, et transferentur montes in cor maris. 45:1 Unto the end, for the sons of Korah, for the hidden. 45:2 God is our refuge and strength: a helper in the troubles which have found us exceedingly. 45:3 Therefore, we will not fear when the earth shall be troubled, and the mountains shall be removed into the heart of the sea. 45:4 Sonuerunt, et turbatae sunt aquae eorum; conturbati sunt montes in fortitudine ejus. 45:4 Their waters roared and were troubled; the mountains were troubled with his strength. 45:5 Fluminis impetus laetificat civitatem Dei: sanctificavit tabernaculum suum Altissimus. 45:5 The stream of the river makes the city of God joyful; the Most High has sanctified his own tabernacle. 45:6 Deus in medio ejus, non commovebitur; adjuvabit eam Deus mane diluculo. 45:6 God is in the midst of it, it shall not be moved. God will help it in the morning early. 45:7 Conturbatae sunt gentes, et inclinata sunt regna: dedit vocem suam, mota est terra. 45:7 Nations were troubled, and kingdoms were bowed down; he uttered his voice, and the earth trembled. 45:8 Dominus virtutum nobiscum; susceptor noster Deus Jacob. 45:8 The Lord of armies is with us; the God of Jacob is our protector. 45:9 Venite, et videte opera Domini, quae posuit prodigia super terram, 45:10 auferens bella usque ad finem terrae. Arcum conteret, et confringet arma, et scuta comburet igni. 45:9 Come and behold the works of the Lord, what wonders he has done upon earth, 45:10 making wars to cease even to the end of the earth. He shall destroy the bow, and break the weapons: and the shield he shall burn in the fire. 45:11 Vacate, et videte quoniam ego sum Deus; exaltabor in gentibus, et exaltabor in terra. 45:12 Dominus virtutum nobiscum; susceptor noster Deus Jacob. 45:11 Be still and see that I am God; I will be exalted among the nations, and I will be exalted on the earth. 45:12 The Lord of armies is with us, the God of Jacob is our protector. 462. Postquam psalmista petiit divinum auxilium contra adversa quae patitur ab hostibus, cum dixit, Deus auribus, et ut jam exauditus ostendit gloriam regis, dicens, eructavit; hic autem exauditus pro populo, ostendit beneficium datum populo. 462. After the psalmist asks for the divine help against the trials which he is suffering from enemies, when he says, O God, with our ears (Ps 43:2), and having been heard, describes the king’s glory, saying, my heart has uttered (Ps 44:2), now here, having been heard on behalf of the people, he describes the favor given to the people. Et sicut per praecedentem psalmum gloria Christi designatur, ita in hoc psalmo designantur beneficia exhibita fidelibus Christi. And just as the preceding psalm described Christ’s glory, so this psalm describes the favors extended to Christ’s faithful. Unde in titulo hoc etiam designatur; et est duplex littera: in finem psalmus David pro arcanis. So the title, too, describes this, and it has a twofold text: unto the end, a psalm of David for the hidden. Arcana quae sunt a principio, sunt abscondita, scilicet quod Filius Dei factus est homo, quod Deus moriatur, quod gentes convertantur ad Christum. Ista fuerunt secretissima. Isa. 24: secretum meum mihi. Ephes. 3: aliis filiis hominum non est agnitum. Et haec sunt revelata per Christum. Matth. 13: eructabo abscondita. Job 28: abscondita produxit in lucem. The hidden things which were from the beginning are secrets, namely, that the Son of God would become man, that God would die, and that the nations would be converted to Christ. These were most secret. My secret to myself (Isa 24:16). Which was not known to the sons of men (Eph 3:5). And these were revealed through Christ. I will utter things hidden (Matt 13:35). Hidden things he has brought forth to light (Job 28:11). Hic ergo psalmus tendens in finem, id est in Christum, est David pro arcanis, id est ad arcanorum manifestationem. Therefore, the psalm here tending unto the end, that is, unto Christ, is of David for the hidden, that is, for the manifestation of what is hidden.
