      Et a prole, ibi, pro patribus tuis. and her offspring, at instead of your fathers. Circa primum duo facit. Primo ponit quomodo acquirat decorem seu gratiositatem. Regarding the first, he first describes how she acquired her demeanor or graciousness, Secundo agit de ipsa gratiositate, ibi, et concupiscet. second, he treats her graciousness alone: the king shall desire. Primo ergo redditur attenta ipsa ad admonitionem; unde dicit, audi filia. Therefore, first she is made attentive to advice, so he says, hearken, O daughter. Vocat futuram Ecclesiam filiam duplici ratione. He calls the future Church daughter for two reasons. Una est, quasi loquens David in persona sua: quia sicut in quantum adhaeremus Christo filio Abrahae, sumus filii Abrahae; ita et filii David cujus filius est Christus. One is that David is speaking as though in his own person, since, insofar as we cling to Christ, the son of Abraham, we are sons of Abraham, so they are also sons of David, whose son is Christ. Vel loquitur in persona apostolorum, ex quibus sumus propagati in Christo Jesu per Evangelium. Or it is spoken in the person of the apostles, from whom we are begotten in Christ Jesus through the Gospel. Dicit itaque: audi filia. Jacob. 1: sit omnis homo velox ad audiendum, scilicet Evangelium, vel verbum Christi. Luc. 11: beati qui audiunt verbum Dei. Vel Scripturam prophetarum, ut credant Christo. Isa. 53: quis credidit auditui nostro? And so he says, hearken, O daughter. Let every man be swift to hear (Jas 1:19), namely, the Gospel or the word of Christ—blessed are those who hear the word of God (Luke 11:28)—or the writings of the prophets, so that they might believe in Christ. Who has believed our report? (Isa 53:1). Et, vide, per fidem hic, sed in futuro videbis per speciem. 1 Cor. 13: videmus nunc et cetera. And see—here through faith, but in the future you will see through an infused vision. We see now through a glass (1 Cor 13:12). Vel vide Christum natum. Baruch 3: post haec in terris visus est. Joan. 1: vidimus eum plenum gratiae et veritatis. Or see Christ born. Afterwards he was seen upon earth (Bar 3:38). We saw him full of grace and truth (John 1:14). Inclina aurem tuam, scilicet per humilitatem ut obedias. Eccl. 6: si inclinaveris aurem tuam, excipies doctrinam. Incline your ear, by humility, to obey. If you incline your ear, you shall receive instruction (Sir 6:34). Obliviscere populum tuum. Monitio imaginaria, quod haec regina venerit ad David vel Salomonem ex populo alieno. Forget your people. In this imagined advice, this queen had come to David or Solomon from a foreign people. And so she is advised how to remember them. Et ideo monetur ut memor sit sui. Et hoc competit Ecclesiae, quae vocata est ad Christum ex alieno populo, vel Judaeorum, vel gentilium quia, secundum Augustinum: nemo potest venire ad vitam novam Christi, nisi poeniteat eum veteris vitae, scilicet peccati. And this is fitting for the Church, which was called to Christ from a foreign people, either that of the Jews or of the gentiles, since, according to Augustine, no one can come to the new life of Christ, unless he repent of his old life, namely, of sin. Et ideo dicit, obliviscere populum tuum. 1 Paral. 16: transierunt de gente in gentem, et de regno ad populum alterum. And so he says, forget your people. They passed from nation to nation and from one kingdom to another (1 Chron 16:20). Et domum patris tui, scilicet Diaboli. Joan. 8: vos ex patre Diabolo estis. Ezech. 16: pater tuus Amorrhaeus. Vel peccati, vel carnalis affectus. Gen. 41: oblivisci me fecit Deus omnium laborum meorum, et domus patris mei. And your father’s house, namely, the devil’s. You are from your father the devil (John 8:44). Your father was an Ammonite (Ezek 16:3). Or that of sin, or carnal affection. God has asked me to forget all my labors and my father’s house (Gen 41:51). Et hoc signat Deut. 21, de puella captivorum, quae debet radi, et debet plorare patrem et matrem sicut mortuum. And this is signified in Deuteronomy 21, where the captive woman must shave and mourn her father and mother as though they were dead (Deut 21:11–14). Et concupiscet rex decorem tuum. Hic promittit ipsi sponsae gratiositatem regis. And the king shall greatly desire your beauty. Here, he promises the favor of the king to the bride. Primo, a rege; et hoc est amoris. The first favor is from the king, and this is love. Secundo, a populo; et hoc est honoris, ibi, vultum tuum deprecabuntur. The second is from the people, and this is honor, at they shall entreat your countenance. Repromittit ergo regis amorem, et ostendit regis dignitatem. Dicit ergo: si oblivisceris populum tuum et domum patris tui, ex hoc acquires decorem spiritualem. Ps. 25: domine, dilexi decorem domus tuae. Therefore, he promises the love of the king and shows the dignity of the king. Therefore, he says, “If you forget your people and your father’s house, you will acquire spiritual beauty.” I have loved, O Lord, the beauty of your house (Ps 25:8). Et hic decor concupiscitur a sponso spirituali: propterea dicit, et concupiscet rex decorem tuum. Et hic decor est pulchritudo justitiae. Jer. 31: benedicat tibi dominus pulchritudo justitiae. Concupiscet, id est delectabitur in eo. Is. 62: quia complacuit domino in te. And this beauty is desired by the spiritual bridegroom; therefore, he says, and the king shall greatly desire your beauty. And this beauty is the loveliness of justice. The Lord bless you, the beauty of justice (Jer 31:23). He will desire, that is, he will delight in it. The Lord has been well pleased in you (Isa 62:4). Et hoc est desiderandum, quia iste rex est magnus in potestate, in natura, in honore. And that the king delight in you is to be desired, since this king is great in power, in nature, and in honor. Primum habet quia rex, ideo est dominus. Ps. 99: scitote quoniam ipse est dominus. He possesses the first because he is king; therefore, he is the Lord. Know that the Lord, he is God (Ps 99:3). Secundum, quia ipse est Deus. Ps. 94: quoniam Deus magnus dominus. He possesses the second because he is God. For the Lord is a great God (Ps 94:3). Tertium, quia adorabunt eum scilicet remoti, et omnes populi totius mundi. Ps. 85: omnes gentes quascumque fecisti, venient, et adorabunt coram te domine. Soph. 2: adorabunt eum omnes viri de loco suo, omnes insulae gentium. He possesses the third because those far away, and all the peoples of the whole world, shall adore him. All the nations you have made shall come and adore before you, O Lord (Ps 85:9). They shall adore him every man from his own place, all the lands of the gentiles (Zeph 2:11). Item adorabunt eum propinqui, quia filiae Tyri in muneribus, quia scilicet Tyrus est vicina terrae promissionis. Likewise, those nearby shall adore him, since there will be the daughters of Tyre with gifts, since Tyre is near the promised land. Unde, filiae, id est habitatores terrae illius, vultum tuum deprecabuntur, id est subdentur tibi cum muneribus: quia hoc impletum est quando mulier Chananaea egressa a finibus Tyri, venit ad Jesum; Matth. 15: Therefore, the daughters, that is, the inhabitants of that land, shall entreat your countenance, that is, they will be placed under you with their gifts. This was fulfilled when a woman of Canaan, who came out of the coasts of Tyre came to Jesus (Matt 15:22). vel, vultum tuum deprecabuntur omnes divites plebis, id est viri qui sunt in Tyro. Or, the rich among the people means the men who are in Tyre. Tyrus interpretatur angustia; unde omnes qui sunt in angustia, deprecabuntur te. Tyre means “anguish,” so all who are in anguish shall entreat you. Ad litteram omnes veniebant ad Christum. Lucae 4: cum sol autem occidisset, omnes qui habebant infirmos variis languoribus et cetera. Isa. 26: domine, in angustia requisierunt te, in tribulatione murmuris doctrina tua eis. In the literal sense, all came to Christ. And when the sun was down, all those who had any sick with diverse diseases brought them to him (Luke 4:40). Lord, they have sought after you in distress; in the tribulation of murmuring your instruction was with them (Isa 26:16). Et offerent munera, id est se ipsos. Vel eleemosynas. Isa. 19: vota vovebunt domino, et solvent. And they shall offer gifts, that is, themselves. Or alms. They shall all make vows to the Lord and fulfill them (Isa 19:21). 459. Omnis gloria. Supra psalmista commendavit sponsam a decore; hic autem commendat eam quantum ad gloriam et intrinsecus et extrinsecus. 459. All the glory of the king’s daughter is within. Above, the psalmist praised the spouse for her beauty, now here he praises her for her glory, both inward and outward. Primum facit cum dicit, omnis gloria ejus. He does the first when he says, all her glory. Ly ejus superfluit; unde construitur expositive ejus, scilicet, filiae regis ab intus: et hoc tripliciter. The word her is superfluous, so it should be construed as explanatory to mean, of the daughter of the king interiorly, and this can be understood in three ways. Primo, quia in interiori conscientia, non in exteriori fama hominum, sicut est fama peccatorum. 2 Cor. 1: gloria nostra haec est testimonium conscientiae nostrae. First, because this glory is in regard to the interior conscience, not in exterior fame among men, as is sinners’ fame. For our glory is this, the testimony of our conscience (2 Cor 1:12). Secundo in interiori justitia, non in exteriori observantia, sicut in veteri testamento. Rom. 2: circumcisio cordis, non carnis; in spiritu, non in littera; cujus laus non est ex hominibus, sed ex Deo. Second, this glory consists in interior justice, not in exterior observance, as in the Old Testament. The circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit, not in the letter; whose praise is not of men, but of God (Rom 2:29).
