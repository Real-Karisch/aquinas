      Primo quantum ad carnem; first, in regard to the flesh; secundo quantum ad animam; unde dicit, et refloruit. second, in regard to the soul, so he says, and my flesh has flourished again. Juventus hominis comparatur flori: quia sicut flos est praesagium fructus, ita juventus hominis est praesagium vitae futurae. Psalm. 89: mane sicut herba transeat. Tunc ergo caro reflorescit, quando antiqua juvenescit; quoniam homo in tristitia videtur senescere, in laetitia videtur juvenescere; sed tamen caro Christi floruit flore honestatis et incorruptionis. Man’s youth is compared to a flower, since just as a flower is a sign of fruit to come, so the youth of a man is a sign of his future life. In the morning he shall grow up like grass (Ps 89:6). Therefore, flesh shall then flourish again when the old will be rejuvenated. For a man seems to grow old in sadness, but in joy he seems to become young again. Nevertheless in Christ it flourished like a noble and incorruptible flower. In primo homine floruit caro ejus per innocentiae puritatem, sed peccando inquinata est. Sed in Christo refloruit in resurrectione: fuit enim de spiritu sancto sine peccato concepta. Isa. 11: flos de radice ejus ascendet. In the first man, his flesh flourished through the purity of innocence, but it was soiled by sin. But in Christ it flourished again in the Resurrection, for it was conceived without sin by the Holy Spirit. A flower shall rise up out of his root (Isa 11:1). Item in primo statu natura humana floruit, quia erat incorruptibilis: sed per peccatum est subjecta corruptioni: Rom. 8: corpus per peccatum mortuum est. Sed Christus refloruit in resurrectione: Cant. 1: et lectulus noster floridus est. Likewise, in the first state, human nature flourished because it was incorruptible, but through sin it became subject to corruption. The body is dead through sin (Rom 8:10). But Christ flourished again in the Resurrection. And our bed is flourishing (Song 1:15). Quantum ad animam dicit, et ex voluntate mea confitebor tibi. Anima quamdiu est in peccato, habet voluntatem a Deo aversam, et ad temporalia conversam; sed quando per conversionem reparatur, tunc convertitur ad Deum voluntas ejus, et confitetur Deum laudando ipsum. In regard to the soul, it is said, and with my will I will give praise to him. As long as the soul is in sin, it is against the will of God and turned to temporal things, but when it is repaired through conversion, then the will is converted to God and confesses God by praising him. Vel si dicatur hoc de Christo, refertur ad membra sua, scilicet fideles: Psalm. 53: voluntarie sacrificabo tibi et confitebor nomini tuo, domine, quoniam bonum est. Or if this is said of Christ, it refers to his members, the faithful. I will freely sacrifice to you, and will give praise, O God, to your name because it is good (Ps 53:8). 254. Dominus. Hic agit gratias pro beneficiis populi. 254. The Lord is the strength of his people. Here he gives thanks for the favors of the people. Et primo ponit beneficia jam exhibita. First, he describes the favors already presented; Secundo petit ea continuari, ibi, salvum fac populum tuum. second, he asks that they be continued, at save, O Lord, your people. Eadem beneficia quae sunt sibi facta cum dicit, dominus adjutor meus et cetera These are the same favors which had been done for him when he says, the Lord is my helper. dicit facta esse populo cum dicit, dominus fortitudo, populi Judaeorum per me liberati a Philistaeis. Et Christus est fortitudo populi ejus liberati per eum a peccato: Isa. 12: fortitudo mea dominus, et factus est mihi in salutem: Psalm. 143: protector meus, et in ipso speravi. He says they were done for the people when he says, the Lord is the strength of his people. The people of the Jews were delivered through me from the Philistines. And Christ is the strength of his people who are delivered by him from sin. The Lord is my strength . . . and he has become my salvation (Isa 12:2). My protector and I have hoped in him (Ps 143:2). Et hic est protector salvationum, id est protegit eos qui salvati sunt per Christum: Act. 4: non est aliud nomen sub caelo, in quo oporteat nos salvos fieri. Christus ergo protegit nos in praesenti, ut non cadamus in peccatum; et ideo, salvationum, id est praedestinatorum ad salutem proteget in futuro, liberando ab omni malo: Apoc. 7: non esurient, neque sitient amplius, neque cadet super illos sol, neque ullus aestus et cetera. And here he is the protector of the salvation, that is, he protects those who were saved through Christ. There is no other name under heaven . . . in which we may be saved (Acts 4:12). Therefore, Christ protects us in the present so that we may not fall into sin, and so he is the salvation, that is, of those predestined to salvation whom he will protect in the future, delivering them from every evil. They shall no more hunger nor thirst, neither shall the sun fall on them, nor any heat (Rev 7:16). 255. Salvum fac. Hic ostendit continuationem beneficiorum sive salutis. 255. Save, O Lord, your people. Here he shows the endurance of the favors or of salvation. Et primo ponit salutem. First, he describes the salvation; Secundo modum, ibi, benedic. second, its way, at bless. Dicit ergo, domine, tu qui es protector meus: salvum fac populum tuum, id est rogo ut ad salutem eos perducas: Joan. 17: non rogo ut tollas eos de mundo, sed ut serves a malo: Isa. 45: salvatus est Israel in domino salute aeterna. Therefore, he says, O Lord, you are my protector. Save your people, that is, I ask that you lead them to salvation. I pray not that you should take them out of the world, but that you should keep them from evil (John 17:15). Israel is saved by the Lord with an eternal salvation (Isa 45:17). Consequenter ponit modum perveniendi ad salutem: ad quam ut perveniatur, requiruntur tria: Afterwards, he describes the way of coming to salvation, to reach which three things are required: scilicet donum gratiae, namely, the gift of grace, gubernatio, governance, et spiritualis profectus. and spiritual progress. Quantum ad primum dicit. Et benedic hereditati tuae, id est confirma dona ejus. Prov. 10: benedictio domini super caput justi. Et dicit hereditati, quia electi sunt haereditas: Psalm. 32: beata gens cujus est et cetera. In regard to grace, he says, and bless your inheritance, that is, confirm his gifts. The blessing of the Lord is upon the head of the just (Prov 10:6). And he says, inheritance, because the elect are an inheritance. Blessed is the nation whose God is the Lord (Ps 32:12). Quantum ad secundum dicit, et rege eos, quia etiam cum gratia necessaria est gubernatio: Pro. 11: ubi non est gubernator, populus corruet. In regard to governance, he says, rule them, since even with grace governance is necessary. Where there is no governor, the people shall fall (Prov 11:14). Quantum ad tertium dicit, et extolle eos, id est promove per profectum, usque in aeternum. In regard to spiritual progress, he says, and exalt them, that is, advance them through progress forward, forever. Duobus modis potest intelligi. This can be understood in two ways. Primo in intellectu, quando elevatur ad manifestam cognitionem veritatis: Isa. 52: ecce intelliget servus meus, et exaltabitur. First, it is in the intellect, when it is elevated to the clear knowledge of the truth. Behold my servant shall understand, he shall be exalted (Isa 52:13). Secundo in affectu, quando non cupit carnalia, sed spiritualia: desiderium habens dissolvi et esse cum Christo, Philip. 1. Quoniam removetur ab hac vita ad vitam gloriae: Isa. 58: sustollam te super altitudinem nubium, et cibabo te et cetera. Second, it is in the affections, when he does not desire carnal things but spiritual. Having a desire to be dissolved and to be with Christ (Phil 1:23). For he is removed from this life to the life of glory. I will lift you up above the high place of the clouds and I will feed you (Isa 58:14). Afferte Domino, filii Dei, 28:1a A psalm for David, at the finishing of the tabernacle. Bring to the Lord, O you children of God, 28:1b afferte Domino filios arietum. 28:1b bring to the Lord the offspring of rams. 28:2 Afferte Domino gloriam et honorem; afferte Domino gloriam nomini ejus; adorate Dominum in atrio sancto ejus. 28:2 Bring to the Lord glory and honor; bring to the Lord glory to his name; adore the Lord in his holy court. 28:3 Vox Domini super aquas; Deus majestatis intonuit: Dominus super aquas multas. 28:4 Vox Domini in virtute; vox Domini in magnificentia. 28:3 The voice of the Lord is upon the waters; the God of majesty has thundered; the Lord is upon many waters. 28:4 The voice of the Lord is in power; the voice of the Lord in magnificence. 28:5 Vox Domini confringentis cedros, et confringet Dominus cedros Libani: 28:5 The voice of the Lord breaks the cedars: yea, the Lord shall break the cedars of Lebanon. 28:6 et comminuet eas, tamquam vitulum Libani, et dilectus quemadmodum filius unicornium. 28:6 And shall reduce them to pieces, as a calf of Lebanon, and as the beloved son of unicorns. 28:7 Vox Domini intercidentis flammam ignis; 28:8 vox Domini concutientis desertum: et commovebit Dominus desertum Cades. 28:9 Vox Domini praeparantis cervos: et revelabit condensa, et in templo ejus omnes dicent gloriam. 28:7 The voice of the Lord divides the flame of fire; 28:8 the voice of the Lord shakes the desert, and the Lord shall shake the desert of Kadesh. 28:9 The voice of the Lord prepares the stags, and he will discover the thick woods, and in his temple all shall speak his glory.
