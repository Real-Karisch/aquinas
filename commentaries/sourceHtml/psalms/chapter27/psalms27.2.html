      In interioribus: quia Deus spiritus est, et eos qui adorant, oportet spirituales esse. Joan. 4: veri adoratores adorabunt Patrem in spiritu et veritate. Et ideo dicit: exaudi deprecationem meam dum oro ad te. It consists in interior things, since God is a spirit, and those who adore him should be spiritual. The true adorers shall adore the Father in spirit and in truth (John 4:23). And so he says, hear, O Lord, the voice of my supplication when I pray to you. Sed debemus revereri etiam in nostris effectibus, et signis exterioribus; unde dicit, dum extollo manus meas ad templum sanctum tuum. But we should be reverent in our performance and exterior signs, so he says, when I lift up my hands to your holy temple. Hoc dupliciter legitur: This is read in two ways. vel ad litteram secundum glossam, quod Judaeis erat praeceptum, ut ubicumque essent, ad illius partem orarent ubi scirent Jerusalem esse. Either according to the literal sense, according to the gloss: the Jews had the precept that wherever they were, they would pray towards the place where they knew Jerusalem was. Vel, dum extollo, id est levo manus meas ad templum, id est ad caelum. Psalm. 10: dominus in templo sancto suo et cetera. Ergo non solum in devotione cordis orabo, sed etiam in signis exterioribus versus caelum orabo, et aliquam devotionem ostendam. Or, when I lift up, that is, I raise, my hands to your temple, that is, towards heaven. The Lord is in his holy temple (Ps 10:5). Therefore, I will pray not only in the devotion of my heart; I will also pray in exterior signs towards heaven, and I will show a certain devotion. Vel, dum manus meas, id est opera mea, extollo ad templum, id est dirigo ad Deum. Ps. 140: dirigatur oratio mea, id est opera mea, sicut incensum et cetera. Or, when I lift up my hands, that is, my works, to the temple, that is, I direct them towards God. Let my prayer, that is, my works, be directed as incense (Ps 140:2). De Christo autem potest exponi: quia ipse levavit manus suas versus templum, quia ipsum non reprobavit, sed approbando expulit vendentes et ementes in eo. It can also be explained about Christ, since he lifted up his hands towards the temple in that he did not condemn it, but he approved it by expelling those selling and buying in it. Vel extulit manus in cruce: Isa. 65: tota die expandi manus meas ad populum non credentem sed contradicentem mihi. Ad templum, id est Ecclesiam construendam per passionem suam. Or, he extended his hands on the Cross. All the day long have I spread my hands to a people who believes not, but contradicts me (Isa 65:2; Rom 10:21). To the temple, that is, the Church, built by his Passion. 249. Ne simul tradas me cum peccatoribus, et cum operantibus iniquitatem. Hic ponit petitionem ut non simul commoveatur in punitione cum impiis: 249. Hand me not over together with the wicked and with the workers of iniquity. Here he puts forward the petition that he not be shaken in the same punishment with the impious. et circa hoc facit duo. He makes two points about this. Primo describit culpam; First, he describes their guilt; secundo ponit poenam, ibi, da illis. and second, he describes their punishment, at give them according to their works. Quantum ad primum, culpam ostendit dicens peccata illorum, scilicet malum propositum et malum actum, In regard to the first, he shows their guilt, mentioning their sins—that is, their evil intention and evil action. dico exaudi vocem meam et cetera et hoc est, dum oro, quod liberer a perditione peccatorum: I say, hear, O Lord, my voice, and do this when I pray, that I may be delivered from the destruction of sinners. quia licet in vita justi sint simul cum peccatoribus, tamen in judicio non erunt simul. Matth. 25: segregabit bonos a malis. Ps. 42: discerne causam meam de gente non sancta et cetera. Gen. 18: numquid perdes justum cum impio? Although the just are often together with sinners during life, nevertheless they will not be with them at the judgment. He separated the good from the evil (cf. Matt 25). Distinguish my cause from the nation that is not holy (Ps 42:1). Will you destroy the just with the wicked? (Gen 18:23). Et ideo dicit, ne simul tradas me, in damnationem, scilicet, cum peccatoribus. Peccatores proprie sunt qui ex habitu peccant, vel peccandi propositum quantum ad actum et executionem peccati habent. And so he says, hand me not over, to damnation, together with the wicked. Properly, wicked sinners are those who sin out of habit or have the intention of sinning in regard to the act and the execution of the sin. Dupliciter quis exequitur actum peccati: Someone carries out the act of sinning in two ways: scilicet per manifestam injustitiam, through manifest injustice et per fraudem. Et hic est qui loquitur pacem quantum ad alios. and through deceit. And this is the one who speaks of peace to others. De primo dicit, cum operantibus iniquitatem ne perdas me. Illi dicuntur iniquitatem operari, qui manifeste faciunt injustitiam. Tamen non omne peccatum fit per injustitiam; specialiter tamen fit, in quantum non reddit Deo obedientiam. About those who do injustice he says, with the workers of iniquity destroy me not. Those are said to be workers of iniquity who openly do injustice. Although not every sin is specifically an act of injustice, every sin does involve injustice insofar as it does not render obedience to God. De secundo dicit, qui loquuntur pacem cum proximo suo. About those who carry out deceit, he says, who speak peace with their neighbor. Et ponit duo: And he describes two things about them. scilicet dulcia verba eorum in ore: Rom. ult.: qui per dulces sermones et benedictiones seducunt corda innocentium. Prov. 29: homo qui blandis fictisque sermonibus loquitur amico suo, rete expandit gressibus suis. Sweet words are in their mouth. By pleasing speeches and good words, they seduce the hearts of the innocent (Rom 16:18). A man who speaks to his friend with flattering and dissembling words spreads a net for his feet (Prov 29:5). Aliud tamen habent in corde; unde sequitur, mala autem in cordibus eorum, scilicet sunt quae parant inimicis suis. Hier. 9: in ore suo pacem cum proximo suo loquitur, et occulte ponit ei insidias. They have something else in their heart; so it follows, but evils are in their hearts, those which they prepare for their enemies. With his mouth he speaks peace with his friend and secretly lies in wait for him (Jer 9:8). Haec omnia possunt referri ad Christum, qui in cruce cum sceleratis deputatus est, ut dicitur Isa. 53. Sed petit, ut non simul, id est propter eamdem causam tradatur, id est crucifigatur cum eis: quia passio sive damnatio illorum fuit propter peccatum proprium, passio autem Christi propter iniquitatem nostram. This can all be referred to Christ, who was considered to be among the criminals on the Cross (Isaiah 53). But he asks that he not be handed over with them—that is, crucified with them for the same cause—because their suffering or condemnation was because of their own sins, but the suffering of Christ because of our iniquity. Item, ne eodem fine tradas me cum peccatoribus iniquis, id est non secundum intentionem eorum qui intendebant quod nomen Christi delerent. Et hi sunt qui loquebantur pacem cum proximo suo: et in Christo quando tentabant ut caperent eum in sermone, sed, mala erant in cordibus eorum, id est mala intentio: quia ut caperent, id est ut reprehendere possent. Matth. 22. Likewise, do not hand me over unjustly to the same end with sinners, that is, not in accordance with the intention of those who intended the name of Christ to perish. And these are those who spoke peace to their neighbor, and to Christ when they tested him to ensnare him in speech, but evils were in their hearts, that is, evil intentions, to ensnare him so they could apprehend him (Matt 22). Sequitur: da illis secundum opera eorum: et secundum nequitiam adinventionum ipsorum. It follows: give them according to their works, and according to the wickedness of their inventions. 250. Secundum opera et cetera. Hic agit de tribulatione et poena eorum. 250. Give to them according to the works of their hands. Here he discusses their tribulation and punishment. Et primo ipsam poenam tangit; First, he touches upon the punishment itself; secundo poenae aequitatem, ibi, secundum opera eorum; second, the justice of the punishment, at according to their works; tertio ejus perpetuitatem, ibi, quoniam non intellexerunt. third, its endurance, at because they have not understood . . . you shall destroy them. Duplex culpa posita est. A double guilt is posited: Una exterior quae est oris, one exterior, which is of the mouth; alia interior quae est cordis. another interior, which is of the heart. Et ideo dicit quantum ad primum, da illis secundum opera eorum. Ps. 61: reddes unicuique juxta opera sua. Da, accipitur praenuntiative, id est dabis; vel conformando se justitiae divinae. Ps. 57: laetabitur justus cum viderit vindictam. Vel ut referatur hoc ad Christum. Tu domine Pater, da illis secundum opera eorum: quasi dicat: ex passione mea sequitur utilitas; sed, tu da illis, scilicet peccatoribus non secundum fructum, sed secundum eorum intentionem quae fuit mala. Joan. 3: erant autem eorum mala opera. And so he says in regard to the exterior guilt, give them according to their works. For you will render to every man according to his works (Ps 61:13). Give: take it as foretelling, that is, you will give. He says this either by conforming himself to the divine justice: the just shall rejoice when he shall see the revenge (Ps 57:11); or it can be referred to Christ. You, O Lord Father, give them according to their works, as if to say, “Something of value did come from my Passion, but you give them, namely, sinners, not according to its fruits but according to their intention, which was evil.” For their works were evil (John 3:19). Quantum ad secundum dicit, et secundum nequitiam adinventionum, ipsorum. Adinventiones sunt viae excogitatae ad nocendum et peccandum. Et hi gravius puniuntur a Deo, sed poena aequa eis debetur. In regard to the interior guilt, he says, according to the wickedness of their inventions. Inventions are their ways of thinking how to do evil and to sin. And these are punished gravely by God, but they should be given a just punishment.
