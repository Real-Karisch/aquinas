      Patres nostri annuntiaverunt nobis. Hic ostendit quod audivit ab illis qui voluerunt docere veritatem, quia patres sunt. Deut. 32: interroga patres tuos et cetera. Item qui potuerunt docere veritatem, quia antiqui. Job 12: in antiquis est sapientia. Our fathers have declared to us. Here, he demonstrates that he has listened to those who wished to teach the truth, because they are fathers. Ask your fathers and they will declare to you (Deut 32:7). Also, those who could teach the truth because they are old. In the ancient is wisdom (Job 12:12). Opus quod operatus es in diebus eorum. Hic ponit tempus in quo ista audivit. Quaedam beneficia sunt antiqua quae ipsi viderunt: unde dicit, et in diebus antiquis. The work you have wrought in their days. Here, he lays out the time in which he heard these things. Certain favors are ancient because the ancient ones saw them; whence he says, and in the days of old. Manus tua. Hic ponit ipsum beneficium. Your hand. Here, he lays out the favor itself. Secundo ponit beneficii ordinem, et beneficii causam. Second, he lays out the ordering of the favor and the cause of the favor. In beneficio consideramus opus, et fructum operis. In a favor we consider the work and the fruit of the work. Opus divinae virtutis est, quia expulit ante eos gentes; unde dicit, manus tua, id est potentia tua. Nam per membra in Deo signatur actio quam exercemus per ipsa. Gentes disperdidit, id est habitantes in terra promissionis destruxit etiam corporaliter. Ps. 58: disperge illos in virtute tua. The work of divine power is that he expelled the nations before them; this is why he says, your hand, that is, your power. For through God’s members is signified the action which we exercise through them. He destroyed the gentiles, that is, he physically destroyed those living in the promised land. Scatter them by your power (Ps 58:12). Fructus est quia ipsi successerunt eis. Job 34: conteret multos et innumerabiles, et stare faciet alios pro eis. Propterea dicit, et plantasti eos. Exod. 15: introduces eos et plantabis et cetera. Quod plantatur, radicatur ut faciat fructum. Isa. 5: expectavi ut faceret uvas, et fecit labruscas. The fruit is that they succeeded them. He shall break in pieces many and innumerable and shall make others stand in their stead (Job 34:24). So he says, and you planted them. You shall bring them in and plant them (Exod 15:17). Whatever is planted is rooted so that it may bear fruit. He looked for it to bring forth grapes, and it brought forth sour grapes (Isa 5:2). Afflixisti populos. Hic est modus et ordo beneficii: quia non statim fuit destructa, quia terra fuisset inculta, et bestiae multiplicatae; et ideo paulatim eos consumpsit; You afflicted the people. Here is the way and the ordering of the favor, since it was not immediately destroyed, since the earth would have been uncultivated and wild beasts many. And so he consumed them little by little. unde dicit, afflixisti populos, et per bella, et per bestias, et scrabrones. Josue 24: misi ante vos scrabrones. Sap. 12: misisti antecessores exercitus tui vespas. So he says, you afflicted the people, both through war and through beasts and hornets. I sent before you hornets (Josh 24:12). You sent wasps, forerunners of your host (Wis 12:8). Et repulisti eos, ita paulatim eos affligens. Ps. 77: ejecit a facie eorum gentes. And you cast them out, thus afflicting them little by little. He cast out the gentiles before them (Ps 77:54). Non enim in gladio suo possederunt terram. Hic ponitur causa beneficii. For they got not the possession of the land by their own sword. Here, he lays out the cause of the favor. Et primo excludit causam opinatam; And first, he excludes the supposed cause; secundo ponit veram causam. second, he puts forward the true cause. Causa opinata posset esse fortitudo populi, quod expulissent eos; et ideo removens hoc dicit, non in gladio suo et cetera. The supposed cause could be the strength of the people which expelled them; and so, setting aside this explanation, he says, not by their own sword. Duplex est fortitudo hominis: The strength of man is twofold: una est ex experientia armorum: et hanc excludit, non in gladio suo possederunt terram. Judith 7: filii Israel non confidunt et cetera. Et sequitur ibidem: vicerunt non cum gladio. one type is from his experience with arms, and he excludes this: they got not the possession of the land by their own sword. The children of Israel trust not in their spears (Jdt 7:8). And it follows afterwards that they did not defeat with the sword (cf. Jdt 7:9). Alia est virtus naturalis; et hanc excludit dicens, et brachium eorum non salvavit eos, id est potestas eorum. Ps. 32: non salvatur rex per multam virtutem. Deut. 32: ne dicerent: manus nostra excelsa, et non dominus fecit haec omnia. Another form of strength is natural power, and he excludes this by saying, neither did their own arm save them, that is, their own power. The king is not saved by a great army (Ps 32:16). Lest they should say, “Our mighty hand and not the Lord has done these things” (Deut 32:27). Causa vero est, quia a Deo factum est; unde dicit, sed dextera tua et cetera. The true cause is that it was done by God, so he says, but your right hand and your arm. Et ponit quatuor: tria ad executionem, quartum ad effectum pertinet. And he puts down four points. Three have to do with the execution, and the fourth with the effect. Si rex vult juvare aliquem, tria facit ei. If a king desires to help anyone, he does three things for him. Primo dat ei favorem; First, he gives him his favor; secundo impendit auxilium operis. second, he devotes help to his effort; Tertio dat consilium in dirigendo. third, he gives advice to direct it. Sic fecit eis Deus. God acted in this way for them. Primo enim fuit eis favorabilis, et dedit eis prosperitatem: et haec signatur in dextera, per quam signatur prosperitas. Exod. 15: manus dextera tua magnificata est. For first, he was favorable to them and gave them prosperity. And this is signified by the right hand, which signified prosperity. Your right hand is magnified (Exod 15:6). Item dedit eis auxilium: et hoc signatur in brachio. Luc. 1: fecit potentiam in brachio suo. Likewise, he gave them help, and this is signified in the arm. He has showed might in his arm (Luke 1:51). Tertio direxit eos, ibi, et illuminatio vultus tui, id est directe procedens a providentia tua. Third, he directed them: and the light of your countenance, that is, proceeding directly from your providence. Quartum pertinet ad effectum. Quoniam complacuisti in eis; quasi dicat: quare fecit nobis ista? Numquid propter merita nostra? Non. Deut. 9: ne dicas in corde tuo cum deleverit eos dominus Deus tuus in conspectu tuo: propter justitiam meam introduxit me dominus ut possiderem terram hanc. The fourth pertains to the effect. Because you were pleased with them, as if to say, “Why did he do this for us? Certainly not because of our own merits? No.” Say not in your heart, when the Lord your God shall have destroyed them in your sight, “For my justice the Lord has brought me in to possess this land” (Deut 9:4). Sed hoc fuit propter gratiam Dei. Quoniam complacuisti et cetera; id est placitum tibi fuit, o Deus, in eis. Matth. 11, quoniam sic placitum fuit ante te. But this came about because of the grace of God. Because you were pleased with them, that is, O God, your good favor was with them. For so it has seemed good in your sight (Matt 11:26). 441. Tu es ipse. Hic ponitur spes quam habet de majori beneficio, quae consurgit ex spe ipsius Dei. 441. You are yourself my king and my God. Here, he lays out the hope which he has for a greater favor, which arises from hope in God himself. Et ideo tria facit. And so he makes three points. Primo proponit causam spei; He first puts forward the cause of hope; secundo fiduciam, ibi, in te inimicos; second, his confidence, at through you we will push down our enemies; tertio gratiarum actionem, ibi, in Deo laudabimus. third, his thanksgiving, at in God shall we glory.
