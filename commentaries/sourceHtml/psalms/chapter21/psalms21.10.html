      Quantum ad tertium dicit, vivent corda eorum et cetera. Joan. 6: si quis manducaverit ex hoc pane, vivet in aeternum. Et ideo dicit, vivet; quasi dicat: quamvis corpore moriantur imitando passionem domini, tamen corde vivent: Ps. 68: quaerite dominum, et vivet anima vestra. In regard to life, he says, their hearts shall live. If any man eat of this bread, he shall live forever (John 6:52). And so he says, he shall live, as if to say, “Although their bodies die in imitation of the Passion of the Lord, nevertheless they shall live in regard to their heart.” Seek the Lord and your soul shall live (Ps 68:33). 198. Reminiscentur. Hic ponit effectus derivatos ad alios. 198. All the ends of the earth shall remember. Here he describes the effects poured out upon others: Et primo communiter ad omnes. first, to all generally; Secundo quantum ad carnales, ibi, manducaverunt. second, to the carnal, at all the fat ones of the earth have eaten; Tertio quantum ad spirituales, ibi, et anima mea. and third, to the spiritual, at and my soul. Est autem triplex effectus. Now there is a threefold effect. Primus est divina cognitio ad quam gentes per apostolos devenerunt. First is the divine knowledge which came to the nations through the apostles. Secundus est effectus ad conversionem ad Christum. Second is the effect of conversion to Christ. Tertius effectus manifestatur per operis perfectionem. Third is the effect manifested through perfect works. Quantum ad primum dicit, reminiscentur. Hominibus naturaliter inserta est quaedam Dei cognitio; sed obliviscuntur domini per peccatum: Deut. 32: oblitus es domini creatoris tui. Gentiles autem habuerant aliquam Dei cognitionem, sed obliti fuerant propter peccata; sed per apostolos fuerunt reducti ad reminiscentiam naturalis cognitionis: Jer. 51: recordamini domini procul. In regard to the divine knowledge, he says, they shall remember. A certain knowledge of God is naturally planted in men, but they forget the Lord through sin. You have forgotten the Lord who created you (Deut 32:18). Now the gentiles had some knowledge of God, but they had forgotten it through sin; but through the apostles they were led back to remember this natural knowledge. Remember the Lord afar off (Jer 51:50). Quantum ad secundum dicit, et revertentur ad dominum, scilicet per amorem. Et hoc fecerunt non solum Judaei, sed, universi fines terrae: Isa. 21: si quaeritis, quaerite; convertimini, et venite: et hi duo effectus etiam ad sacramentum altaris pertinent, quod est quoddam memoriale dominicae passionis, ut dicitur 1 Cor. 11. Ideo dicit, reminiscentur, quia conversio animae ad Deum est effectus sacramenti altaris: Ps. 22: super aquam refectionis educavit me. In regard to conversion, he says, and shall be returned to the Lord, namely, through love. And not only the Jews did this, but all the ends of the earth. If you seek: seek, return, come (Isa 21:12), and these two effects pertain to the sacrament of the altar which is a certain memorial of the Lord’s Passion, as is said in 1 Corinthians 11. And so he says, they shall remember, since conversion of the soul to the Lord is the effect of the sacrament of the altar. He has brought me up on the water of refreshment (Ps 22:2). Colentes eum non in ceremoniis, sed, in conspectu ejus, scilicet spirituali cultu: Joan. 4: veri adoratores adorabunt Patrem in spiritu et veritate. They will be worshipping him not in ceremonies, but in his sight, namely, with spiritual worship. The true adorers shall adore the Father in spirit and in truth (John 4:23). Et omnes familiae gentium: Soph. 2: adorabunt eum viri de loco suo, omnes insulae gentium; quasi, ut intelligatur quod gentiles coluerunt Deum Israel: et sicut proselyti euntes ad habitandum cum Judaeis, recesserunt a loco suo. And all the kindreds of the gentiles. They shall adore him, every man from his own place, all the islands of the gentiles (Zeph 2:11), so it should be understood that the gentiles worshipped the God of Israel, and as those who were converted, going to dwell with the Jews, left their own place. 199. Quoniam. Potestas spiritualis totius mundi est Christi: Dan. 7: et dedit ei potestatem et honorem et regnum et cetera. Et non solum Judaeorum, secundum illud, Isa. 9: super solium David; sed ipse dominabitur gentium: Ps. 2: postula a me, et dabo tibi gentes hereditatem. 199. For the kingdom is the Lord’s. Spiritual power over the whole world belongs to Christ. And he gave him power and glory and a kingdom (Dan 7:14). And it is not only over the Jews (according to Isaiah 9:7, upon the throne of David), but also, he shall have dominion over the nations: ask of me and I will give you the gentiles for your inheritance (Ps 2:8). 200. Manducaverunt. Hic ponitur effectus qui secutus est quantum ad carnales: et ponit duo. 200. All the fat ones of the earth have eaten. Here the effects which follow in regard to the carnal are described, and he describes two. Primo ponit bonum eorum. First, he describes their good. Secundo ponit eorum defectum, ibi, in conspectu ejus cadent, carnales: Second, he describes their lack: the carnal shall fall before him. et hi perceperunt duplex bonum: And these received a double good: scilicet participationem sacramenti, namely, participation in the sacrament, et venerationem Dei in cultu. and veneration of God in worship. Quantum ad primum dicit, manducaverunt, licet indigne, quia carnales sunt: 1 Cor. 15: caro et sanguis regnum Dei non possidebunt. In regard to the first, he says, they have eaten, although unworthily, since they are carnal. Flesh and blood cannot possess the kingdom of God (1 Cor 15:50). De bonis dixit supra quod saturabuntur et laudabunt dominum, et vivent, quia venerantur sacramentum per fidem quam habent. He said above about the good that they were filled and praised the Lord and shall live, since they venerated the sacrament through the faith which they have. Sed isti quia sunt pingues terrae, id est in terrenis defixi, id est non in spiritualibus elevati: cadent in conspectu ejus, scilicet Dei: Hier. 31: inebriabo animas sacerdotum pinguedine, et populus meus bonis meis adimplebitur. But these, because they are the fat ones of the earth, that is, attached to earthly things (that is, not elevated to spiritual things), shall fall before him, namely, God. I will fill the soul of the priests with fatness and my people shall be filled with my good things (Jer 31:14). Isti designantur per vaccas pingues, Amos 4. Deut. 32: incrassatus est dilectus, et recalcitravit: nam, qui indigne manducat et bibit, judicium sibi manducat et bibit, ut dicitur 1 Cor. 11: et bene dicit, omnes qui descendunt: id est si qui affectu demerguntur ad terrena: quia etsi isti videantur stare in conspectu hominum, tamen in conspectu Dei cadent: Isa. 8: offendent ex eis plurimi, et cadent. These are signified by fat cows (Amos 4:1). The beloved grew fat and kicked (Deut 32:15). For he who eats and drinks unworthily eats and drinks judgment to himself (1 Cor 11:29). And he says well, all who go down, that is, they are submerged in the earth through their affection, since if any think they stand before men, they nevertheless fall before God (cf. 1 Cor 10:12). Very many of them shall stumble and fall (Isa 8:15). Vel, cadent in conspectu ejus, id est substernentur ei etiam inimici, omnes qui descendunt in terram, id est in corruptionem peccati: Phil. 2: in nomine Jesu omne genu flectatur. Or, they shall fall before him, that is, even his enemies are subjected to him, all who go down to the earth, that is, in the corruption of sin. At the name of Jesus, every knee should bow (Phil 2:10). 201. Et anima. Hic ponitur effectus quoad spirituales. Anima Christi sunt illi in quibus requiescit Spiritus Sanctus, spirituales scilicet, qui dupliciter se habent ad Deum. 201. And to him my soul shall live. Here he describes the effects in the spiritual. The soul of Christ is those in whom the Holy Spirit rests, namely, the spiritual, who are ordered to God in a twofold way: Primo quantum ad cor, quia vivunt illi, scilicet Deo, vel Christo, Gal. 2: vivo ego et cetera. 2 Cor. 5: qui vivunt, jam non sibi ipsis vivant, sed ei qui pro ipsis mortuus est. first, in heart, since they live for him, namely God or Christ. I live, now not I, but Christ lives in me (Gal 2:20). They also who live may not now live to themselves, but unto him who died for them (2 Cor 5:15). Secundo quantum ad opus, et semen meum. Semen bonum sunt filii regni; quasi dicat: filii regni quos ego seminavi, servierunt soli Deo: quia omnia opera quae fecerunt, ad ejus gloriam retulerunt. Second, they are ordered to God in their work: and my seed. The good seed is the sons of the kingdom, as if to say, “I have sown those sons of the kingdom who served God alone,” since all the works which they did redounded to his glory. 202. Annunciabitur. Hic exponit quando fiet: quia non in tempore suo, sed in futuris temporibus: 202. There shall be declared to the Lord. Here he explains when this shall be, since it did not take place during his own time but will take place in future times. et ponit tria. He describes three things. Primo praenuntiat fidei praedicationem. First, he announces the preaching of the faith. Secundo a quibus debeat praedicari. Second, he says by whom it will be preached. Tertio quibus praedicetur. Third, he tells to whom it will be preached. Quantum ad primum dicit, annuntiabitur domino generatio ventura: In regard to the first, he says, there shall be declared to the Lord a generation to come. et hoc potest dupliciter exponi. This can be explained in two ways. Uno modo cum Hieronymo; quasi dicat, generatio ventura annuntiabitur, id est evangelizabitur. Et ad hoc, ut convertantur ad dominum, sive etiam annuntiabitur passive; quasi dicat, per praedicationem apostolorum illa generatio adducetur ad dominum: Matth. 11: pauperes evangelizantur. One way is according to Jerome, and it is as if he were to say, “There shall be declared, that is, evangelized, a generation to come.” This is so that they may be converted to the Lord. For shall be declared is passive, as if to say, “Through the preaching of the apostles this generation shall be brought to the Lord.” The poor have the Gospel preached to them (Matt 11:15).
