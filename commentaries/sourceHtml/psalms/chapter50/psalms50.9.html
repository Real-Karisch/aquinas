      Oculus David ferebatur ad duo: David’s eye was drawn towards two things: unum propinquum, quod erat figurale; one near, which signified; aliud remotum, quod erat figuratum. the other removed, which was what was signified. Primum quia legitur, quod David aedificavit muros civitatis Jerusalem, sed non consummaverat, et his consummatis aedificandum erat templum: et ideo dicit, fac in bona voluntate tua, ut aedificentur muri Jerusalem. Et tunc aedificatis muris, aedificabitur templum. Et tunc acceptabis sacrificium et cetera. Et hoc totum erat figurale. The former is that David built the walls of the city of Jerusalem, but he did not finish them, and when they were finished the temple would be built, and so he says, deal in your good will with Zion, that the walls of Jerusalem may be built up. And when you build up the walls, the temple will be built: then will you accept the sacrifice. And all this was signifying. Sed si referamus ad figuratum, sic dicendum est, quod est duplex Jerusalem: scilicet caelestis: Gal. 4: illa quae sursum est Jerusalem, libera est, quae est mater nostra. Alia est praesens Ecclesia exemplata ab illa. Apoc. 21: vidi civitatem sanctam Jerusalem et cetera. But if we refer to what is signified, then it must be said that there are two Jerusalems, one heavenly—that Jerusalem, which is above, is free, which is our mother (Gal 4:26)—and the other is the present Church modeled on the heavenly one: I saw the holy city Jerusalem coming down out of heaven from God (Rev 21:2). Et utraque habet muros. Muri caelestis Jerusalem sunt munimenta aeternitatis et immortalitatis, quam consecuti sunt sancti per Christum: Rom. 8: qui suscitavit Jesum Christum, vivificabit et mortalia corpora nostra. Muri praesentis Jerusalem, scilicet Ecclesiae, sunt sacramenta gratiae et doctores. Ezech. 13: non stetistis ex adverso, nec opposuistis murum pro domo Israel ut staretis in praelio. Aedificationem istorum praevidebat spiritu prophetiae, Isa. 56. And both cities have walls. The walls of the heavenly Jerusalem are the bastions of eternity and immortality, which the holy ones attained through Christ: he who raised Jesus Christ from the dead shall quicken also our mortal bodies (Rom 8:11). The walls of the present Jerusalem, the Church, are the sacraments of grace and the teachers: you have not set up a wall for the house of Israel, to stand in battle (Ezek 13:5). He provided for their building up by the spirit of prophecy (Isa 56). Ad hoc ergo quod aedificentur isti muri, tu domine fac benigne, id est ostende benignitatem hanc, quam benignitatem Apostolus Tit. 3, dicit impletam: apparuit benignitas et humanitas salvatoris nostri Dei. Et hoc non propter merita nostra, sed propter tuam voluntatem bonam. Rom. 12: ut probetis quae sit voluntas Dei bona, et beneplacens, et perfecta. 1 Thess. 4: haec est voluntas Dei et cetera. In order, then, that these walls be built up, deal favorably, O Lord, that is, show this favor, a favor the Apostle said had been fulfilled: the favor and kindness of God our Savior appeared (Titus 3:4). And this not because of our merits, but because of your good will: be reformed, that you may demonstrate what is the good, and the acceptable, and the perfect will of God (Rom 12:2). This is the will of God, your sanctification (1 Thess 4:3). Et hoc facias ad hoc, ut aedificentur muri Jerusalem, vel militantis, vel triumphantis. Sed quid erit? And you should do this that the walls of Jerusalem, either militant or triumphant, may be built up. But what will this Jerusalem be? Tunc acceptabis sacrificium justitiae. Hoc autem tripliciter exponitur: et duo primi modi pertinent ad Ecclesiam praesentem. Then you shall accept the sacrifice of justice. This has a triple meaning, and the first two pertain to the present Church. Uno modo, quod hoc referamus ad sacrificium, non quo trucidantur pecora, sed quo occiduntur homines propter Christum; et in hoc est duplex gradus: First, we apply this to the sacrifice not in which calves are slaughtered but in which men are killed for Christ’s sake; and in this there is a twofold hierarchy. quia primum locum tenet sacrificium Christi. Gal. 2: dilexit me, et tradidit semetipsum pro me. Et ideo, dicit, tunc, id est in aedificatione murorum Jerusalem, id est Ecclesiae, acceptabis sacrificium justitiae, quo Christus se obtulit, qui justus est. Joan. 8: quis ex vobis arguet me de peccato? First, because Christ’s sacrifice itself holds the first place: he loved me and delivered himself over for me (Gal 2:20). And this is why he says, then, that is, in the building up of the walls of Jerusalem, of the Church, you shall accept the sacrifice of justice, in which Christ, who is just, offered himself. Which of you shall convict me of sin? (John 8:46). Et quia tantae virtutis est, ut satisfaceret pro homine, ut justificaret eum. And because he is of such strength as to be able to satisfy for man, to justify him. Secundum locum tenent alii sancti, qui se propter Deum obtulerunt in sacrificium; unde dicit, oblationes, scilicet confessores, qui confitendo Christum, quantum in se est, obtulerunt se morti, licet non sint occisi. Judic. 5: qui sponte obtulistis de Israel animas vestras ad periculum, benedicite domino. Et holocausta. Isti sunt martyres. Joan. 15: majorem caritatem nemo habet et cetera. The other holy ones hold second place; they offered themselves as sacrifices for God’s sake, which is why he says, offerings, namely, the confessors, who, in confessing Christ as he is in himself, offered themselves to death, even though they were not killed. O you of Israel, who have willingly offered your lives to danger, bless the Lord (Judg 5:2). And holocausts: these are the martyrs. Greater love than this no man has, that a man lay down his life for his friends (Jn 15:13). Et tunc acceptabis, quando ista ipsi sancti imponent se tamquam vitulos super altare tuum, id est super fidem tuam, et super Christum, id est exponent se tamquam vitulos propter Christum, et fidem Christi ad immolandum. And then you shall accept, when the holy ones themselves lay themselves down like calves upon your altar, that is, upon your faith and upon Christ—that is, they lay themselves out like calves to be immolated for the sake of Christ and Christ’s faith. Alio modo, ut referatur quantum ad opera justorum: et sic est sensus: acceptabis justitiam sicut sacrificium, quia opera justitiae et misericordiae sunt sicut sacrificium. Heb. ult.: beneficentiae et communionis nolite oblivisci: talibus enim hostiis promeretur Deus. In another way, this could refer to the works of the just: “You will accept justice as a sacrifice,” because works of justice and mercy are like a sacrifice. Do not forget to do good and to impart, for by such sacrifices God’s favor is obtained (Heb 13:16). Et tunc accipies oblationes. Secundum Gregorium: holocaustum est, quod totum incendebatur: et signat perfectos viros, qui totaliter dant se Deo. Oblationes sunt illi qui aliquid offerunt et aliquid reservant, et tunc sacerdotes imponent vitulos, id est conversos de novo: super altare, id est super fidem Christi. And then you will accept offerings. Gregory says that a holocaust is what is completely burned up, and signifies perfect men, who give themselves totally to God. Offerings represent those who offer something and reserve something, and then priests shall lay calves, that is, new converts, upon the altar, that is, upon the faith of Christ. Vel tunc praelati majores imponent praedicatores mugientes doctrina fidei, super altare, id est super confessione fidei. Another interpretation is that higher prelates lay preachers “bellowing” the faith’s doctrines upon the altar, that is, upon the confession of faith. Tertia expositio est de caelesti Jerusalem: et est sensus, tunc, scilicet quando aedificabuntur muri caelestis Jerusalem, acceptabis sacrificium justitiae. A third exposition is about the heavenly Jerusalem, and the sense is that then, that is, when the walls of the heavenly Jerusalem are built, you shall accept the sacrifice of justice. Hic quandoque fit sacrificium poenitentiae; sed ibi est tantum laudis. Isa. 60: populus tuus, omnes justi in perpetuum hereditabunt terram. Et hoc est illud sacrificium laudis de quo Ps. 83: beati qui habitant in domo tua, in saecula saeculorum laudabunt te. Here on earth, that means sometimes the sacrifice of repentance, but there in heaven can only be the sacrifice of praise. Your people shall be all just, they shall inherit the land forever (Isa 60:12). And this is the sacrifice of praise about which it is said, blessed are those who dwell in your house, O Lord. They shall praise you for ever and ever (Ps 83:5). Et tunc oblationes, id est sanctos minores, et holocausta, id est sanctos majores, acceptabis. And then you shall accept offerings, that is, the lesser holy ones, and holocausts, that is, the great holy ones. Et hoc offerent angeli, quibus dicitur Matth. 13: triticum autem congregate in horreum meum. Et isti angeli, imponent sanctos, super altare tuum, id est in gloriam caelestem. Apoc. 8: data sunt ei incensa multa, ut darent de orationibus sanctorum omnium super altare aureum. Ps. 68: placebit Deo super vitulum novellum cornua producentem et ungulas. And the angels will offer this, to whom it is said in Matthew 13:30: but the wheat gather into my barn. And those angels shall lay the holy ones upon your altar, that is, in heavenly glory. There was given to him much incense, that he should offer the prayers of all the holy ones upon the golden altar (Rev 8:3). And it shall please God better than a young calf, that brings forth horns and hoofs (Ps 68:32). Super Psalmo 51 Psalm 51 Quid gloriaris in malitia Why do you glory in malice? 51:1 In finem. Intellectus David, 51:2 cum venit Doeg Idumaeus, et nuntiavit Sauli: Venit David in domum Abimelech. 51:3 Quid gloriaris in malitia, qui potens es in iniquitate? 51:4 Tota die injustitiam cogitavit lingua tua; sicut novacula acuta fecisti dolum. 51:1 Unto the end, understanding for David, 51:2 when Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, “David went to the house of Abimelech.” 51:3 Why do you glory in malice, you who are mighty in iniquity? 51:4 All the day long your tongue has devised injustice: as a sharp razor, you have wrought deceit. 51:5 Dilexisti malitiam super benignitatem; iniquitatem magis quam loqui aequitatem. 51:6 Dilexisti omnia verba praecipitationis; lingua dolosa. 51:5 You have loved malice more than goodness, and iniquity rather than to speak righteousness. 51:6 You have loved all the words of ruin, O deceitful tongue. 51:7 Propterea Deus destruet te in finem; evellet te, et emigrabit te de tabernaculo tuo, et radicem tuam de terra viventium. 51:7 Therefore will God destroy you forever: he will pluck you out, and remove you from your dwelling place, and your root out of the land of the living. 51:8 Videbunt justi, et timebunt; et super eum ridebunt, et dicent: 51:9 ‘Ecce homo qui non posuit Deum adjutorem suum; sed speravit in multitudine divitiarum suarum, et praevaluit in vanitate sua.’ 51:8 The just shall see and fear, and shall laugh at him, and say: 51:9 “Behold the man who made not God his helper, but trusted in the abundance of his riches, and prevailed in his vanity.” 51:10 Ego autem, sicut oliva fructifera in domo Dei; speravi in misericordia Dei, in aeternum et in saeculum saeculi. 51:11 Confitebor tibi in saeculum, quia fecisti; et exspectabo nomen tuum, quoniam bonum est in conspectu sanctorum tuorum. 51:10 But I, as a fruitful olive tree in the house of God, have hoped in the mercy of God forever, yea, forever and ever. 51:11 I will praise you forever, because you have done it: and I will wait on your name, for it is good in the sight of your saints. 510. Hic incipit secunda quinquagena. Et sicut dictum est in principio libri, prima quinquagena pertinet ad poenitentes; ista secunda quinquagena pertinet ad proficientes. 510. Here begins the second set of fifty psalms. And as was said in the beginning of the book, the first fifty pertain to penitents; this second fifty to the proficient. Circa quos sunt tria per ordinem videnda: About these, three things should be examined in order: nam primus gradus proficientium est justificatio: for the first stage of the proficient is justification; secundus gradus est exercitium bonorum operum: the second, the doing of good works; tertius est consideratio divinorum operum. the third, the consideration of divine works. Et sic ista quinquagena dividitur in tres partes. And thus this group of fifty is divided into three parts: Primo tangit de his quae pertinent ad justificationem; the first touches on what pertains to justification; secundo agit de his quae pertinent ad executionem bonorum operum, ibi, Deus judicium; the second is about what pertains to the doing of good works, at give to the king your judgment, O God (Ps 71:2);
