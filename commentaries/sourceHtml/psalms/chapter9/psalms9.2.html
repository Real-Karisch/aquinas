      Primus fuit occultus, quantum ad divinitatem et ejus gloriam, quae latebat in infirmitate carnis. Isa. 45: vere tu es Deus absconditus. The first was hidden in that his divinity and his glory were hidden in the weakness of his flesh. Truly you are a hidden God (Isa 45:15). Secundus erit manifestus. Luc. 21: videbunt Filium hominis venientem in nube cum potestate magna et majestate. The second will be manifest. They shall see the Son of Man coming in a cloud, with great power and majesty (Luke 21:27). Item est duplex Christi judicium. There are also two judgments of Christ. Unum occultum; et hoc est in dispositione mundi, secundum quod permittit bonos persecutionem pati a malis. Ps. 35: judicia tua abyssus multa. One is hidden, and this is in the disposition of the world in which he allows the good to suffer persecution from the evil. Your judgments are a great deep (Ps 35:7). Item aliud est manifestum in fine. 1 Cor. 4: quousque veniat dominus, qui et illuminabit abscondita tenebrarum, et manifestabit consilia cordium. Agitur enim de occulto judicio, secundum quod boni afflictionem patiuntur a malis. Job 30: expectabam bona, et ecce mala; praestolabar lucem et cetera. Et totum in idem redit: quia gratias agit de liberatione ab inimicis. Likewise the other will be manifest at the end. Until the Lord comes who will both bring to light the hidden things of darkness, and will make manifest the counsels of the hearts (1 Cor 4:5). This treats the hidden judgment according to which the good suffer affliction from the evil. I expected good things, and evils are come upon me: I waited for light, and darkness broke out (Job 30:26). And the whole is referred to here since he gives thanks for deliverance from his enemies. Dividitur autem iste psalmus in duas partes. This psalm is divided into two parts. Primo ponit gratiarum actionem; The first lays out thanksgiving; secundo materiam gratiarum actionum, ibi, in convertendo. the second, the reason for thanksgiving: when my enemy will be turned back. Gratias agit tripliciter: corde, ore et opere. Thanks is given in three ways: in heart, in words, and in deed. Ore dupliciter: laudando et praedicando. It is given in words in two ways: by praising and by preaching. Laudando, quia dicit, confitebor. By praising, since he says, I will confess to you. Est autem triplex confessio: Now, there are three kinds of confession: scilicet fidei: Rom. 10: confessio fit ad salutem. Ps. 31: dixi, confitebor et cetera. namely, that of faith—confession is made unto salvation (Rom 10:10). I said I will confess (Ps 31:5); Peccatorum. Jac. ult.: confitemini alterutrum peccata vestra. that of sins: confess your sins to one another (Jas 5:16); Laudis (Tob. 12): coram omnibus viventibus confitemini ei et cetera. and that of praise: give glory to him in the sight of all who live (Tob 12:6). Et de hac confessione agitur hic. O domine confitebor tibi, id est gratias agam, id est laudabo te. Ps. 68: laudabo nomen Dei mei et cetera. And this is the sort of confession referred to here. I will confess to you, O Lord, that is, I will give thanks, or I will praise you. I will praise the name of my God with a song (Ps 68:31). Haec in corde: quia 1 Reg. 16: homines vident quae apparent, Deus autem intuetur cor. This confession is in the heart: man sees those things that appear, but the Lord beholds the heart (1 Sam 16:7). Contra quod Isa. 29, et Marc. 7: populus hic labiis me honorat, cor autem eorum longe est a me. On the contrary: this people honors me with their lips but their heart is far from me (Mark 7:6; Isa 29:13). Sed dicit, in toto, quia Deus est major corde nostro. 1 Joan. 3. Et ideo, quantumcumque laudemus secundum posse nostrum, minus est ipso. Deut. 6: diliges dominum Deum tuum ex toto corde tuo. Qui ergo non totum cor dat Deo, sed aliquid aliud vult habere simul cum ipso, perdit eum. Isa. 28: angustatum est stratum, ita ut alter decidat: et pallium breve est, et utrumque operire non potest. Ille ergo toto corde laudat eum, qui nihil acceptat contra Deum, sed totum in eum actu vel habitu refert. Tob. 12: opera Dei revelare et confiteri (scilicet annuntiando) honorificum est. But he says, with my whole heart, since God is greater than our heart (1 John 3:20). And so, however much we praise him according to our ability, it is less than he himself is. You shall love the Lord your God with your whole heart (Deut 6:5). Therefore, he who does not give his whole heart to God, but desires to hold some other thing alongside him, loses him. For the bed is straightened, so that one must fall out, and a short covering cannot cover both (Isa 28:20). Therefore, he praises him with his whole heart who will accept nothing contrary to God but directs everything to him in act and habit. It is honorable to confess (that is, by proclaiming) the works of God (Tob 12:7). Narrabo, praedicando et annunciando, aliis, quia Deus dat beneficia aliis communicanda: et hoc bene fit aliis enuntiando. Luc. 8: vade et annuntia quanta fecerit tibi Deus: et ideo dicit, narrabo mirabilia tua. I will relate by praising and proclaiming to others because God gives favors to be communicated to others. Go and announce how great things God has done to you (Luke 8:39). And so he says, I will relate all your wonders. Sed rationem habet quod dicit, mirabilia, quia opera Dei mirabilia sunt. Ps. 76: tu es Deus, qui facis mirabilia. But there is a reason why he says wonders: because the works of God are wonders. You are the God who does wonders (Ps 76:15). Sed quid est quod dicit, omnia? Hoc videtur impossibile. Job 5: qui facit magna et inscrutabilia mirabilia et absque numero. Quomodo ergo dicit, omnia? But why does he say all? This seems impossible. Who does great things and unsearchable things and wonders without number (Job 5:9). Therefore, why does he say all? Dicendum, quod omnia secundum genus, vel omnia quaecumque narrat, omnia sunt mirabilia: vel omnia quia intentio narrantis non debet sistere in aliquo, sed procedere quantum potest. Hilarius in Lib. de Trin.: qui pie infinita prosequitur, etsi nunquam perveniet, proficiet in prodeundo. Eccl. 43: glorificantes Deum quantum potestis, supervalebit adhuc, et admirabilis majestas ejus: et 42: nonne fecit Deus sanctos suos enarrare mirabilia? It should be said that all means according to genus, either that all the things that are related are wonders, or all because the intention of the one relating should not stop at anything but go as far as it is able. Hilary in his book On the Trinity says, he who piously pursues infinite things, even if he never arrives, becomes expert in going forward. Glorify the Lord as much as you can, for he will yet far exceed, and his magnificence is wonderful; and, has not God made the saints to declare all his wonderful works? (Sir 43:32; 42:17). 58. Laetabor. Hic gratias agit quantum ad cor. 58. I will be glad. Here he gives thanks in the heart. Aliqui narrant aliis bona sua, et gaudent de se, sicut peccatores. Luc. 18, de Pharisaeo, qui dicebat: gratias tibi ago, quia non sum et cetera. Some, such as sinners, tell others about their good things and rejoice in themselves, like the Pharisee who said, O God, I give you thanks that I am not as the rest of men (Luke 18:11). Sed hic gaudet in Deo semper. Eccl. 35: in omni dato hilarem fac vultum tuum, et in exultatione sanctifica decimas tuas. But this speaker always rejoices in God. In every gift show a cheerful countenance and sanctify your tithes with joy (Sir 35:11). Laetabor, interius in corde, et exultabo in te, exiliens ad exteriorem laetitiam. Habac. 3: ego autem in domino gaudebo, et exultabo in Deo Jesu meo. I will be glad, interiorly in my heart, and rejoice in you, bursting forth into exterior praise. But I will rejoice in the Lord, and I will exult in God my Jesus (Hab 3:18). Psallam. Hic gratias agit quantum ad opus: psallere enim est opus manuale, et per hoc intelligitur bona operatio: quia omnia opera nostra ad gloriam Dei terminari debent. Matth. 5: sic luceat lux vestra et cetera. Ps. 145: psallam Deo meo, quamdiu fuero. I will sing. Here he gives thanks in deed, for to sing is manual labor, and through this a good work is understood, since all our works should end in the glory of God. So let your light shine before men, that they may see your good works and glorify your Father who is in heaven (Matt 5:16). I shall sing to my God as long as I shall be (Ps 145:2). Sed addit, nomini tuo, altissime; quasi dicat: Deus non proficit ex hoc quod confitebor et psallam tibi, quia nomen tuum altissimum est, sed nobis proficit. Ps. 34: oratio mea in sinu meo convertetur. Job 35: si juste egeris, quid donabis ei, aut quid de manu tua accipiet? Job 22: quid prodest Deo si justus fueris, aut quid ei conferes si immaculata fuerit via tua? But he adds, to your name, O Most High, as if to say, “God does not benefit from what I will confess and I will sing to you, since his name is Most High, but we benefit.” My prayer shall be turned into my bosom (Ps 34:13). And if you do justly, which shall you give him, or what shall he receive from your hand? (Job 35:7). What does it profit God if you are just? Or what do you give him if your way is unspotted? (Job 22:3). 59. In. Hic ponitur materia gratiarum actionis. 59. When my enemy. Here he describes the matter for thanksgiving: Primo in speciali pro se, first, in particular for himself; secundo in generali, convertantur. second, in general: the wicked shall be turned to hell. Circa primum tria facit. He makes three points about the first. Primo proponit factum; First, he describes what was done; secundo auctoritatem facientis laudat, ibi, dominus in aeternum permanet. second, he praises the authority of the one acting: the Lord remains forever; Tertio subdit fructum, ibi, sperent in te. third, he adds the fruits: let them trust in you.
