      Secunda contemptus Dei, ibi, dixit enim in corde suo. The second is contempt of God, at for he has said in his heart. Tertia praesumptio, ibi, non est Deus: The third is presumption, at God has forgotten. circa primum tria facit. He makes three points about this adulation. Primo ponit adulationem ejus. First, he describes his adulation; Secundo effectum. second, its effect; Tertio divinam clementiam. third, the divine mercy. Laudantur autem, secundum glossam, peccatores de duplici peccato: scilicet carnalis concupiscentiae quantum ad se ipsos, et injustitiae quantum ad proximum; ideo superbiunt. Now, according to the gloss, sinners are praised on account of two types of sin, namely, for carnal concupiscence among one another and for injustice against neighbors, and so they are proud. Sed secundum Hieronymum, quoniam laudatur, et ideo exacerbavit, in desideriis, quantum ad concupiscentiam, et iniquus, quantum ad injustitiam contra proximum. But according to Jerome: for he is praised, and so he has provoked by what he desires, in regard to concupiscence, and the unjust in regard to injustice against his neighbor. Littera Hieronymi habet, vir avarus. Alia littera, raptor: Isa. 5: vae qui dicunt bonum malum, quoniam ita est. Jerome’s text has, the avaricious man. Another text has, the plunderer. Woe to you who call good evil (Isa 5:20), for so it happens. Exacerbavit dominum peccator, id est ad iracundiam provocavit. The sinner has provoked the Lord, that is, he has stirred him up to wrath. Hieronymus habet totum hoc sub uno versu: et alius versus incipit, ibi, peccator secundum multitudinem et cetera. Potest autem legi secundum multitudinem irae suae non quaeret. Vel quia citra condignum punit, vel quia magna ira non punit modo, ut fortius puniat in futuro. Jerome has all of this as one sentence, and another begins at the sinner according to the multitude. But it is possible to read it, according to the multitude of his wrath he will not seek him, either because he punishes less than is deserved or because, in the greatness of his wrath, he does not punish right away, so that he may punish more severely in the future. Vel irae suae, scilicet peccatori, non quaeret, peccatum Deus. Or, according their wrath, namely that of the sinner, he, God, will not seek the sin. 72. Non. Supra posuit psalmista quasdam causas malitiae impiorum: quarum una erat permissiva, quia Deus recedebat longe; secunda inductiva, scilicet lingua blandiens: 72. God is not before his eyes. Above, the psalmist described some causes of the malice of the wicked, one of which was permissive, since God retired afar off, and the second was encouraging, namely, a flattering tongue. hic ponuntur aliae causae motivae, scilicet intrinsecae: quae sunt duae: Here he sets down other motivating causes, namely the intrinsic causes, which are two: scilicet Dei contemptus, namely, contempt of God, et propria praesumptio. Secunda, ibi, dixit enim in corde suo. and individual presumption. He talks about the second when he says, he has said in his heart. Circa primum duo facit. He makes two points about the first. Ponit primo, quod de Deo non recogitat. First, he notes down that he does not think about God. Secundo, quod ejus judicia non formidat, ibi, auferuntur. Second, that he does not fear his judgments: your judgments are removed. Circa primum duo facit. Regarding the first, he lays out two things: Quia primo ponit aversionem a Deo, sive ejus contemptum. first, his turning away from God, or his contempt of him, Secundo aversionis effectum, ibi, inquinatae. Dicit ergo, non est Deus in conspectu ejus. and, second, the effects of this ignoring: his ways are filthy. Hieronymus habet, in cogitationibus quia nihil de eo cogitat; et continuatur sic: non quaeret Deum peccator, quia non est in conspectu ejus, id est in intentione, vel cogitatione: Job 21: dixerunt Deo, recede a nobis: scientiam viarum tuarum nolumus. Therefore, he says, God is not before his eyes. Jerome has, in his thoughts, because he does not think about him at all, and he continues this thought: the sinner will not seek God, since he is not in his sight, that is, in his intention or thought. They have said to God, “Depart from us, we desire not the knowledge of your ways” (Job 21:14). Effectus est, quia inquinatae sunt, id est sordent omni tempore viae suae. Viae peccatoris sunt cogitationes, vel voluntates: Sap. 1: in malevolam animam non introibit sapientia, nec habitabit in corpore subdito peccatis. Et dicuntur inquinari per peccatum, vel ad similitudinem peccatorum praecedentis temporis. The effect is that his ways are filthy, that is, vile, at all times. The ways of the sinner are thoughts or desires. Wisdom will not enter into a malicious soul, nor dwell in a body subject to sins (Wis 1:4). And they are said to become filthy through sin or likeness to the sinners of earlier times. Hoc allegorice de Antichristo, moraliter de peccatoribus dici potest: quia eo ipso quod Deus non est in intentione eorum, convertunt se ad temporalia; ex quibus anima inquinatur, in quantum permiscetur eis quae sunt pejora quam anima. Sed anima permixta Deo, qui est anima melior, non inquinatur, sed clarificatur; Thren. 1: sordes ejus in pedibus ejus, nec recordata est finis sui: Ps. 34: viae illorum tenebrae et lubricum: Hier. 2: me dereliquerunt fontem aquae vivae, et foderunt sibi cisternas dissipatas quae continere non valent aquas. This can be said allegorically about the Antichrist but morally about sinners, since because God is not in their intention they turn to temporal things, in which the soul becomes filthy insofar as it is mixed up in those things which are lower than the soul. But the soul mingled with God, who is better than the soul, is not made filthy but purified. Her filthiness is on her feet, and she has not remembered her end (Lam 1:9). Let their way become dark and slippery and let the angel of the Lord pursue them (Ps 34:6). They have forsaken me, the fountain of living water, and have digged to themselves cisterns, broken cisterns, that can hold no water (Jer 2:13). Hieronymus habet, parturiunt viae ejus, quia peccatores proponunt facere diversa: Luc. 12: anima mea, habes multa bona reposita in annos plurimos, comede et epulare. Et dixit dominus, stulte, hac nocte et cetera. Jerome has, his ways are in travail, because sinners propose to do many things: “Soul, you have much goods laid up for many years . . . eat . . . make good cheer.” But God said to him, “You fool, this night do they require your soul of you” (Luke 12:19–20). Ps. 7: concepit dolorem, et peperit iniquitatem. Temporalibus adhaerens, in uno non infigitur, quia non sufficit: et propter hoc diversa cogitat. He has conceived sorrow and brought forth iniquity (Ps 7:15). In clinging to temporal things he does not hold fast to one, since it is not sufficient, and for this reason he thinks of many things. 73. Auferuntur. Hic ostendit quomodo contemnunt divinum judicium. 73. Your judgments are removed. Here he describes how they despise the divine judgment. Et primo ponit causam. And first, he describes the cause. Secundo ostendit effectum, ibi, omnium inimicorum. Second, he shows its effect, at he shall rule over all his enemies. Judicia tua, id est beneficia tua, auferuntur a facie ejus, id est a mente et memoria ejus: et sic non timebit quia non cogitat de eis: Prov. 28: viri mali non cogitant judicia: qui autem requirunt Deum, animadvertunt omnia: Dan. 13: declinaverunt oculos suos ne viderent caelum. Your judgments, that is, your favors, are removed from his sight, that is from his mind and memory, and so he will not fear, since he does not think about them. Evil men think not on judgment (Prov 28:5). But those who seek God pay attention to everything. They turned away their eyes that they might not look to heaven (Dan 13:9). Sed Hieronymi litteram oportet extorte dicere omnes inimicos suos despicient, quasi scilicet non credit judicia Dei. But Jerome’s text has, convolutedly, they will despise all their enemies, as though to say they do not believe the judgments of God. Et dominabitur, supple in corde suo. And he shall rule; add, “in his heart.” Vel aliquando ex Dei permissione mali dominantur inimicis. Et haec est causa, quare non cognoscuntur divina judicia: Habac. 1: ipse de regibus triumphavit: ibidem: impius praevalet adversus justum propterea egressum est judicium perversum. Or, sometimes the evil do rule over their enemies by the permission of God. And this is the reason why they do not know the divine judgments. He shall triumph over kings, . . . the wicked prevails against the just, therefore wrong judgment goes forth (Hab 1:10, 4). Alia causa est praesumptio de se ipso: et praesumunt duo. Another cause is presumption about themselves. And they presume two things: Stabilitatem; et hoc est quod dicit: dixit in corde suo, non movebor a generatione, id est dominium meum non extendetur ab una gente in aliam, non multis mala inferam: first, stability: he has said in his heart, “I shall not be moved from generation to generation,” that is, my dominion shall not pass from one nation to another, nor will I inflict many evils.
