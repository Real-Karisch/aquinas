      Et dicendum quod terra de qua loquitur, est terra viventium: et haec est in alto, quasi stabilitas aeternorum et spiritualium bonorum: et ad hanc non pervenit, quamdiu mens jacet in imis, sed oportet quod exaltetur. Isa. 58: sustollam te super altitudinem terrae. And it should be said that the earth about which he speaks is the land of the living, and this is high with the stability of eternal and spiritual goods. And to this he will not come as long as he lowers his mind to inferior things, but rather when it is exalted. I will lift you up above the high places of the earth (Isa 58:14). Vel etiam potest intelligi de ista terra. Illi ergo hereditate capiunt terram, qui terrae dominantur: et tales sunt qui habent cor elevatum a terra, quia non habent cor pressum ad terram, sicut sunt servi terrae et possessio terrae. Et ideo oportet, ad hoc quod capiant eam, ut exaltentur. Prov. 4: glorificaberis ab ea, cum eam et cetera. Or it can be understood about this earth. Therefore, those hold the earth as an inheritance who master the earth. These are those who have a heart elevated above the earth, since they do not have a heart pressed down to the earth, as do those who are slaves to the earth and the possession of the land. And so, for them to hold it, it is necessary for them to be exalted. You shall be glorified by her when you shall embrace her (Prov 4:8). Cum perierint peccatores videbis. Isti laetabuntur de perditione malorum. Ps. 57: laetabitur justus, cum viderit vindictam et cetera. Job 22: videbunt justi, et laetabuntur. When the sinners shall perish, you shall see. These will be made joyful by the destruction of the wicked. The just shall rejoice when he shall see the revenge (Ps 57:11). The just shall see and shall rejoice (Job 22:19). Sed non propter perditionem impiorum, quia Deus non propter ipsam laetatur. Sap. 1: non laetatur in perditione virorum: sed de divina justitia. Isa. ult.: egredientur et videbunt cadavera virorum, qui praevaricati sunt in me. Item de divina misericordia ipsis exhibita, qua sunt liberati a malis, sive a poenis damnatorum. But they will not be joyful because of the destruction of the wicked, since God is not joyful because of this. He has no pleasure in the destruction of the living (Wis 1:13). Rather, it is because of the divine justice. They shall go out and see the carcasses of the men who have transgressed against me (Isa 66:24). Likewise, in these is shown the divine mercy, by which they are freed from evils or from the punishments of the damned. Et ad hoc videndum oportet quod sit etiam exaltatus: quia si es deprehensus, non videbis perditionem ipsorum. Psalm. 72: hoc labor est ante me, scilicet facta peccatorum, donec et cetera. And it seems that he must be exalted for this, since if you were pressed down, you would not see their destruction. This is a labor in my sight, namely, the deeds of sinners, until I go into the sanctuary (Ps 72:16–17). 393. Vidi impium. Hic probat duo circa perditionem peccatorum. 393. I have seen the wicked highly exalted. Here he gives evidence for two things concerning the destruction of sinners. Primo ponit prosperitatem ipsorum; First, he puts forward their prosperity; secundo perditionem; second, their destruction. unde dicit: vidi impium, id est quemcumque peccatorem, superexaltatum, id est supra statum suae conditionis: et supra alios homines, quantum ad statum mundi. Job 21: quare impii vivunt, confortati sunt, roborati divitiis et cetera. So he says, I have seen the wicked, that is, any sort of sinner, highly exalted, that is, above the state proper to his condition and above other men as to worldly status. Why do the wicked live, are advanced, and strengthened with riches (Job 21:7). Et elevatum sicut cedros Libani. Hic est duplex littera. And lifted up like the cedars of Lebanon. This has a twofold literal sense. Vel supra cedros Libani, quae inter omnes arbores majorem altitudinem habet; quasi dicat: excellenter super alios vidi eum. Eccl. 10: positum stultum, id est peccatorem, in dignitate sublimi. Amos 2: altitudo cedrorum quasi altitudo illius, et fortis ipse quasi quercus. He is above the cedars of Lebanon, which, among all trees have the greatest height, as if to say, “I have seen him surpassing others.” A fool, that is, a sinner, set in high dignity (Eccl 10:6). Whose height was like the height of cedars and who was strong as an oak (Amos 2:9). Sed quare impii vivunt, est duplex ratio. But there is a twofold reason why the wicked live. Una, quia quandoque sublimantur mali propter peccata hominum. Job 34: qui regnare facit hominem hypocritam propter peccata populi. Et ideo mali sunt quasi flagella Dei ad populum cum dominantur. Isa. 10: vae Assur virga furoris mei, et baculus ipse est. One is when the wicked have been raised up because of the sins of men. Who makes a man who is a hypocrite reign for the sins of the people (Job 34:30). And so the evil are like the scourge of God to the people when they rule. Woe to the Assyrian; he is the rod and the staff of my anger (Isa 10:5). Alia ratio est, ut mali cum dominantur, gravius pereant. Job 30: elevasti me, et quasi super ventum ponens elisisti me valide. Unde quandoque in eo quod magis desiderant, magis dejiciuntur. Another reason is that when the wicked rule, they perish greatly. You have lifted me up and set me as it were upon the wind and you have mightily dashed me (Job 30:22). When they are in the midst of something which they greatly desire, they are greatly cast down. Alia ratio est ad instructionem justorum, ut non multum reputent haec temporalia, quae etiam boni habent et mali, ut dicit Augustinus. Job 21: verumtamen non sunt in manu eorum bona sua, sicut bona animae. Non curo de eis. Another reason is for the instruction of the just so that they may not value temporal things too highly, since both the good and evil have them, as Augustine says. Yet because their good things, such as a good soul, are not in their hand (Job 21:16). Item ut juste damnentur, qui abutuntur bonis datis. Isa. 1: filios enutrivi et exaltavi et cetera. Likewise, it is so that they may be condemned justly, since they were given an abundance of goods. I have brought up children and exalted them (Isa 1:2). Vel hoc potest referri ad haereticum, qui elevatur in suo sensu. Or this can be referred to a heretic who is exalted in his own understanding. Et finaliter dicitur, et transivi. And finally is said, and I passed by, and lo, he was not. Hic primo ostendit dejectionem peccatoris; This first shows the casting down of the sinner; secundo ostendit destructionem loci ejus. second, the destruction of his place. Dicit autem, transivi. Non dicit, transivit, quasi dicat, transivi ego per considerationem. Deut. 3: transibo, et videbo. Quamdiu homo defigit oculum in rebus mundi, videtur ei quod mali sunt potentes; quando autem mente transit ad spiritualia et Dei judicia, pro nihilo reputat eos. Now, he says, I passed by. He does not say, “He passed by.” It is as if he were to say, “I passed by through my consideration.” I will pass over and will see (Deut 3:25). As long as a man fixes his eyes on the things of the earth, it seems to him that the wicked are powerful, but when he turns his mind to spiritual things and the judgment of God, he reputes them as nothing. Unde, transivi, per conversionem: quia quamdiu aliquis est saecularis affectu et corpore, placent ei res mundi: sed quando affectu transit ab eis, pro nihilo habet in saeculo abundantes. Ps. 14: ad nihilum deductus est et cetera. Et ideo sancti viri reputantur superbi, quia non appretiantur nec reputant homines propter divitias vel dignitates, si non suppetant eis virtutes; So, I passed by, through conversion, since as long as someone is worldly in affection and body, the things of the world please him, but when he passes them by in affection, he considers as nothing the abundance in this world. He is brought to nothing (Ps 14:4). And so holy men are considered to be proud, since they do not esteem or admire men on account of their riches or honors, if they are not supported by the virtues. unde sequitur, et ecce non erat etiam in praesenti, quia nihil reputat justus homo gloriam peccatorum, quae videtur aliquid. 1 Mach. 2: gloria hominis peccatoris stercus et vermis est; hodie extollitur et cras non invenietur. Jer. 4: aspexi terram, id est gloriam terrenam, et ecce vacua erat, et nihil. So it follows, and lo, he was not, for in the present the just man values the glory of sinners as nothing when he sees it. The glory of a sinful man is dung and worms; today he is lifted up and tomorrow he shall not be found (1 Macc 2:62–63). I beheld the earth, that is, the glory of the earth, and behold, it was void and nothing (Jer 4:23). Vel secundum Origenem, non erat, quia quando homo elevatur ad divina, conformatur divino intellectui: et quae sunt futura, sunt ei quasi praesentia. Unde qui videt quod tempus hujus vitae est breve, damnationem impiorum videt quasi praesentem. 1 Cor. 1: elegit Deus quae non sunt, ut ea quae sunt, destrueret. Or, according to Origen, he was not, since when a man is elevated to divine things, he is intellectually conformed to the divine and those things which are future are as though present. God chose those things that are not, that he might bring to naught things that are (1 Cor 1:28). Unde dicit, quaesivi eum, et non est inventus locus ejus, id est dignitates ejus, vel familia et domus ejus; quasi dicat, non solum ipse, sed quicquid ad eum pertinet, destruitur. Job 20: oculus qui eum viderat, non videbit, neque ultra intuebitur eum locus ejus. So he says, I sought him and his place was not found, that is, his honors, or his household and home, as if to say, “Not he himself alone but whatever belongs to him is destroyed.” The eyes that had seen him shall see no more, neither shall his place anymore behold him (Job 20:9). Vel locus ejus, id est mundus in quo quiescit, quia transit mundus et concupiscentia ejus. 1 Cor. 7: praeterit enim figura hujus mundi. Or his place, that is, the world in which he rested, is destroyed, since the world and its lust passes away. For the fashion of this world passes away (1 Cor 7:31). 394. Custodi innocentiam, et vide aequitatem. Hic exhortatur ad justitiam quae est ad proximum: 394. Keep innocence, and behold justice. Here he exhorts to that justice which is towards one’s neighbor. et circa hoc tria facit. He makes three points regarding this. Primo ponit exhortationem; First, he gives the exhortation; secundo praemium, ibi, quoniam sunt; second, the reward, at for there are remnants; tertio exponit quod dixerat, ibi, salus. third, he explains what he said, at the salvation of the just. Proximo debet homo facere, ut primo ei non noceat; A man should act towards his neighbor so as first not to harm him; secundo, ut debita reddat. second, to repay to him what is owed. Quantum ad primum dicit, custodi innocentiam, nihil nocendo. Job 22: salvabitur innocens, salvabitur autem in munditia manuum suarum. In regard to the first, he says, keep innocence, by doing no harm. The innocent shall be saved, and he shall be saved by the cleanness of his hands (Job 22:30). Et vide aequitatem. Alia littera habet, directionem, id est rectitudinem et justitiam, vide, id est judica: Job 6: id quod justum est, judicate. And behold justice. Another text has, direction, that is, righteousness and justice. Behold, that is, judge. That which is just, judge (Job 6:29). Quoniam sunt reliquiae homini pacifico, hoc est praemium justorum. Origenes dicit, quod quando spiritus separatur a carne, ossa hominis dicuntur reliquiae; ita quando anima recedit, quod remanet, scilicet corpus, dicitur reliquiae: et ideo non solum praemiabitur in anima, sed etiam corpus ejus ad immortalitatem perducetur. 1 Cor. 15. For there are remnants for the peaceable man, this is, the reward of the just. Origen says that when the spirit is separated from the flesh, the bones of a man are called remnants, so what the soul leaves, that which remains (namely, the body) is called the remnants, and so he will not only be rewarded in soul, but also his body will be brought to immortality (1 Cor 15:53).
