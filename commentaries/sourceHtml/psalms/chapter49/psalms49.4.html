      Alia per testes. Isa. 44: vos testes mei. Act. 1: eritis mihi testes et cetera. Another is through witnesses. You are my witnesses (Isa 44:8). You shall be witnesses to me (Acts 1:8). Et sic ista possunt esse verba Christi populum instruentis. Audi populus meus, et loquar; Israel, et testificabor tibi, per miracula. Joan. 5: opera quae dedit mihi pater ut perficiam. Item, ibid.: scrutamini Scripturas et ideo loquar per miracula et per Scripturas, id est apparebit quod ego vera loquor, et quod verus sum per Scripturas. And so these can be Christ’s words instructing the people. Hear, O my people, and I will speak; O Israel, and I will testify to you by miracles. The works which the Father has given me to perfect (John 5:36). Likewise: search the Scriptures . . . and the same are they that give testimony of me (John 5:39). And so I speak through miracles and through the Scriptures, that is, it will become apparent through the Scriptures that I speak truth and am true. Et quid testificabor? Deus Deus tuus ego sum, scilicet singulariter. Exod. 10: ego dominus: et dicit, sum, propter aeternitatem, quia non declinat nec in praeterito nec in futuro. Et dicit, Deus tuus, quia de semine Abrahae. Rom. 9: ex quibus Christus est secundum carnem. And to what do I testify? I am God, your God, namely, uniquely. I am the Lord (Exod 10:2), and he says I am because of his eternity, since he does not fail, either in the past or in the future. And he says, your God, since he is of the seed of Abraham. Of whom is Christ, according to the flesh (Rom 9:5). 494. Non in sacrificiis tuis arguam te. Hic agit de ipsa disceptatione; 494. I will not reprove you for your sacrifices. Here, he treats the discussion itself. et circa hoc tria facit. He makes three points regarding it. Primo reprobat vetus sacrificium; First, he rejects the old sacrifice; secundo inducit sacrificium novum, ibi, immola Deo; second, he introduces the new one, at offer to God; tertio ab hoc sacrificio repellit malos, ibi, peccatori. third, he drives the wicked away from this sacrifice, at but to the sinner. Sacrificia sunt protestationes fidei; et ideo praemissurus de vultu Dei, praemittit de fide unius Dei. Now sacrifices are professions of faith, and so, about to set forth the face of God, he sets forth the faith in the one God. Et primo proponit intentum; And first, he describes the intention; secundo assignat rationem. second, gives a reason. Dicit ergo: veniam et judicabo, et, non arguam te de sacrificiis tuis, quae non omisisti. Therefore, he says, “I will come and judge, and I will not reprove you for your sacrifices, which you did not omit.” Sed contra. Qui tunc omisisset, errasset; sed pro omni errato adducetur in judicium, ut dicitur Eccl. 12. But on the contrary, he who omitted sacrifice erred, but they are brought to judgment for every error (cf. Sir 12:14). Dicendum, quod homo arguit, quando non facit voluntatem superioris. Voluntas autem Dei est sanctificatio vestra. 1 Thessalonic. 4. It should be said that a man reproves when he does not do the will of a superior. For this is the will of God, your sanctification (1 Thess 4:3). Haec autem sacrificia non conferunt ad sanctitatem vestram; et ideo non sunt per se volita a Deo, sed prout sunt signa alterius; et ideo dicit Oseae 6: misericordiam volo, et non sacrificium; et prout sunt signa interioris virtutis, et ideo de virtutibus arguuntur per se, quas non exercuerunt, non de sacrificiis. Now these sacrifices did not effect your holiness, and so they were not willed by God in themselves but rather are a sign of something else. And so he says, I desire mercy and not sacrifice (Hos 6:6). And they are rather signs of interior virtue, and so he reproves regarding the virtues themselves, which they did not exercise, not regarding sacrifices. Rationem manifestat. Primo ex parte eorum; He shows the reason first on their part; secundo ex parte sua; unde dicit, second, on his own part. holocausta tua in conspectu meo sunt semper; quasi dicat: non arguam te de sacrificiis, quia promptus es ad sacrificia carnalia offerenda, quia libenter offerebant sacrificia propter hoc quod delectabantur in eis in conviviis. Isa. 22: ecce gaudium immolare victimas et cetera. Your burnt offerings are always in my sight, as if to say, “I do not reprove your sacrifices, since you are prompt to offer sacrifices of flesh,” since they offered sacrifices gladly so they might delight in them in a banquet. Behold, joy and gladness, offering victims (Isa 22:13). Vel aliter, non arguam te in sacrificiis, carnalibus, quia holocausta tua, scilicet spiritualia, in conspectu meo sunt semper, id est mihi placent. Et haec sunt, sicut Gregorius, quando totum Deo offertur, scilicet qui se ipsum offert, et totum quod habet, et sic expendit in Dei servitio. Or it can also be taken to mean, I will not reprove you for your bodily sacrifices, for your holocausts, namely, spiritual offerings, are always in my sight, that is, they please me. And these are those, according to Gregory, which are totally offered to God, namely, he who offers himself and all that he has and thus expends it in the service of God. Ex parte Dei manifestat rationem cum dicit, non accipiam de domo tua vitulos. Ratio quare non arguam te de sacrificiis, est, quia quae non quaero principaliter, non principaliter arguo de eis. He explains the reason on God’s part when he says, I will not take calves out of your house. The reason why I will not reprove you for your sacrifices is that I do not principally reprove about things I do not principally seek. Duo erant principalia sacrificia, vitulus et hircus. Lev. 4. Et haec duo ostendit se non accipere, id est acceptare de domo carnalium, de gregibus tuis, scilicet carnalibus, accipiam, id est acceptabo, hircos. Mich. 6: numquid placari potuit dominus in millibus arietum? Now there were two principal sacrifices: the calf and the goat (cf. Lev 4:3, 23). And he demonstrates that he does not receive these two, that is, accept them from the slaughter house. Nor from your flock—that is, of carnal things—will I accept he-goats. May the Lord be appeased with thousands of rams? (Mic 6:7). Quoniam meae sunt omnes ferae sylvarum. Probat quod dixit, scilicet quod nec vitulos nec hircos approbat; et quod si indigeret, non acciperet de domo sua. For all the beasts of the woods are mine. He gives evidence for what he said, namely, that he approves neither calves nor goats, and that even if he lacked them he would not take them from his house. Nullus petit aliquid quod sit in potestate sua; omnia autem quae isti offerebant, erant in potestate Dei. No one asks someone else for what is in his own power, but all those things which they offered were in God’s power. Tria offerebantur in veteri testamento: Three things were offered in the Old Testament: animalia quadrupedia, four-footed animals, aves birds, et fructus, scilicet primitias, et panes. produce (namely, the first fruits), and bread. Quantum ad primum dicit, omnes ferae sylvarum meae sunt. Quadrupedia in duo genera dividuntur: quia quaedam sunt silvestria, et quaedam domestica: et licet silvestria non offerantur, tamen enumerat ea ut magis appareat quod etiam domestica sunt sua. In regard to the first, he says, all the beasts of the woods are mine. Four-footed animals are divided into two types: some are wild and some domesticated, and although the wild animals are not offered, nevertheless he includes them, to underscore the fact that the domesticated animals are his in addition to these. Mystice autem per ista animalia possunt designari diversa genera personarum; unde dicit, ferae, id est infideles, jumenta, id est fideles, boves in montibus, id est apostoli, omnia mea sunt. Now mystically these animals signify different types of people. So he says, beasts, that is, the faithless; the cattle, that is, the faithful; and the oxen on the hills, that is, the apostles, are all mine. Quantum ad secundum dicit, cognovi omnia volatilia caeli, id est ista subsunt meae providentiae. In regard to the second, he says, I know all the fowls of the air, that is, they are under my providence. Per haec volatilia sancti angeli intelliguntur, qui sunt similitudo. These fowls signify the holy angels, which are a likeness. Quantum ad tertium dicit, et pulchritudo agri mecum est, id est quicquid pulchrum est in eis, mihi servit. In regard to the third, he says, with me is the beauty of the field, that is, whatever beauty is in them serves me. Vel pulchritudo agri mecum est, quia ubique sum. Et est, quia semper sum, sine praeterito et futuro. Or, the beauty of the field is with me since I am everywhere. And it is, since I always am, without past or future. Si esuriero, non dicam tibi. Concludit per impossibile. Si indigerem eis, non dicerem tibi, id est non quaererem a te. Quare? Quia meus est orbis terrae, et plenitudo ejus. Ps. 23: domini est terra et cetera. If I should be hungry, I would not tell you. He concludes using what would be logically impossible. If I needed them, I would not tell you, that is, I would not seek them from you. Why? Because the world is mine and its fullness. The earth is the Lord’s (Ps 23:1). 495. Numquid manducabo. Supra psalmista ex persona domini assignavit rationem quare dominus non accipiet hircos, etiam si indigeret; hic autem ostendit, quod non indiget. 495. Shall I eat the flesh of bullocks? Above, the psalmist in the person of the Lord gave the reason why the Lord would not accept calves, even if he lacked them. Now he shows that he does not lack. Sciendum est autem quod in lege praecipitur quod carnes holocaustorum comburerentur, et sanguis effundebatur ad altaris crepidinem. Et posset aliquis suspicari, quod Deus delectaretur in sanguine et carnibus illorum. Haec etiam fuit opinio gentilium, quod dii eorum delectarentur odoribus carnium et sanguinis effusione, ut dicit Augustinus. It should be noted that in the law it is prescribed that the flesh of holocausts be burnt, and their blood was poured out upon the base of the altar. And someone could think that God delighted in their blood and flesh. This was the opinion of the gentiles—that their gods delighted in the odors of the flesh and in the pouring out of blood, as Augustine says.
