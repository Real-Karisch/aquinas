      Secundo aedificiorum destructionem: praecipitavit omnia moenia. Ps. 88: destruxisti omnes sepes ejus. Second, the destruction of the buildings: he has overthrown all the walls. You have broken down all his hedges (Ps 88:41). Tertio concludit populi humiliationem, et replevit, idest multiplicavit. Ps. 43: humiliata est in pulvere anima nostra, conglutinatus est in terra venter noster. Third, he concludes with the humiliation of the people: he has filled, that is, multiplied, the humiliation. Our soul is humbled down to the dust: our belly cleaves to the earth (Ps 43:25). Lectio 6 Lecture 6 2:6 Vau. Et dissipavit quasi hortum tentorium suum; demolitus est tabernaculum suum: oblivioni tradidit Dominus in Sion festivitatem et sabbatum; et in opprobrium, et in indignationem furoris sui regem, et sacerdotem. 2:6 Vau. And he has destroyed his tent as a garden, he has thrown down his tabernacle: the Lord has caused feasts and sabbaths to be forgotten in Zion; and has delivered up king and priest to reproach, and to the indignation of his wrath. 28. Hic plangit destructionem aedificiorum principalium: 28. Here he weeps over the destruction of the principal buildings. et primo templi; And first, of the temple; secundo domus regiae, ibi, cogitavit Dominus dissipare murum filiae Sion. second, of the house of the king, at the Lord has purposed to destroy the wall of the daughter of Zion (Lam 2:8). Prima in duas. The first is divided in two parts. In prima tangit destructionem ipsius templi; First, he touches upon the destruction of the temple itself; secundo eorum quae erant in templo, ibi, repulit Dominus altare suum. second, that of the objects that were in the temple, at the Lord has cast off his altar (Lam 2:7). Circa primum tria facit. Concerning the first, he does three things. Primo tangit templi destructionem: tentorium, quod in deserto factum, in Sylo positum fuerat, tabernaculum, quod Salomon aedificaverat, quasi hortum, qui facile destruitur. Psal. 77: repulit tabernaculum Silo, tabernaculum suum ubi habitavit in hominibus. Jer. 26: dabo domum istam sicut Silo, et urbem hanc dabo in maledictionem. First, he touches upon the destruction of the temple: his tent, which had been made in the desert and was installed at Shiloh; his tabernacle, which was built by Solomon; as a garden, which is easy to destroy. And he put away the tabernacle of Shiloh, his tabernacle where he dwelt among men (Ps 77:60). I will make this house like Shiloh: and I will make this city a curse (Jer 26:6). Secundo festorum templi cessationem: oblivioni tradidit Dominus in Sion festivitatem et sabbatum; quasi, non placent sicut ante. Isa. 1: Calendas vestras et solemnitates vestras odivit anima mea. Second, the cessation of the festivals of the temple: the Lord has caused feasts and sabbaths to be forgotten in Zion, as if they do not please him as before. My soul hates your new moons, and your solemnities (Isa 1:14). Tertio ministrorum reprobationem: et in opprobrium, scilicet dedit, regem, ad quem pertinebat templi defensio, et sacerdotem ad quem pertinebat administratio. Job 12: ducit sacerdotes eorum ingloriosos, et optimates supplantat. Third, the reprobation of its ministers: and has to reproach, that is, he delivered up, king, to whom pertained the defense of the temple, and priest, to whom pertained its administration. He leads away priests without glory, and overthrows nobles (Job 12:19). Lectio 7 Lecture 7 2:7 Zain. Repulit Dominus altare suum, maledixit sanctificationi suae: tradidit in manus inimici muros turrium ejus. Vocem dederunt in domo Domini, sicut in die solemni. 2:7 Zain. The Lord has cast off his altar, he has cursed his sanctuary: he has delivered the walls of the towers thereof into the hand of the enemy: they have made a noise in the house of the Lord, as in the day of a solemn feast. 29. Hic tangit destructionem aedificiorum quae in templo erant: 29. Here he touches on the destruction of the structures that were in the temple. et primo altaris pertinentis ad religionem: altare, holocaustorum, repulit, tradens illud hostibus ad profanandum, sanctificationi, sacrificiis placationis, quae offerebantur. 1 Mach. 4: viderunt sanctificationem desertam, altare profanatum. Tradidit in manus. 1 Mach. 2: sancta facta sunt in manu extraneorum. And first, of the altar for worship: his altar, of holocausts, the Lord has cast off, handing it over to their enemies to be profaned; his sanctuary, the sacrifices of propitiation that were offered there. They saw the sanctuary desolate, and the altar profaned (1 Macc 4:38). The holy places are come into the hands of strangers (1 Macc 2:8). Tertio templi ab hominibus combustionem: vocem dederunt, blasphemiae, vel tumultus bellici, sicut in die solemni, consueverant sacerdotes laudantes Deum. Isa. ult.: vox populi de civitate, vox de templo, vox Domini reddentes retributionem inimicis suis. Third, the burning of the temple by men: they have made a noise, of blasphemies or cries of war; as in the day of a solemn feast, as the priests were accustomed to use in praising God. A voice of the people from the city, a voice from the temple, the voice of the Lord that renders recompense to his enemies (Isa 66:6). Lectio 8 Lecture 8 2:8 Heth. Cogitavit Dominus dissipare murum filiae Sion: tetendit funiculum suum, et non avertit manum suam a perditione: luxitque antemurale, et murus pariter dissipatus est. 2:8 Heth. The Lord has purposed to destroy the wall of the daughter of Zion: he has stretched out his line, and has not withdrawn his hand from destroying: and the bulwark has mourned, and the wall has been destroyed together. 30. Hic tangit destructionem arcis Sion: 30. Here he touches upon the destruction of the citadel of Zion. et primo ponit divinam deliberationem: cogitavit; quasi, cogitans statuit, non statim implens, filiae Sion, arcis vel ipsam Jerusalem. Isa. 14: hoc consilium quod cogitavi super omnem terram etc. And first, he presents the divine deliberation: the Lord has purposed, as if he has decided meditatively, without acting immediately; of the daughter of Zion, her citadel or Jerusalem herself. This is the counsel, that I have purposed upon all the earth (Isa 14:26). Secundo justum judicium: tetendit funiculum, quasi ad metiendum justitiam suam, ut poena culpae aequaretur, non avertit, quia nihil dimisit de poena justa. Isa. 34: extendetur super ipsam mensura, ut redigatur in nihilum. Second, he presents the just judgment: he has stretched out his line, as if to measure his justice, so that the punishment would equal the fault; he has not withdrawn his hand, for he has lessened nothing of the just punishment. A line shall be stretched out upon it, to bring it to nothing (Isa 34:11). Tertio ostendit justitiae effectum: luxitque antemurale, idest destructum ad luctum provocavit, sicut supra 1: viae Sion lugent. Isa. 21: luxit et defluxit terra, et infirmata est; defluxit orbis. Third, he shows the effect of justice: and the bulwark has mourned, that is, the ruins provoked mourning, as above: the ways of Zion mourn (Lam 1:4). The earth mourned, and faded away, and is weakened: the world faded away (Isa 24:4). Lectio 9 Lecture 9 2:9 Teth. Defixae sunt in terra porta ejus: perdidit, et contrivit vectes ejus: regem ejus, et principes ejus in gentibus. Non est lex, et prophetae ejus non invenerunt visionem a Domino. 2:9 Teth. Her gates are sunk into the ground: he has destroyed, and broken her bars: her king and her princes are in the gentiles: the law is no more, and her prophets have found no vision from the Lord. 31. Hic plangit destructionem hominum: 31. Here he weeps over the destruction of men. et primo eorum quorum miseria est valde miserabilis secundum gradum dignitatis; And first of all, of those whose misfortune is more intensely miserable according to the degree of their dignity; secundo quorum est miserabilis propter reverentiam conditionis, ibi, sederunt in terra, conticuerunt senes; second, of those whose misfortune is miserable because of reverence for their condition, at the ancients sit upon the ground, they have held their peace (Lam 2:10); tertio illorum quorum est miserabilis propter defectum aetatis, ibi, defecerunt prae lacrymis oculis mei. third, of those whose misfortune is miserable because of their lack of years, at my eyes have failed with weeping (Lam 2:11). Circa primum duo. Concerning the first, he makes two points.
