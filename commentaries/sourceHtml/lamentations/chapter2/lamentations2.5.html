      Circa primum duo facit. Concerning the first, he does two things. Primo ostendit plagam incurabilem; First, he shows the incurable wound; secundo assignat causam, ibi, prophetae tui viderunt tibi falsa. second, he assigns the cause, at your prophets have seen false and foolish things for you (Lam 2:14). Circa primum duo facit. Concerning the first, he does two things. Primo ostendit quod non potest leniri consolationibus ex comparatione aliorum, quod solet esse afflictis aliqualis consolatio: cui comparabo? Similitudo plus est quam comparatio: quaelibet enim distantia ejusdem rationis comparabilia sunt, dummodo in infinitum non distent: nec tamen similia, si eamdem qualitatem participent, nec omnia habeant aequalia; sed tantum illa quorum unum in participatione qualitatis alterum non excedit. Excedit autem in hoc plagas aliorum, quod et temporalem, et spiritualem gloriam amisit, qua aliae gentes caruerunt. Consolabor; quasi dicat: nihil alii pejus vel simile tibi passi sunt. Supra 1: adduxisti diem consolationis, et fient similes mei. First, he shows that it cannot be softened by consolations drawn from a comparison with others, which is usually a kind consolation for the afflicted: to what shall I compare you? A similarity is more than just a comparison, for any differences of the same nature are comparable, provided the difference is not infinite; neither are they similar just because they participate in the same quality, nor because they have everything equally. But only those differences are similar in which one does not exceed the other in participating in the quality. But her wounds exceed those of others in that she has lost the temporal and spiritual glory that other nations did not have. That I may comfort you, as if to say, “Others have not suffered anything like or worse than you.” You have brought a day of consolation, and they shall be like unto me (Lam 1:21). Secundo ostendit quod non potest sanari medicinis, propter suam magnitudinem: magna velut mare, quod latissimum est et inquietum. Jerem. 30: insanabilis plaga tua. Second, he shows that this wound cannot be cured by medication, because of its size: great as the sea, which is very wide and restless. Your wound is incurable (Jer 30:12). Lectio 14 Lecture 14 2:14 Nun. Prophetae tui viderunt tibi falsa, et stulta; nec aperiebant iniquitatem tuam, ut te ad poenitentiam provocarent: viderunt autem tibi assumptiones falsas et ejectiones. 2:14 Nun. Your prophets have seen false and foolish things for you: and they have not laid open your iniquity, to excite you to penance: but they have seen for you false assumptions and banishments. 36. Hic assignat causam incurabilitatis, quia scilicet medici suo tempore curare neglexerunt, scilicet prophetae. 36. Here he assigns the cause of its incurability, because the physicians of the time, namely, the prophets, neglected to treat her. Et primo ostendit falsitatem prophetarum quantum ad falsa quae praedicabant: viderunt, videre se dicebant, falsa et stulta: quia stultum erat credere quia Deus peccatores non puniret. Ezech. 22: prophetae ejus liniebant eos absque temperamento. 2 Pet. 2: fuerunt enim pseudoprophetae, sicut et in vobis erunt magistri mendaces. Et quantum ad ea quae reticebant: nec aperiebant iniquitatem tuam. Isa. 58: clama, ne cesses, quasi tuba exalta vocem tuam: annuntia populo meo scelera eorum. And first, he shows the falsity of the prophets as regards the false things they predicted: your prophets have seen, that is, they said they had seen; false and foolish things: because it was foolish to believe that God would not punish sinners. Her prophets have daubed them without tempering the mortar (Ezek 22:28). But there were also false prophets among the people, even as there shall be among you lying teachers (2 Pet 2:1). And as regards the things they did not say, and they have not laid open your iniquity. Cry, cease not, lift up your voice like a trumpet, and show my people their wicked doings (Isa 58:1). Secundo exponit cujusmodi falsa videbant: viderunt autem tibi assumptiones, scilicet onera deprimentia, sicut onus assumptum in altum magis comprimit. Mich. 3: haec dicit Dominus super prophetas qui seducunt populum meum. Ejectiones, liberationes. Jer. 28: confringam jugum Nabuchodonosor regis Babylonis post duos annos dierum. Vel assumptiones, quod Deus vos ad se sumet, ejectiones, quod adversarii eorum ejicientur. Second, he explains the kind of things seen in their false visions: but they have seen for you false assumptions, namely, overwhelming burdens, as a burden carried higher weighs one down the more. Thus says the Lord concerning the prophets that make my people err (Micah 3:5). Banishments, liberations. Even so will I break the yoke of Nebuchadnezzar the king of Babylon after two full years of days (Jer 28:11). Or assumptions, that God would take you to himself; banishments, that their enemies would be expelled. Lectio 15 Lecture 15 2:15 Samech. All they that passed by the way have clapped their hands at you: they have hissed and wagged their heads at the daughter of Jerusalem, saying, ‘Is this the city of perfect beauty, the joy of all the earth?’ 37. Here he presents the astonishment of the spectators. et primo amicorum; And first, of friends; secundo inimicorum, ibi, aperuerunt super te os suum omnes. second, of enemies, at all your enemies have opened their mouth against you (Lam 2:16). Circa primum ponit compassionis amicorum admirationem expressam quatuor signis. Plausu manuum, plauserunt omnes transeuntes per viam, terrae tuae, videntes terrae vastitatem: sibilo, sibilaverunt: motu capitis, et moverunt capita sua: verbo, haeccine est urbs perfecti decoris, quia in spiritualibus et temporalibus (erat enim civitas regalis et sacerdotalis) gaudium universae terrae? quia conveniebant undique in solemnitatibus. Psalm. 47: fundatur exultatione universae terrae, mons Sion, latera aquilonis, civitas regis magni. Isa. 22: urbs frequens, civitas exultans. Jerem. 22: pertransibunt gentes multae per civitatem hanc; et dicet unusquisque proximo suo: quare fecit Dominus sic civitati huic grandi? Concerning the first, he presents the astonishment of compassionate friends, which is expressed by four signs. By the clapping of hands: all they that passed by the way, of your land, seeing the devastation of the land, have clapped their hands. By hissing: they have hissed. By shaking their heads: and wagged their heads. By words: saying, ‘Is this the city of perfect beauty,’ because in things spiritual and temporal (for it was a royal and priestly city), it was ‘the joy of all the earth,’ for they came to her from everywhere for the solemn feasts. With the joy of the whole earth is mount Zion founded, on the sides of the north, the city of the great king (Ps 47:3). A populous city, a joyous city (Isa 22:2). And many nations shall pass by this city: and they shall say every man to his neighbor: why has the Lord done so to this great city? (Jer 22:8). Lectio 16 Lecture 16 2:16 Phe. All your enemies have opened their mouth against you: they have hissed, and gnashed with the teeth, and have said: we will swallow her up: lo, this is the day which we looked for: we have found it, we have seen it. 38. Hic ponit insultationem hostium: aperuerunt, accusando, inimici, Idumaei, Moabitae et alii, sibilaverunt, detrahendo, fremuerunt, comminando: devorabimus, insultando. Psalm. 21: aperuerunt super me os suum, sicut leo rapiens et rugiens. Jer. 51: comedit me, devoravit me Nabuchodonosor rex Babylonis. Ps. 34. Dixerunt: euge euge animae nostrae: viderunt oculi nostri. 38. Here he presents the insults of their enemies: your enemies have opened their mouth, by accusing; your enemies, Idumeans, Moabites, and others; they have hissed, by disparaging; and gnashed with the teeth, by threatening; we will swallow her up, by insulting. They have opened their mouths against me, as a lion ravening and roaring (Ps 21:14). Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon has eaten me up, he has devoured me (Jer 51:34). They said, well done, well done, our eyes have seen it (Ps 34:21). Lectio 17 Lecture 17 2:17 Ain. Fecit Dominus quae cogitavit, complevit sermonem suum, quem praeceperat a diebus antiquis: destruxit, et non pepercit: et laetificavit super te inimicum, et exaltavit cornu hostium tuorum. 2:17 Ain. The Lord has done that which he purposed, he has fulfilled his word, which he commanded in the days of old: he has destroyed, and has not spared, and he has caused the enemy to rejoice over you, and has set up the horn of your adversaries. 39. Hic ponit judicis commendationem. 39. Here he presents the approval of the judge. Et primo ponitur constantia in proposito: fecit Dominus quae cogitavit. Isa. 14: hoc est consilium quod cogitavi. And first, there is his consistency in doing what he proposed: the Lord has done that which he purposed. This is the counsel, that I have purposed (Isa 14:26). Secundo ostenditur verax in verbo, complevit: praeceperat enim destructionem populi a diebus antiquis, Deut. 28. Vel etiam per priores prophetas. Num. 23: dixit, et non faciet; locutus est, et non implebit? Second, there is shown the veracity of his speech, he has fulfilled his word: for he decreed the destruction of the people in the days of old (Deut 28:20). Or also by previous prophets. Has he said then, and will he not do? Has he spoken, and will he not fulfill? (Num 23:19). Tertio ostenditur justus in facto: destruxit, Judaeam, et non pepercit, quasi deserens rigorem justitiae, cornu, potestatem. Psal. 88: exaltasti dexteram deprimentium eum. Third, there is shown what is just in the action: he has destroyed, Judea; and has not spared, as if abandoning the rigor of justice; the horn, that is, power. You have set up the right hand of them that oppress him (Ps 88:43). Lectio 18 Lecture 18 2:18 Sade.
