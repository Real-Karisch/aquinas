      Secundo auxilii subtractionem: non est qui consoletur eam, auxilium contra hostes praebens, ex omnibus caris, Aegyptiis, et aliis eis foederatis. Eccl. 4: vidi calumnias quae sub sole geruntur, et lacrymas innocentum, et consolatorem neminem. Second, the withdrawal of aid: there is none to comfort her, by offering help against her enemies; among all them that were dear to her, the Egyptians and her other allies. I saw the oppressions that are done under the sun, and the tears of the innocent, and they had no comforter (Eccl 4:1). Tertio affectus immutationem: omnes amici. Job 19: fratres meos longe fecit a me, et noti mei quasi alieni recesserunt a me. Third, the change of affections: all her friends. He has put my brethren far from me, and my acquaintance like strangers have departed from me (Job 19:13). Huic praeponitur littera beth, quae interpretatur domus, quia domus Jacob est plorans. At the top of this verse is placed the letter, beth, which is translated “house,” because the house of Jacob is weeping. Tertius decimus conquestionis locus. This is the thirteenth topic of complaint. Allegorice. Ecclesia plorat adversitates, in maxillis, praelatis, ex omnibus caris, angelis sanctis, spreverunt, conformantes se divinae justitiae. Allegorically, the Church weeps over her adversities, on her cheeks: the prelates; among all them that were dear to her: the holy angels; have despised her: for complying with divine justice. Moraliter anima plorat, in nocte, peccata, in maxillis, conscientia, in qua loquitur hominis operatio: ex omnibus caris, privatis affectionibus, spreverunt, affectus pravae delectationis. Morally, the soul weeps, in the night: her sins; on her cheeks: conscience, in which the action of man speaks; among all them that were dear to her: private affections; have despised the affections of depraved enjoyment. Lectio 3 Lecture 3 1:3Ghimel. Migravit Judas propter afflictionem, et multitudinem servitutis: habitavit inter gentes, nec invenit requiem: omnes persecutores ejus apprehenderunt eam inter angustias. 1:3 Ghimel. Judah has removed her dwelling place because of her affliction, and the greatness of her bondage: she has dwelt among the nations, and she has found no rest: all her persecutors have taken her in the midst of straits. 3. Hic prosequitur miseriam servitutis per partes, ut ostendat ipsius magnitudinem. 3. Here he writes in detail about the misfortune of their servitude in order to show its greatness. Et dividitur in duas partes. And this is divided in two parts. In prima plangit ipsam quantum ad hominum oppressionem; In the first, he deplores this misfortune as regards the oppression of men; secundo quantum ad bonorum direptionem, ibi, manum suam misit hostis ad omnia desiderabilia ejus. in the second, as regards the pillage of their goods, at the enemy has put out his hand to all her desirable things (Lam 1:10). Prima in duas. The first is divided in two. In prima plangit oppressionis miseriam; In the first, he deplores the misfortune of oppression; in secunda ostendit miseriae rationem, ibi, peccatum peccavit Jerusalem. in the second, he shows the reason for their misfortune, at Jerusalem has grievously sinned (Lam 1:8). Circa primum duo facit. Concerning the first, he does two things. Primo plangit eorum miseriam quantum ad praesentia quae sustinebant; First, he deplores their misfortune as regards things they were enduring in the present; secundo quantum ad praeterita quae recogitabant, ibi, et recordata est Jerusalem dierum afflictionis suae. second, as regards past events which they brooded over, at Jerusalem has remembered the days of her affliction (Lam 1:7). Circa primum tria ponit. Concerning the first, he makes three points. Primo miseriam servitutis quantum ad fugientes; First, the misfortune of servitude for those who fled; secundo quantum ad remanentes, ibi, viae Sion lugent etc.; second, for those who remained, at the ways of Zion mourn (Lam 1:4); tertio quantum ad captivos, ibi, facti sunt hostes ejus in capite. third, for the captives, at her adversaries are become her lords (Lam 1:5). Circa primum tria ponit. Concerning the first, he makes three points. Primo fugam: migravit, fugiens a terra sua ad terras vicinas, propter afflictionem, in personis, et multitudinem servitutis, in tributis, quam in terra sua Chaldaeis patiebantur. Isa. 16: habitabunt apud te profugi mei. Moab esto latibulum eorum. Ibid. 21: qui habitatis terram austri, cum panibus occurrite fugienti: a facie enim gladiorum fugerunt, a facie gladii imminentis. First, the flight: removed her dwelling place, fleeing her land to neighboring countries, because of her affliction, amongst the people, and the greatness of her bondage, in the tributes that they endured in their own land from the Chaldeans. My fugitives shall dwell with you: O Moab, be a covert to them (Isa 16:4). You that inhabit the land of the south, meet with bread him that flees. For they are fled from before the swords, from the sword that hung over them (Isa 21:14–15). Secundo plangit fugientium imminentem angustiam: habitavit inter gentes, Moabitas et Ismahelitas, nec invenit requiem, quia ibi quoque angustiam passa est ab illis. Deut. 28: in gentibus quoque illis non quiesces: neque erit requies vestigia pedis tui. Second, he deplores the anguish that threatens the fugitives: she has dwelt among the nations, Moabites and Ishmaelites, and has found no rest, because here too she suffered anguish on their account. Neither shall you be quiet, even in those nations, nor shall there be any rest for the sole of your foot (Deut 28:65). Tertio praedicit angustias, superveniente hostium potentia: omnes persecutores, Chaldaei, apprehenderunt eam inter angustias, quas ab illis ad quos fugerant patiebantur, sicut in Aegypto. Jerem. 42: gladius, quem vos formidatis, apprehendet vos in terra Aegypti, et fames pro qua estis soliciti, adhaerebit vobis. Ezech. 6: qui quaerunt eam non deficient, in menstruis ejus quaerent eam. Third, he predicts their anxiety at the arrival of the enemy’s power: all her persecutors, the Chaldeans, have taken her in the midst of straights, which they suffered from those to whom they had fled, as in Egypt. The sword which you fear, shall overtake you there in the land of Egypt: and the famine, whereof you are afraid, shall cleave to you (Jer 42:16). All that seek her shall not fail: in her menstruation they shall find her (Jer 2:24). Huic versui praeponitur littera ghimel, quod interpretatur plenitudo: quia ex multitudine peccatorum passi sunt plenitudinem miseriae. Matth. 23: et vos implete mensuram patrum vestrorum. Luc. 6: in qua mensura mensi fueritis, remetietur vobis. At the top of this verse is placed the letter, ghimel, which means “fullness,” since because of the multitude of their sins they have suffered the fullness of misfortune. Fill you up then the measure of your fathers (Matt 23:32). With the same measure with which you measure shall it be measured to you again (Luke 6:38). Decimus conquestionis locus. This is the tenth topic of complaint. Allegorice Judas, Ecclesia, quae Christo conjungitur, migravit, de hostibus in hostes, apud quos requiem petit, et non invenit, sed ab omnibus ad libitum opprimitur. Allegorically, Judah: the Church, which is united with Christ; has removed her dwelling place: from enemies to enemies, among whom she seeks rest; and she has found no rest: but is oppressed by all at will. Moraliter Judas, anima quae Deum confiteri debet, migravit, ad vitia, inter gentes, vitia, vel daemones, requiem, illudentibus vitiis, vel daemonibus, persecutores, daemones, apprehenderunt, in morte. Morally, Judah: the soul which ought to confess to God; has removed her dwelling place: towards vices; among the nations: vices or demons; no rest: because of the mockery of vices or demons; her persecutors: the demons; have taken her: in death. Lectio 4 Lecture 4 1:4 Daleth. Viae Sion lugent, eo quod non sint qui veniant ad solemnitatem: omnes portae ejus destructae: sacerdotes ejus gementes; virgines ejus squalidae; et ipsa oppressa amaritudine. 1:4 Daleth. The ways of Zion mourn, because there are none that come to the solemn feast: all her gates are broken down; her priests sigh: her virgins are in affliction, and she is oppressed with bitterness. 4. Hic plangit miseriam remanentium, 4. Here he deplores the misery of those who remain. primo quantum ad peregrinos quibus frequentabatur. Viae Sion lugent, luctum excitant, ad solemnitatem, triplicem, Pascha, Pentecosten, Scenopegia. Isa. 33: dissipatae sunt viae, cessavit transiens per semitam, irritum factum est pactum. First, with regard to the pilgrims who used to visit her. The ways of Zion mourn: they arouse mourning; to the solemn feast, which is three-fold: Passover, Pentecost, and Tabernacles. The ways are made desolate, no one passes by the road, the covenant is made void (Isa 33:8).
